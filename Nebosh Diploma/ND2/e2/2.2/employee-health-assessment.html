Employee health assessments and individual health improvement plans

Employee health assessments provided by occupational health professionals comprise of a series of medical tests (such as cholesterol levels, blood pressure, lung function body mass index etc) together with evaluations of general physical and mental health to provide individuals with a better understanding of their health, wellbeing and risks.

Many services will then work with the individual to develop their personal health improvement plan and can also provide support services including progress monitoring and access to helplines etc.

The link between health and wellbeing and safety culture

As has been discussed, wellbeing is largely about feeling good – comfortable healthy and happy, and is as much about the individual’s subjective sense of worth and value as it is about diet, exercise and healthy behaviours. The positive impact of leadership and culture should not be underestimated. Rather than compartmentalising culture consider the potential positive impacts of working for an organisation and leaders who value their colleague’s safety and physical and psychological health and wellbeing. Workers who are healthy and happy at work are more likely to be loyal, and more likely to contribute additional discretionary effort.

Why line managers must be trained on wellbeing strategies and initiatives

As with any other workplace initiative the role of line management is critical and line managers need to understand their priorities, in line with the organisations core values. In addition to training and development of awareness and understanding line managers also require sufficient resources, including time to be able to constructively play their part in any new initiatives. If they are expected to accommodate additional work without additional resources, it is likely that they will become a ‘bottleneck’ to the roll-out of new initiatives.
