Other action in connection with an emergency

Emergency plans should include procedures for determining whether an emergency has occurred, and when to activate the plan in response to an emergency. This should include identifying an appropriately trained person who will take the decision, in consultation with others, on when an emergency has occurred.

Emergency planning should look beyond the immediate response and long-term recovery issues, and also look at secondary impacts including media attention and public interest.

Working with the media

Media interest and involvement in an emergency will result in efforts to gather newsworthy information, and the dissemination of that information via the various news media (television, radio, internet and print).

A key individual should be identified to act as media coordinator to manage a steady stream of accurate information and ensure that key information is released at appropriate times.

Local television and radio stations can play an important role in broadcasting emergency information to the local community.

Managing public interest

    Regulation 17 of CoMAH requires the competent authority to make information on any establishment covered by the CoMAH Regulations to make the following information available to the public upon request:

  • The name of the operator and the address of the establishment
  • Confirmation that CoMAH applies to the establishment and that the notification, and the safety report have been sent to the competent authority
  • A simple explanation of the activities undertaken
  • The hazard classification of the relevant dangerous substances with an indication of their principal dangerous characteristics in simple terms
  • General information about how the public will be warned, and expected behaviour in the event of a major accident
  • Details of where further relevant information can be obtained.

    For upper tier establishments the following information must also be made available:

  • General information on the nature of the major accident hazards, including their potential consequences on human health and the environment
  • Summary details of the main types of major accident scenarios and the control measures to address them
  • Confirmation that the operator is required to make adequate arrangements within the establishment, and to liaise with the emergency services, to deal with major accidents and to minimise their consequences
  • Appropriate information from the external emergency plan in relation to dealing with consequences outside the establishment including advice on co-operating with instructions or requests from the emergency services at the time of an accident.

    Regulation 18 of CoMAH requires the identification of a Public Information Zone (PIZ) around an upper tier CoMAH site, in which site operators are obliged to inform, without request, those who live and/or work within the zone with information on:

  • Safety measures at the establishment
  • The requisite behaviour in the event of a major accident at the establishment.

An effective method must be in place for warning members of the public of the presence of an escalating situation leading to a major accident scenario and the initiation of emergency plans, requirements for evacuation etc.
