Marking of products

All products, except components that comply with EPS, are to be marked with the CE conformity mark, followed by the number of the relevant notified body, where the notified body is involved with the production / QA procedures for the product. In addition the product must be marked with:

  • name and address of manufacturer
  • product designation series or type
  • serial number (if applicable)
  • year of manufacture
  • the specific explosion protection Ex mark
  • the product Group and Category
  • the letter G for suitability for use in gases, vapours or mists or D for dusts
  • where necessary with all information essential for safe use, for example, hazard group, temperature class, and Ex protection concept.

UKCA marking (UK Conformity Assessed marking) will be required for all Ex products being placed on the market in Great Britain (England, Scotland and Wales) going forward (CE marks will remain acceptable in Northern Ireland). The new process has been operational since 1st January 2021 and there is a transitional phase until 1st January 2022 during which existing CE marked stock can be used.

From 1st January 2022 only UKCA issued Ex certificates (UKEX) will be accepted in the Great Britain market.

Hazard group

Equipment and protective systems intended for use in potentially explosive atmospheres are grouped, based on ignition energy, as:

  • Group I – Mining: methane (firedamp), for example, and / or combustible dust. Note: Group I is not on the NEBOSH National Diploma syllabus
  • Group II – Surface industry which is sub-categorised into: IIA (propane, for example), IIB (ethylene, for example), and IIC (hydrogen, for example). A IIC gas poses a much higher risk than IIA or IIB and is much easier to ignite. Note: Equipment suitable for use with a IIC gas will also be suitable for use with IIA and IIB.

Temperature class

Flammable gases and vapours are classified into temperature classes according to their flammability. The maximum surface temperature of electrical apparatus should always be lower than the auto-ignition temperature of the gas / air or vapour / air mixture in which it is used.

Equipment classified in a higher temperature class (such as T5) may also be used for applications in which a lower temperature class is required (such as T2 or T3).

The temperature class is based on the autoignition temperature of the gas, as shown in the table below.

Combustible dusts are not divided into temperature classes. The minimum ignition temperature of the dust cloud is compared with the maximum surface temperature of the apparatus, and a safety factor applied. The maximum surface temperature of the apparatus must not exceed 2 / 3 of the minimum ignition temperature of the dust cloud.

As dust can also settle on equipment, the minimum ignition temperature of the dust layer must also be taken into account.

Ex protection concept

Only explosion protected equipment may be used in areas in which an explosive atmosphere may still be expected, despite the implementation of prevention measures.

There are various types of explosion protection as determined by the construction regulations of the BS EN 60079 series of standards. (BS EN 60079-0:2012+A11:2013 Explosive atmospheres. Equipment. General requirements provides the overview).

BS EN 60079-0:2012 Explosive atmospheres. Equipment. General requirements defines the standards for electrical equipment to be used in areas with combustible dust.
