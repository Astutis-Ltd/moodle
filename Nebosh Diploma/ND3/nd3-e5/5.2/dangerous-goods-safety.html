Dangerous Goods Safety Advisers (DGSA)

The ADR requires many of those involved in carriage of dangerous goods to appoint a Dangerous Goods Safety Adviser (DGSA). It applies to carriers, packers, fillers, loaders and unloaders, subject to certain exemptions.

Department for Transport (DfT) guidance is that final unloaders (consignees) will not need to appoint a DGSA, but that intermediate unloaders (such as freight forwarders and consolidators, and operators of ‘in-transit’ storage facilities) will need to have a DGSA.

The exemptions arise from ADR, and have been implemented in the UK by the CDG Regulations. The exemptions do not apply to international carriage.

  • Exemption (a) is carriage related activities for:
    • small loads
    • limited quantities
    • excepted quantities.
  • Exemption (b) arises where the main or secondary activity of the duty holder is not the carriage of dangerous goods (or related activities). Companies whose business is not the transport of dangerous goods, per se, but whose activities involve such transport will not normally be required to appoint a DGSA. Examples include:
    • construction companies taking dangerous goods to and from sites
    • repair organisations that occasionally recover vehicles, which are still loaded with dangerous goods.

The DGSA has three main duties:

  1. Monitoring compliance with rules governing transport of dangerous goods
  2. Where the defendant’s conduct has been calculated to make a profit that will exceed compensation payable to the claimant.
  3. Preparing an annual report to management on the business’ activities in the transport of dangerous goods.

The DGSA is also responsible for:

  • monitoring procedures and safety measures
  • investigating and compiling reports on any accidents or emergencies
  • advising on the potential security aspects of transport.

These regulations can apply to any person who allows dangerous goods to be carried, not just the transport operator. This could include cargo consignors, freight forwarders, warehouse workers and manufacturers producing goods that will be collected from their factory.

Monitoring compliance

The DGSA will be responsible for auditing the arrangements in place for ensuring compliance with CDG/RID, including:

  • vehicle purchasing procedures (ensuring new vehicles are suitable for contents to be transported)
  • dangerous goods are properly identified and the correct placards are in place
  • selection of suitable, competent sub-contractors
  • maintaining and inspecting equipment to ensure it is in good, safe working order
  • ensuring that all necessary documents (Tremcard) and emergency equipment is in situ
  • employee training and record keeping
  • planning of on-going training to maintain risk awareness
  • provision of detailed operational procedures and instructions
  • accident investigation arrangements / implementation of measures to avoid recurrence
  • loading/unloading of dangerous goods
  • emergency procedures
  • security plans
  • document control and retention.

Training

DGSAs must obtain a Vocational Training Certificate (VTC) after receiving appropriate training and passing a written examination.

Training courses for DGSAs are run by independent providers and by the trade associations for each mode of transport. Course lengths vary from two to five days, depending on the mode(s) of transport covered.

The Department for Transport (DfT) approves the mandatory DGSA exams, and has appointed the Scottish Qualification Authority (SQA) as its agent. The SQA sets, marks and organises the exams, and issues the vocational training certificates for the whole of the UK.

The approved examination is set on modular principles, with regard to certain classes of dangerous goods or discreet industry sectors. The VTC will relate to one or more specific modes of transport – road, rail or inland waterway – covering the classes of dangerous goods.

A VTC is valid throughout the Member States of the European Community for 5 years, from either the date of issue, or from the date of successful completion of a refresher course.
