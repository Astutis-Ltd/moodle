Maintenance and inspection of fork lift trucks

Forklift trucks are both mobile work equipment and lifting equipment, and are subject to the requirements of Parts II and III of PUWER, and LOLER (see Element 7.2). The employer is obliged to ensure that work fork lift trucks are maintained in safe working order and are subject to proper inspection, maintenance and servicing, with thorough examinations at appropriate intervals, in accordance with the manufacturer’s or authorised supplier’s instructions.

Repairs and adjustments should only be made by suitably qualified and authorised personnel or contractors.

Depending on the nature of use, trucks should be subject to pre-use and / or weekly inspections.

Pre-use checks

At the beginning of each shift the operator should check the lift truck and report to the supervisor any defects which might affect its safe operation to ensure they are put right. Checks should include:

  1. Tyre pressures – ensuring that pneumatic tyres, if fitted, are inflated to the proper pressure
  2. Tyres checks – signs of damage such as swarf, nails or other embedded material, cuts and bubbles
  3. Parking brake, service brakes, and steering gear to ensure that they are working efficiently. Fuel, water and oil levels (internal combustion engines) to check for leaks
  4. Batteries of battery-operated lift trucks to check:
    1. that they are adequately charged and leak free
    2. that the charger is switched off
    3. the charge lead disconnected and properly stored
    4. the battery retention device is in place.
  5. Systems for lifting, tilting and manipulation, including attachments are working properly
  6. Hydraulic systems should be free from obvious leaks, and hydraulic fluid levels should be correct when the fork arms are in the parked position
  7. Audible warning signals, lights, and mirrors, if fitted, should all be in good working order.
