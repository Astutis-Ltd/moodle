Precautions

Dimensions

The platform should be as small as possible, compatible with the number of persons that it is intended to carry and the work they are likely to undertake.

The forward length of the platform should be less than 2 times the rated load centre distance of the truck, and the width of the platform should not overhang the truck load wheels by more than 250mm either side.

Floor

The floor of the platform should be of adequate strength, horizontal when attached to the truck in its elevated position, and slip resistant. Openings in mesh floors should be less than 15mm diameter.

Rails and toe boards

All platform edges shall be guarded by a top rail, at least one intermediate rail, and a 150mm toe board. The upper surface should be between 1 000mm and 1 100mm from the platform floor.

Overhead protection

Overhead protection is likely to be needed if the platform is being used where there are risks from falling objects, or other overhead hazards.

Gate

The access gate(if provided) should open inwards, upwardsor sideways and returnautomatically to the closed position. The gate should be self-locking in the closed position, or provided with an interlock device that locks the gate automatically, and ensures it cannot be opened once the platform is raised.

Hand holds

Suitably sized (to accommodate a gloved hand) and positioned handholds should be provided within the confines of the working platform.

Safety harness anchorage

Work restraint anchorages should be included on the working platform. They should be of sufficient strength to comply with BS EN 795:2012 Personal fall protection equipment. Anchor devices, and should be marked as work restraint anchorages that are inappropriate for the use of fall arrest equipment.

People on the working platform

The platform should be positioned to prevent the need for people to lean out of the raised working platform when carrying out their work. Work restraint harnesses will also help prevent workers from overbalancing and falling over the rails.

Method of securing

The platform must be properly secured and locked on to the forks or carriage, in accordance with the platform and truck manufacturer’s recommendations.

Preventing access to moving parts

Screens or guards should be fitted to the platform to prevent the risk of trapping and / or crushing by the mechanisms on the truck.

Tilting mechanism, side shift and variable geometry attachments

These should not be capable of movement whilst the working platform is elevated.

Truck operator

Truck operator The truck operator should remain at the controls of the truck while the platform is in an elevated position.

Truck operation

The truck / working platform combination should only be used on firm, well maintained and level surfaces, as gradients and uneven, or inconsistent, ground conditions can affect the stability of the truck.

The truck should not be moved while the working platform is elevated, other than minor controlled, positional adjustments. Note: Very Narrow Aisle (VNA) trucks, fitted with an elevated operator position and automatic speed limitation that are operating within VNA aisles, are an exception.

The parking brake should be applied whenever the working platform is elevated.

Where applicable, the transmission should be placed in neutral before elevating the platform, and stabilisers and / or axle locking devices deployed as per manufacturer’s instructions.

Communication

Adequate arrangements must be made for communication between the truck operator and persons on the platform, especially when raising and lowering. This may be by way of hand held radios or hand signals where verbal communication is difficult.

Training

People expected to work on platforms and trucks should be properly trained, and given full instructions on safe systems of working with platforms, including the action to be taken in the event of an emergency.

Segregation of work

The working area below the work at height should be segregated from the normal work activity. Warning cones, lights, barriers or signs should be used as appropriate, to prevent people and vehicles from approaching the work at height activity.

Maintenance

The platform should be used and maintained in good working order and repair, and in accordance with the instructions provided by the working platform manufacturer.

Exclusions from use

Non-integrated working platforms are not suitable for use on various types of trucks including:

  • those requiring manual sequencing of the lift / tilt controls to maintain the working platform horizontal while being lifted
  • variable reach trucks (tele-handlers) that can lift to a nominal height greater than 6m.

Visibility

It is recommended that the platform is painted in a conspicuous colour.

Warning signs

Permanent notices should be prominently displayed on the platform. These notices should:

  • Remind the operator to apply the parking brake and ensure the transmission is in neutral or equivalent before elevating the platform
  • Inform the truck operator to stay at the truck controls while the platform is in a raised position.

Identification plate

An identification plate should be attached to the platform giving the following information:

  1. name and address of the platform manufacturer or authorised representative
  2. serial number and year of manufacture
  3. un-laden weight of the platform and the position of the centre of gravity
  4. lost load centre (if the platform is carriage mounted)
  5. maximum allowable load in kg
  6. maximum number of persons that the platform is intended to carry.
