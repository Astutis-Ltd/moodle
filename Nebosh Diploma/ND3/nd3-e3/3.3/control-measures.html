Control measures

Reducing the quantity of dangerous substances to a minimum

The quantity of any dangerous substance stored or processed in plant and equipment should be kept as small as is reasonably practicable, having regard to the processes and operations being carried on.

Plant and equipment should be designed, installed and operated to prevent the unintentional accumulation or build-up of dangerous substances and their flammable residues. Ductwork for flammable dusts should be designed to maintain adequate velocity throughout its length, with smooth inner surfaces and large radius bends. Suitable access points should be provided for inspection and cleaning.

Measures to avoid or minimise the release of a dangerous substance

Plant and equipment handling or storing dangerous substances should be designed to prevent any premature loss of contents during its expected life and during foreseeable operating and emergency conditions. The plant or equipment should be able to withstand foreseeable corrosion and abrasion during use, and be compatible with any dangerous substances that may be present.

Work processes and operations should be designed to minimise the release of dangerous substances by using pipework, closed vessels and enclosed systems.

Where dangerous substances are liable to be spilled, or to leak and give rise to a hazardous situation, the spill / leak should be contained or run off to a container or to a safe place, or otherwise treated to make it safe.

Controlling the release of a dangerous substance at source

Adequate local exhaust ventilation should be provided to prevent or minimise releases into the workroom from access points, or openings, for charging or removing materials on plant and equipment.

Where there may be a release of dangerous substances that could give rise to an explosive atmosphere, the plant, equipment or process should be enclosed within a cabinet, or other suitable enclosure, and provided with mechanical ventilation.

Isolation valves should be provided to minimise leaks after use, to control leaks during use, and to enable safe isolation of the plant for maintenance.

Preventing the formation of an explosive atmosphere

Any plant, equipment or process that may release dangerous substances should preferably be located in the open air.

Avoiding ignition sources

Work equipment should be designed, constructed and maintained to ensure it cannot unintentionally ignite hazardous concentrations of dangerous substances, which may arise either in the workplace or within the equipment itself.

Avoiding adverse conditions that could result in harmful physical effects arising from a dangerous substance

Many chemical substances can give rise to harmful physical effects by generating heat and / or generating pressure from released gases and / or vapours following self-reaction, reaction with air or moisture, or reaction with other chemical substances. They may also release other flammable or toxic substances.

Plant used for chemical reaction processes should be designed, constructed, provided with sufficient monitoring and control, and operated so as to avoid any adverse conditions that could allow dangerous substances to give rise to hazardous heat and pressure effects, such as the uncontrolled release of the plant contents.

A chemical reaction hazard assessment should be undertaken to consider:

  • materials of construction
  • vessel design pressure
  • agitator configuration and speed
  • reactant feed controls
  • safe operating temperatures and pressures
  • heat transfer and cooling rates
  • process instrumentation and interlocks
  • process venting.
