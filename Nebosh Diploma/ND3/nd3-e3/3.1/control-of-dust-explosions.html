Control of dust explosions

The basic approach to preventing dust explosions is to prevent the sides of the ‘dust explosion pentagon’ coming together.

Engineering solutions should be implemented to eliminate ignition sources caused by mechanical failures, overheating of bearings and drive belts (condition monitoring and planned preventative maintenance), sparks from tramp metal (X-rays and magnetic metal recovery systems), electrical sparks (specification of equipment suitable for use in flammable atmospheres) and static electricity (earthing and equipotential bonding).

Dust generation should be eliminated or controlled through specifying different materials, pneumatic dust collection, and good housekeeping / vacuuming to address settled dust.

Oxygen levels can be kept below levels that could sustain combustion through enclosure and inerting, notably at points where dusts or powders are transferred into flammable liquids.

Mitigatory measures to limit the damage / consequences of a dust cloud explosion include site layout design to ensure the separation and segregation of high-risk areas and the specification of explosion suppression and venting systems as discussed in section 4 of this element.
