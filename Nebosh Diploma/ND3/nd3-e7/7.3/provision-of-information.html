Prevention and testing strategies

The designer or supplier of a pressure system or component part should consider the most effective way of providing the appropriate information to those who need it. The following items should be considered where relevant:

  • design standards used and evidence of compliance with national / European / international standards or documentation showing conformity
  • design pressures (maximum and minimum)
  • fatigue life
  • design temperatures (maximum and minimum)
  • creep life
  • intended contents, especially where the design has been carried out for a specific process
  • flow rates and discharge capacities
  • corrosion allowances
  • wall thickness
  • volume capacities, especially for storage vessels. Depending on the intended contents these may be expressed as maximum volume, pressure or filling ratio
  • materials of construction.

  • standard to which the vessel was constructed
  • minimum and maximum allowable pressures
  • design temperature.

A CE mark should also be affixed.

None of the information may be removed or falsified.

Safe operating limits

A pressure system must not be operated unless the safe operating limits have been established. Where the system consists of a standard production item, the designer / manufacturer should assess the safe operating limits and pass the relevant information to the user / owner.

Small, simple systems may need little more than the establishment of the maximum pressure for safe operation. Complex, larger systems are likely to need a wide range of conditions specified such as:

  • maximum and minimum temperatures
  • maximum and minimum pressures
  • nature, volumes and flow rates of contents
  • operating times
  • heat input or coolant flow.

In all cases the safe operating limits should incorporate a suitable margin of safety.
