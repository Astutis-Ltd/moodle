Prevention and testing strategies

Design and construction

The designer, manufacturer, importer or supplier of a pressure system should consider and take due account of the following, where applicable:

  1. The expected working life (the design life) of the system
  2. The properties of the contained fluid (special considerations will apply if the pressure vessel is to be used for low temperature storage, e.g. Liquid nitrogen, or a highly corrosive material)
  3. The most onerous combination of temperature, pressure and other relevant parameters to which the equipment may be subjected under reasonably foreseeable circumstances. Including the conditions which will exist during start-up, shutdown and stand-by operation
  4. The need for system examination to ensure continued integrity throughout its design life (vessels should be provided with suitably sized openings, including manholes and handholes where appropriate, to allow adequate examination of the interior)
  5. Any foreseeable changes to the design conditions (including allowances for corrosion and wear from agitators)
  6. Conditions for standby operation
  7. Protection against system failure, using suitable measuring, control and protective devices as appropriate:
    • where part of the system has a lower safe operating limit than other parts, suitable pressure-reducing valves, safety valves, pressure relief and indicating devices should be provided
    • suitable measuring or indicating devices should be provided to give clear indications of relevant critical conditions within the system, e.g. temperatures, pressures, liquid levels
    • equipment, such as boilers, in which a low level (or into which a low flow rate) of water could lead to unsafe conditions should be fitted with at least one suitable water level indicator and to an alarm
    • the devices and associated inlet and outlet pipework should have an adequate discharge capacity in order to limit pressure to within the safe operating limits.
  8. Suitable materials for each component part:
    • e.g. steam boiler stop (crown) valves should not be made from flake graphite (grey) cast iron
    • plastic pipes are not suitable for use where there is the possibility of their becoming brittle or otherwise damaged due to exposure to heat or other adverse conditions.
  9. The external forces expected to be exerted on the system including thermal loads and wind loading:
    • reasonably foreseeable vibration or shock loading, e.g. from water hammer should be considered and suitable expansion bends and / or joints and drains should be incorporated in the pipework as necessary.
  10. Safe access for operation, maintenance and examination, including the fitting of access safety devices or suitable guards, as appropriate:
    • e.g. equipment such as an autoclave which needs to be regularly accessed during process operations for loading and unloading, should be provided with suitable door safety devices.

Modification and repair

Any person who modifies or repairs a pressure system at work must ensure that nothing about the way in which it is modified or repaired gives rise to danger or otherwise impairs the operation of any protective device or inspection facility.

The following should be taken into account:

  1. the original design specification
  2. the duty for which the system is to be used after the repair or modification, including any change in relevant fluid
  3. the effects any such work may have on the integrity of the pressure system
  4. whether the protective devices are still adequate
  5. continued suitability of the written scheme of examination.

Repair or modification of non-pressure containing parts of the system should be carried out so that the integrity of the pressure system and the operation of any protective devices is not adversely affected.

Any repair or modification (including extensions or additions) should be designed in accordance with appropriate standards, taking into account the expected future duty of the system as well as the original design specification.

All work should be done by a competent person, or following consultation with a competent person.
