Types and frequencies of inspection and the statutory examination of pressure systems

Pressure systems are ‘work equipment’ and as such must comply with the requirements of PUWER, including Regulation 6 (Inspection) as discussed earlier in section 7.3.

Where the safety of work equipment depends upon installation conditions or can be affected by deterioration due to environmental conditions the equipment should be inspected before first use and periodically thereafter to ensure that deterioration over time does not give rise to dangerous situations.

Note: these concerns should be identified through general risk assessment as required by Regulation 3 of the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999.

Regular inspections are required wherever work equipment is of a type where the safe operation is critically dependent on its condition in use and deterioration would lead to a significant risk to the operator or other worker.

The extent of the inspection required will depend on the potential risks from the work equipment. Inspection should include, where appropriate, visual checks (e.g. pre-use), functional checks and tests.

Inspections under Regulation 6 of PUWER are only required where the statutory examination under the Pressure Systems Safety Regulations 2000 (PSSR) do not fully cover all the significant health and safety risks which are likely to arise from the use of the equipment, in a way that satisfies the requirements of PUWER.

Regulation 8 of PSSR requires the user of an installed system / owner of a mobile system to ensure that a Written Scheme of Examination (WSE) has been prepared before a pressure system is operated.

The WSE should be drawn up by a competent person, or if drawn up by someone other than a competent person certified as suitable by a competent person and should specify the nature and frequency of inspection of the following system parts:

  • all protective devices
  • every pressure vessel and every pipeline in which (in either case) a defect may give rise to danger
  • those parts of the pipework in which a defect may give rise to danger, and such parts of the system shall be identified in the scheme.

Note: Simple pressure vessels are exempt from the requirement for a WSE. Simple pressure vessels have the following characteristics or limitations as defined in the Simple Pressure Vessels (Safety) Regulations 1991:

  • Intended to contain air or nitrogen at a gauge pressure >0.5 bar but less than or equal to 30 bar
  • Not intended to be exposed to flame (i.e. ‘unfired’)
  • Manufactured in series, i.e. more than one vessel of the same type is manufactured during a given period by the same continuous manufacturing processes, in accordance with a common design of welded non-alloy steel or non-alloy aluminium construction or non-age hardening aluminium alloy
  • A maximum working pressure (PS) of not more than 30 bar, and a PS.V (the product of PS and the vessel’s capacity expressed in litres) of not more than 10,000 bar litres
  • A minimum working temperature of not lower than -50°C, and a maximum working temperature of not >300°C for steel vessels and not >100°C for aluminium or aluminium alloy vessels.

Additionally, vessels consist of either:

  • a cylindrical component with circular cross-section, closed at each end, each end either outwardly dished or flat and also co-axial with the cylindrical component
  • two co-axial outwardly dished ends.
