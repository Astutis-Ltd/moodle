Portable electric sander and abrasive wheel – benchtop grinder

Portable electric sander

Mechanical hazards

  • Contact with abrasive material – abrasion
  • Entanglement – disc sander
  • Drawing in – belt sander.

Non-mechanical hazards

  • Dust – respiratory hazard and fire / explosion hazard
  • Noise
  • Vibration
  • Electricity
  • Trailing cable – trip hazard
  • Manual handling
  • Environmental hazards, for example, exposure to weather.

Control measures

  • Double insulted equipment
  • Reduced voltage
  • Residual Current Device (RCD
  • Regular inspection, testing and maintenance
  • Competent operators
  • Pre use inspections
  • Ensure abrasive material is properly fixed
  • Inspect work surface to be sanded for obstructions, for example, nail heads
  • Regular emptying of dust bags
  • Suitable work clothing
  • Suitable PPE – eye protection, dust mask, hearing protection as necessary
  • Safe storage and handling.

Abrasive wheel – benchtop grinder

Mechanical hazards

  • Contact with rotating wheel
  • Trap between wheel and tool rest
  • Entanglement around axle
  • Ejection of parts of work piece or from burst wheel.

Non-mechanical hazards

  • Dust
  • Sparks / fire
  • Electricity
  • Noise
  • Vibration
  • Stability of machine
  • Positioning / working posture.

Control measures

  • Wheel enclosed as much as possible
  • Adjustable guard over exposed part of wheel
  • Wearing of eye protection
  • Bolted securely to workbench
  • Electric wiring insulated and enclosed in machine casing
  • Regularly inspected for general and electrical safety
  • Maintained in accordance with manufacturers recommendations
  • Local Exhaust Ventilation (LEV)
  • Used only by competent, authorised personnel.
