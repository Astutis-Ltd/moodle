B-SAFE and electric burn

The dangers (risk of personal injury) associated with electricity can be remembered by the acronym B-SAFE, as summarised in the table below.

Burns

Shock

From contact with a live conductor.

Note: mains electricity (230V AC) can kill.

Arcing

Occurs when electricity flows through the air from one conductor to another.

May cause burns or shock if a person is the second conductor or is close to the arc.

Produces intense heat which may cause a fire or explosion.

Fire

Explosion

Electricity could be the source of ignition in a potentially flammable or explosive atmosphere.

Electric burn

Electric burns are different from burns due to fire, arcing or explosion. Electric burns are due to the heating effect caused by the passage of electric current through body tissues.

They are most commonly associated with electric shock, and often occur in and on the skin layers at the point of contact with the electrical conductors which gave rise to the electric shock.

At high frequencies, such as Radio Frequencies (RF) and microwaves, it may not even be necessary for contact to be made with live conductors for an electric burn to be received.

Electric burns are usually painful and very slow to heal. Permanent scarring is common.
