Suitable and sufficient lighting conditions

Regulation 8 of the Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992 (as amended) requires every workplace to have suitable and sufficient lighting, which should so far as is reasonably practicable, be by natural light.

Suitable and sufficient emergency lighting should also be provided in any room in which workers are exposed to danger in the event of failure of artificial lighting.

Other regulations requiring provision of lighting include:

Regulation Requirement
Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015 (CDM)

Regulation 35 - Lighting

Every place of work and approach thereto and every traffic route shall be provided with suitable and sufficient lighting, which shall be, so far as is reasonably practicable, by natural light.

The colour of any artificial lighting provided shall not adversely affect or change the perception of any sign or signal provided for the purposes of health and safety.

Suitable and sufficient secondary lighting shall be provided in any place where there would be a risk to the health or safety of any person in the event of failure of primary artificial lighting.

Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations 1998 (PUWER)

Regulation 21 - Lighting

 Every employer shall ensure that suitable and sufficient lighting, which takes account of the operations to be carried out, is provided at any place where a person uses work equipment.

Good lighting at work is important to ensure the efficient identification and avoidance of hazards in the workplace. Poor lighting can cause eyestrain, migraine and headaches, and is also linked to Sick Building Syndrome in new and refurbished buildings.

Good lighting should be visually comfortable and provide sufficient quantity of light, of a suitable quality.

Different levels of lighting are required for different types of work. In general, the more detailed the task, the greater the light requirement. A process control room should be lit at an illuminance of 300 lux, a corridor or walkway may only require 50 lux, whilst studying an engineering drawing may require 750 lux.
