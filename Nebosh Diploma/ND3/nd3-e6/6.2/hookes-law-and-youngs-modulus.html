Hooke’s law and Young’s modulus

In mechanics and physics, Hooke’s law of elasticity is an approximation that states that the extension of a spring is in direct proportion with the load applied to it. Many materials obey this law, as long as the load does not exceed the material’s elastic limit. Materials for which Hooke’s law is a useful approximation are known as linear-elastic. Steel, carbon-fibre, and glass are linear materials. Rubber is a non-linear material.

Young’s modulus (or tensile modulus) (E) is a material property that describes the stiffness of an elastic material. It is the ratio of the uniaxial stress over the uniaxial strain, in the range of stress in which Hooke’s law holds.

E = tensile stress ÷ tensile strain

As strain has no units Young’s modulus has the same units as stress, i.e. Nm-2.
