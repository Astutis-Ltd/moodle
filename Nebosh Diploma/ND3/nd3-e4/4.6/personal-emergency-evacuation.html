Personal emergency evacuation plan

Disabled workers cannot be put at a substantial disadvantage by a provision, criterion or practice of their employer. The employer has a duty to make reasonable adjustments to ensure, so far as is reasonable, that a disabled person has the same access to everything that is involved in getting and doing a job as a non-disabled person.

A Personal Emergency Evacuation Plan (PEEP) is an individual plan, tailored to the needs of a disabled employee (also regular visitors or service users) that defines the route the person is likely to take, and what support they will need in the event of an evacuation.

Arrangements for occasional disabled visitors or service users can be made within standard evacuation plans, and offered to disabled visitors as part of the reception procedure.

    The key questions to be addressed by a PEEP are:

  1. How will the individual be given information on the evacuation procedure?
  2. How will the individuals’ attention be drawn to the need to evacuate?
  3. What support (assistance and / or equipment) will be necessary to ensure the individual can safely evacuate the premises?

    Measures might include:

  • Provision of emergency instructions in different formats for different disabilities, for example:
    • braille, large print or audio instructions and a tactile map of the building for an employee with a visual impairment
    • pictorial instructions or a physical demonstration of an evacuation route for those with learning disabilities.
  • Means of alerting people of the need to evacuate, for example, visual fire warning system, or vibrating alarm for a deaf employee.
  • Installation of evacuation chairs:
    • training staff in the use of evacuation chairs, and making them aware of the people they would be expected to help
    • running evacuation drills using evacuation chairs and other measures.
  • Colour coding of escape routes for those with orientation disorders, autism or dyslexia
  • ‘Buddy’ systems to provide extra support
  • Provision of an easy means for a disabled person to contact someone for help, advice or information.
