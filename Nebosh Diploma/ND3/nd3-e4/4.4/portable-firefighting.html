Portable Fire-fighting Equipment (PFE)

Provision of PFE

Suitable fire-fighting equipment should be readily available at all times to ensure small fires do not grow into large fires. On most occasions the equipment available will be of the hand held portable type (portable fire-fighting equipment) but in some circumstances fixed or automatic fire-fighting equipment may be provided.

Class of fire Provision

Class A carbonaceous solids

This type of PFE is generally present in all premises and occupancies.

The minimum provision of Class A PFE would be 2 x 13A* extinguishers on each storey for floor spaces of up to 400m2. For smaller premises <100m2 1 x 13A extinguisher would suffice. For larger spaces the provision can be calculated by multiplying the floor area in m2 x 0.065.

*13A rating means that the extinguisher is capable of extinguishing a fire in a 1.3m test crib under test conditions.

Class B flammable liquids

PFE for Class B fires should be provided for on a room by room basis.

The requirement is based on the surface area of a single open-topped container of flammable liquid, for example, mixing vessel, dip tank, spillage in bunded area.

A rating of 21B means that the extinguisher is capable of extinguishing a fire of 21 litres of fuel in a test tray with a surface area of approx. 0.66m2 under test conditions.

Different types of extinguisher with the same rating may have different characteristics.

Powders are probably the most effective medium against Class B fires but are not effective if part of the fuel surface is shielded from the powder discharge.

Foam is effective against contained fires where it will provide semi- permanent protection, but is not effective against running fires.

Re-ignition of fuel is less likely with a foam extinguisher than powders or gases.

Class C gas

Provision of one specialist Class C PFE is required where gas has been identified as a specific fire hazard in a risk assessment.

Note: For Class C fires the fuel supply should be isolated as a priority.

Class D metal

 Provision of specialist Class D PFE is required where metal has been identified as a specific fire hazard in a risk assessment.

Class F fats and cooking oils

Provision of PFE for Class F fires is based on the ability to cope with a fire of specified surface areas under test conditions.

Note: For Class F fires the fuel supply should be isolated as a priority.
