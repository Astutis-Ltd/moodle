Safety I, safety II and safety differently

The below table amalgamates comparison tables put forward by Hollnagel (2014) and Dekker (2015) in their respective books. The gaps are left to illustrate the differences in thinking. (H) and (D) are used in the traditional safety column to indicate whether the point was made by Hollnagel (H) or Dekker (D).


Traditional / Modernist / safety I Safety II Safety Differently/New era
Definition of safety As few things as possible go wrong (H)

the absence of negatives (injuries / incidents) that show where things go wrong (D) 		As many things as possible go right Safety is the presence of capabilities, capacities and competencies that make things go right
Safety management principle Reactive – responds when something bad happens or is categorised as an unacceptable risk (H) Proactive, continually trying to anticipate developments and events
Responsibility for safety Safety is a bureaucratic accountability directed upwards in an organisation (D)
Safety is an ethical responsibility directed downward in an organisation
Explanation of accidents Accidents are caused by failures and malfunctions. The purpose of an investigation is to identify causes and contributory factors (H) Things basically happen the same way regardless of outcome. The purpose of the investigation is to understand how things usually go right as a basis for explaining how things occasionally go wrong
Cause-effect relationships Linear and unproblematic (D) The causality credo – Causes are constructed not found. The process is social not logical / rational Complex and non-linear
Attitude to human factor Humans are predominantly seen as a liability or a hazard (H)

People are a problem to control (D) 		Humans are a resource, necessary for system flexibility and resilience People are a solution to harness
Role of performance variability Harmful should be prevented as far as possible (H) Inevitable, but also useful. Should be monitored and managed
Vocabulary About control constraint and human deficit (D)
About empowerment diversity and human opportunity
