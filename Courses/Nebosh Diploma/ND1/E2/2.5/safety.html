Safety I, safety II and safety differently

The below table amalgamates comparison tables put forward by Hollnagel (2014) and Dekker (2015) in their respective books. The gaps are left to illustrate the differences in thinking. (H) and (D) are used in the traditional safety column to indicate whether the point was made by Hollnagel (H) or Dekker (D).

Definition of safety

  • Traditional / Modernist / safety I : As few things as possible go wrong (H), the absence of negatives (injuries / incidents) that show where things go wrong (D)
  • Safety II: As many things as possible go right
  • Safety Differently/New era: Safety is the presence of capabilities, capacities and competencies that make things go right

Safety management principle

  • Traditional / Modernist / safety I : Reactive – responds when something bad happens or is categorised as an unacceptable risk (H)
  • Safety II: Proactive, continually trying to anticipate developments and events
  • Safety Differently/New era: -

Responsibility for safety

  • Traditional / Modernist / safety I : Safety is a bureaucratic accountability directed upwards in an organisation (D)
  • Safety II: -
  • Safety Differently/New era: Safety is an ethical responsibility directed downward in an organisation

Explanation of accidents

  • Traditional / Modernist / safety I : Accidents are caused by failures and malfunctions. The purpose of an investigation is to identify causes and contributory factors (H)
  • Safety II: Things basically happen the same way regardless of outcome. The purpose of the investigation is to understand how things usually go right as a basis for explaining how things occasionally go wrong
  • Safety Differently/New era:

Cause-effect relationships

  • Traditional / Modernist / safety I : Linear and unproblematic (D)
  • Safety II: The causality credo – Causes are constructed not found. The process is social not logical / rational
  • Safety Differently/New era: Complex and non-linear

Attitude to human factor

  • Traditional / Modernist / safety I : Humans are predominantly seen as a liability or a hazard (H), People are a problem to control (D)
  • Safety II: Humans are a resource, necessary for system flexibility and resilience
  • Safety Differently/New era: People are a solution to harness

Role of performance variability

  • Traditional / Modernist / safety I : Harmful should be prevented as far as possible (H)
  • Safety II: Inevitable, but also useful. Should be monitored and managed
  • Safety Differently/New era: -

Vocabulary

  • Traditional / Modernist / safety I : About control constraint and human deficit (D)
  • Safety II: -
  • Safety Differently/New era: About control constraint and human deficit (D)
