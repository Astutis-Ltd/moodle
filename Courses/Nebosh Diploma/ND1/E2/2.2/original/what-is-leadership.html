What is leadership?


Finding a consensual definition of leadership is surprisingly difficult because of the different types of groups that have leaders, the different contexts of leadership and the different theoretical perspectives of research.

Example definitions of leaders or leadership include:

...an influencing process aimed at goal achievement
(Stogdill, 1950)
...those members of the group who (most) influence the activities of the group
(Krech et al, 1962)
...a process of social influence through which an individual enlists and mobilises the aid of others in the attainment of a collective goal
(Chemers, 2001)

The general features of leaders / leadership are generally acknowledged as:

‣
Vision – a clear idea of what is to be achieved and why

‣
Influence – the ability to communicate effectively and convince others to contribute

‣
Visibility – in particular when it is needed. Leaders visibly take the lead in a crisis

‣
Followers – by default and by definition a leader must have followers.
