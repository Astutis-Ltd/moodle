Safety culture and climate


The term ‘safety culture’ has been widely used since the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) report into the Chernobyl nuclear accident (1986). The term ‘safety climate’ has been used over a similar period, and both terms have often been used interchangeably to describe employees’ attitudes to safety.

In general, safety climate is regarded as more superficial and transient than safety culture, and as an indicator or ‘snapshot’ of an organisation’s overall safety culture. There are still no universally accepted definitions of either term.

HSE commissioned research based upon the work done by Cooper suggests that a useful distinction between the concepts is to use safety culture to refer to the behavioural aspects (i.e. what people do), and the situational aspects of the company (i.e. what the organisation has), and ‘safety climate’ to refer to the psychological characteristics of employees (i.e. how people feel).
