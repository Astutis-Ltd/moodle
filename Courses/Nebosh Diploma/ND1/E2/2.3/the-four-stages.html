The four stages of consultation

Consultation is a two-way process involving an exchange of views between parties. Consultation in health and safety matters should involve open dialogue between the employer (or those representing management) and employees (or those representing the workers). Views, opinions and relevant information should be shared openly and in a timely manner. Consultation should occur prior to any decision being made by the employer.

HSG263 advises employers that consultation involves:

  • Employers and employees talking to each other about issues;
  • Employers listening to employees concerns and bringing employers concerns to the employeeâ€™s attention;
  • Employers and employees seeking and sharing views and information;
  • Employers and employees discussing issues in good time; and
  • Employers considering what employees say before making decisions.
