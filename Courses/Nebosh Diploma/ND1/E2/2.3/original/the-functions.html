The functions and rights of health
and safety representatives


The Trade Union and Labour Relations (Consolidation) Act 1992:

‣
Defines trade unions and their various legal entitlements and obligations

‣
Protects the right of workers to join or leave a trade union without suffering discrimination or detriment

‣
Provides a framework for collective bargaining with employers

‣
Protects the right of workers in a union to take industrial action when reasonable notice is given, and when that action is ‘in contemplation or furtherance of a trade dispute’.


Part III of the Act deals with ‘rights in relation to union membership and activities’ and includes sections 168-170 which address ‘Time off for trade union duties and activities.’

The provisions relate to employees who are an official of an independent trade union recognised by the employer (trade union officials - including safety representatives).


Section 168: Time off for carrying out trade union duties

Trade union officials are permitted to take time off during working hours for the purpose of carrying out official duties such as:

‣
negotiations / collective bargaining

‣
performance on behalf of employees of the employer of functions which the employer has agreed may be performed by the trade union

‣
receipt of information from the employer and consultation / negotiations under the Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) Regulations (TUPE).


Trade union officials are also permitted to take time off during his working hours for the purpose of undergoing training in aspects of industrial relations. The amount of time off permitted and the purposes for which, the occasions on which and any conditions subject to which time off may be taken off have to be reasonable in all the circumstances having regard to any relevant provisions of the ACAS Code of Practice.


Section 168A: Time off for union learning representatives

Section 168A makes similar provision for Trade Union learning representatives to take time off during working hours to exercise their duties and to receive training.


Section 169: Payment for time off under section 168

Where an employee is permitted to take time off under section 168 / 168A the employer has to pay him for the time taken off. Where the employee’s pay for the work does not vary with the amount of work done, he shall be paid as if he had worked at that work for the whole of that time.

Where the employee’s pay varies with the amount of work done the payment is calculated by reference to the average hourly earnings for that work.


Section 170: Time off for trade union activities

Employees who are members of an independent trade union recognised by the employer must be permitted to take time off during working hours for the purpose of taking part in:

‣
any activities of the union

‣
any activities in relation to which the employee is acting as a representative of the union. The right does not extend to activities which consist of industrial action.
