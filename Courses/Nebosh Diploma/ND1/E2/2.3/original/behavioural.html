Behavioural aspects associated with
consultation


The behavioural aspects of consultation may be considered at the individual or group level.


Tokenism

If the leadership team or individual corporate leader does not see the value in true consultation with the workforce and the benefits of workforce engagement there is a danger that efforts to consult will not be made seriously and only a token gesture will be made. Tokenism is about making half-hearted gestures perhaps for appearances sake rather than because of any real enthusiasm to act.

Research into employee (or other stakeholder) engagement recognises a spectrum of efforts from tokenism at the bottom end through legally compliant arrangements for formal consultation (perhaps with little enthusiasm to achieve anything other than compliance) through to concerted efforts to encourage workforce participation, involvement and engagement.


Peer group pressure

Peer group pressure arises where a group has established its values and normed its behaviours and will not tolerate group members expressing incompatible opinions or behaving differently.

Formal and informal discussions with groups of workers such as safety committees, safety circles, departmental meetings etc may be affected if there is a reluctance to express different opinions.


Conflict

As discussed earlier conflicts arise where two individuals or groups (e.g. ‘management’ and ‘workers’) have incompatible concerns. Collaborative efforts between committed managers and engaged workers should ensure that concerns are shared and that practical effective solutions are found.


The role of the health and safety practitioner in the consultative process

The safety practitioner as ‘competent person’ should play a significant part in consultation processes, for example:

‣
the design and implementation of legally compliant formal consultation arrangements

‣
participation in safety committees and the sharing of management information on progress against action plans and findings from monitoring and auditing activities

‣
provision of impartial professional advice on legal compliance and best practice

‣
advocating the benefits of informal consultation arrangements and workforce engagement generally.
