Post-modern organisations – use of contractors

Typical 21st century ‘post-modern’ organisations have the following features:

  • information rich
  • de-layered
  • downsized
  • few boundaries
  • high commitment highly skilled
  • autonomous
  • knowledge workers
  • subcontracting
  • networking.

Handy described this sort of organisation in his shamrock model where employees (qualified professional technicians and managers) are one of three ‘leaves’, contracted specialists are another leaf, and a flexible labour force, including temporary and part-time workers, are the third leaf.
