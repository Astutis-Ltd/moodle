Authority, responsibility and accountability

Authority is the right or power assigned to a manager in order to take action to achieve certain organisational objectives.

Responsibility is a duty or obligation to perform an assigned task or activity.

Accountability is the liability created for the use of authority. It is the answerability for performance of the assigned duties whereby decisions must be justified and consequences accepted.

Output standards can be used to specify how individuals will be held accountable for their health and safety responsibilities.

People with specific responsibilities for health and safety can be held accountable through:

  • individual job descriptions containing references to health and safety responsibilities
  • performance review and appraisal systems measuring and rewarding individual performance in health and safety activities
  • procedures identifying and acting upon failures which may be integrated with normal disciplinary arrangements.

Power

Power is the capacity to cause another person to behave in a certain way, no matter what that person’s desires or defences. The term ‘authority’ is often used for power perceived as legitimate by the social structure. The study of power in a society is referred to as ‘politics’.

French and Raven (1959) identified five critical bases of social power:

  • Legitimate – the right to make demands, and expect compliance because of position in a hierarchical management structure
  • Reward – the ability to compensate another for compliance
  • Expert – from a person’s superior skill and knowledge
  • Referent – from setting a benchmark of performance that others aspire to
  • Coercive – the capacity to punish others for noncompliance.
