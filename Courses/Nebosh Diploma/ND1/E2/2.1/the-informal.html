The informal organisation

People’s social and psychological needs mean that the development of an informal organisation is inevitable. Individual relationships and social groupings will occur independently from the formal structure and may have quite different norms of behaviour.

These informal relationships and social groupings are not defined, they are flexible and employees may well have varying degrees of involvement over time.

The morale and performance of staff can be greatly influenced by the informal organisation, which can:

  • help provide a sense of belonging
  • provide ‘the grapevine’ for quick informal communication
  • motivate staff through status and social interaction
  • control members through norming of behaviour
  • highlight any weaknesses in the formal organisation.
