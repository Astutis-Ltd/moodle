Including third parties in management
arrangements


Management arrangements are essential for ensuring the effective co-operation and co-ordination of efforts between multiple parties to ensure that:

‣
Hazards have been identified and are understood

‣
Risks have been evaluated

‣
Appropriate preventive and protective measures, including emergency arrangements are in place

‣
That all parties and workers understand their role in ensuring the safety of everyone involved in, or affected by the work.





The above image represents a situation where a client (the employer) appoints a contractor to do work on site, where the work will involve the contractor's employees and a specialist sub-contractor, who will be working in the same area as the employer's own staff.

Each employer has responsibility for ensuring the health and safety of their own employees and any non-employees that may be affected.

Effective communication systems are required to ensure that:

‣
Activities are co-ordinated to ensure the health and safety of all

‣
Information on risks and control measures is provided to all parties and workers involved.


The extent of the responsibilities of each party will depend on the circumstances.
