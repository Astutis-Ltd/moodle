Definitions of third parties

A third party is an individual who does not have a direct connection with a legal transaction but might be affected by it.

Visitor

A visitor is a person who is invited onto premises or is permitted to be there.

All lawful visitors, whether on site for the benefit of the occupier or not, are owed a common duty of care to ensure they are kept reasonably safe while on site.

Trespasser

A trespasser is generally a person without lawful authority to be in particular vicinity. In common law a trespasser is a person who:

  • goes onto premises without invitation or permission
  • remains on premises after the invitation or permission has been removed
  • visits a part of the premises to which the invitation or permission does not extend
  • deposits goods on premises without permission.

Contractor

A contractor is a party engaged to perform a specific task, without direction from the engaging party (client).

An independent contractor (i.e. one who has control over the undertaking of the work) is responsible for any torts arising from their work.

Agency Worker

An agency worker is someone who has a contract with an agency (e.g. recruitment / ‘temp’ agency) but works temporarily for a hirer. Agency workers are classed as ‘workers’ rather than ‘employees’.

Member of the public

A member of the public is a person who is ‘not at work’ and who does not fit into other categories such as a trespasser. The term covers people off-site such as neighbours and passers-by.

It has been held that the common law duty of care is not limited to workers in the workplace, but could extend to those nearby residents affected.
