Regulation of health and safety performance through internal rules and procedures

Regardless of whether an organisation is subject to legislative requirements or is free to self-regulate, it will be essential for it to develop and implement its own internal rules and procedures. Rules and procedures will be devised at a local level to provide workers with a definitive set of parameters within which they must operate. Not only will such rules and procedures help to ensure greater standards of safety and quality, but they will also form the basis of disciplinary hearings in the event of a breach.

In order for rules and procedures to be effective, the following must be true:

  • The rules and procedures must be relevant to the workplace and / or task
  • They must be kept up to date
  • Adequate, appropriate levels of supervision must be in place to identify failures to follow the rules and ensure compliance
  • The organisation must have the will to sanction any breaking of the rules.

It is widely accepted that adopting a ‘blame culture’ is counter-productive to good and effective safety management. Furthermore, an over-abundance of rules and procedures is likely to be perceived by the workforce as excessive, the danger then being that they will fail to differentiate between those rules that serve a useful and important purpose in managing risks and those that are superfluous – the end result being that all rules are ignored. Such situations are likely to be characterised by a poor safety culture with increasing injury and illness rates.

It might also be said that a true ‘no-blame’ culture is almost as ineffective – if workers are not held accountable when appropriate, there is the possibility that they will continue to violate rules in the knowledge that there will be no punishment.

Many organisations now adopt a ‘fair blame’ approach. This recognises that the first option is not to blame a worker when an incident occurs but that, should a worker be found to have violated a rule or procedure, then they can expect to be disciplined.
