The purpose of enforcement

Health and safety legislation imposes a range of duties on employers and others to ensure the health, safety and welfare of people at work and the health and safety of others who could be affected by the work. The required standards of behaviour captured in legislation are the minimum acceptable standards of the society at that time.

Enforcement agencies are required to ensure that minimum acceptable standards are met and maintained or exceeded and that where the required standards are not met that the duty holders are held to account.

In the UK the law on health and safety at work is regulated by the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) and Local Authorities (LAs). The role of the regulators is to ensure that businesses are effectively and proportionately managing their health and safety risks to workers and others. The regulators are able to use a range of interventions to influence, encourage and advise businesses to improve the management of health and safety risks and, where necessary, hold to account those who fail to meet their responsibilities.

Enforcement is intended to ensure that duty holders:

  • deal immediately with serious risks
  • comply with the law
  • are held to account if they fail in their responsibilities.

The emphasis of the enforcement agencies efforts is on prevention but strong punitive action is taken where appropriate, such as when people are put at serious risk or there is deliberate flouting of the law.
