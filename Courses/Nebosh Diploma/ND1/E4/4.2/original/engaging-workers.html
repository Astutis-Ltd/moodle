Engaging workers in the risk assessment
process


A risk assessment should be completed by someone with a knowledge of the activity, process or material that is being assessed. Workers and their safety representatives are a valuable source of information.

There is a real difference between ‘work as imagined’ (the way management believes that work should be done) and ‘work as done’ (the way operational staff actually work at the sharp end, reacting to the work and the working environment).

Gaining insight into the way work is actually done delivers a more authentic risk assessment and the development of pragmatic controls that allow workers to work safely.

If workers are involved in risk assessment and the development of risk controls they are invested in the process and are more likely to own the risk controls and work in accordance with them.
