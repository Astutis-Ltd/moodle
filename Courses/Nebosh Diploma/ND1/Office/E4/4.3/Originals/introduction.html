Introduction


Risk (in the broader context of enterprise risk management) is defined in ISO 31000 – Risk Management Principles and Guidelines as: the effect of uncertainty on objectives.

The following notes are offered in support of the definition:

‣
an effect is a deviation from the expected which may be positive and/or negative

‣
objectives can have different aspects (such as financial, health and safety, and environmental goals) and can apply at different levels (such as strategic, organisation-wide, project, product and process)

‣
risk is often characterised by reference to potential events and consequences, or a combination of these

‣
risk is often expressed in terms of a combination of the consequences of an event (including changes in circumstances) and the associated likelihood of occurrence.


Note also the link between risks and objectives. Risk management processes can most easily be applied when the objectives of the organisation are comprehensive and fully stated.

Risk management is defined as:

…co-ordinated activities to direct and control an organisation with regard to risk.


Risk management is a central part of the strategic management of any organisation. It is the systematic process whereby organisations address the risks attached to their activities. A successful risk management framework should be:

‣
proportionate to the level of risk in the organisation

‣
effectively aligned with other corporate activities

‣
responsive to changing circumstances.


Risk management is concerned with the assessment of significant risks and the implementation of suitable risk responses with the aim of achieving maximum sustainable value from all organisational activities.

Effective risk management increases the probability of success and reduces both the probability of failure and the level of uncertainty associated with achieving the objectives of the organisation.
