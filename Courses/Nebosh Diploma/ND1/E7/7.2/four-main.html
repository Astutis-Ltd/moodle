Four main endeavours

For the purposes of the NEBOSH diploma syllabus there are four main types of endeavour that organisations engage in to demonstrate that they are socially responsible.

It is not logical or practical to expect all organisations to engage in the same types of CSR activities. What suits a particular organisation will be driven by a range of diverse factors including, industrial sector, financial status and societal environment.

Philanthropic efforts

  • Donating time, money, and other resources to charities and Non-Governmental Organisations (NGOâ€™s) on a local, national, or international level
  • Donating to worthy causes such as: human rights projects, natural disaster relief aid, and education programmes.

Environmental protection and sustainability

  • Efforts to reduce the organisations carbon footprint
  • Environmental conservation projects
  • Assisting local community efforts by contributing resources to food drives.

Company diversity

  • Acknowledge and address the need for a diverse workforce
  • Implement policies and procedures in line with best practise for equality and diversity in the workplace.

Volunteering commitments

  • Improving local profile by working together with the local community
  • Providing employees with opportunities (time or resources) to help improve the local community and environment.
