The benefits of CSR

Having a CSR policy and actively engaging in socially responsible practises shows stakeholders that the organisation cares about the communities it operates in and is actively taking steps to improve the local communities and environment.

This can help to develop trust between the organisation and a range of stakeholders including workers, neighbours, customers and other supply chain partners.

The presence of trust can bring a range of business benefits including:

  • improved brand reputation and recognition
  • increased customer loyalty
  • increased worker satisfaction and loyalty / better staff retention and recruitment
  • sustainability benefits of waste minimisation, energy efficiency etc
  • socially responsible investment
  • new and better business relationships.

Conversely engaging with CSR superficially, in pursuit of business advantage often has negative consequences. There are many examples of organisations suffering serious reputational damage as a result of tokenism, opportunism or false representation.
