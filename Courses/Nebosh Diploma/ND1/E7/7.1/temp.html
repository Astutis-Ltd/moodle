Acting together:
  • promoting broader ownership of health and safety in Great Britain
  • the regulators will retain their key roles, providing targeted advice and guidance for key audience groups and taking enforcement action when and where necessary, however encouraging others to take ownership of health and safety is a critical component of the scheme.
Tackling ill health:
  • highlighting and tackling the costs of work-related ill health
  • a key element of the theme is earlier prevention, involving a greater focus on health issues at work, while continuing to ensure that maintaining standards around safety remains a priority
  • this will require long-term and coordinated action across all sectors, bringing in additional partners such as the NHS and others.
Managing risk well:
  • simplifying risk management and helping business to grow
  • sensible and proportionate risk management is integral to delivering their business. This approach supports growth, enables innovation and protects an organisation’s most vital asset, its people.
Supporting small employers:
  • giving SMEs simple advice so they know what they have to do
  • managing workplace risks shouldn’t be complicated or costly for SME’s
  • larger business and intermediaries have opportunities to work smarter, developing efficient supply chains and productive relationships by working with and supporting small businesses
  • making support available which is quick and easy for small businesses to understand, so that they can fulfil their obligations.
Keeping pace with change:
  • anticipating and tackling new health and safety challenges
  • Great Britain leads the world in anticipating and tackling the new health and safety challenges that come with social, economic and technological change
  • this ability to horizon scan combined with unrivalled expertise and research facilities means that Great Britain can play a leading role in anticipating new health and safety challenges.
Sharing our success:
  • promoting the benefits of Great Britain’s world-class health and safety system
  • by sharing best practice and the latest thinking and innovation around the world health and safety systems overseas can be influenced making it easier for British businesses to expand into new markets and territories
  • there may also be commercial opportunities for selling British health and safety ‘products’ or consultancy services.
