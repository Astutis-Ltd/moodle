The role of formal health and safety management systems in supply chains

Management systems can be used to establish minimum performance standards required within the supply chain with expectations that suppliers’ systems will dovetail effectively with those of the focal company.

    Examples include:

  • ISO 45001: 2018 - Occupational health and safety management systems. Requirements with guidance for use
  • ISO 14001: 2015 - Environmental management systems. Requirements with guidance for use
  • ISO 9001: 2015 - Quality management systems. Requirements
  • SA 8000 – Social Accountability.

There are also potential benefits for the supplier in that if they are independently audited and third party certificated as meeting an appropriate standard they should not need to be separately audited by each of their customers.

The certification bodies approval also expresses ‘justified confidence’ in the supplier which can be commercially beneficial in marketing their services.
