Adequate resources and equipment


Before work commences it is essential that checks are made on the competence of the personnel arriving on site to do the work and that they have all the equipment and materials necessary to undertake the job safely.

The case of R v Associated Octel 1996 provides a clear illustration of the importance of this stage. In June 1990, while the Associated Octel plant was shut down for annual maintenance, a firm called Resin Glass Products Ltd (RGP) was engaged in repairing the lining of a tank. One of RGPâ€™s employees was cleaning the lining of the tank with acetone, which he had in an old paint bucket. The electric bulb of the inspection lamp he was using broke and ignited the flammable vapour from the acetone in the bucket. This caused a flash fire which badly burned the RGP employee.

The House of Lords held that Associated Octel had breached the requirements of section 3(1) of the Health and Safety at Work Act by not ensuring that RGP and Mr Cuthbert had the appropriate equipment to undertake the job safely, e.g. a lamp suitable for use in a flammable atmosphere, a closed container for the acetone, or forced air extraction for the tank.
