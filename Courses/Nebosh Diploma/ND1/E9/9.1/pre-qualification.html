Pre-qualification schemes

Several contractor safety certification schemes, such as the Contractors Health and Safety Assessment Scheme (CHAS) and the National Access and Scaffolding Confederation (NASC), which via appropriate membership, enable organisations to demonstrate their health and safety management credentials. The purpose of such schemes is to enable buyers (procurers) to ask suppliers to be accredited to a particular scheme and so provide evidence of compliance with basic health and safety law.

Mutual recognition between different accreditation schemes is facilitated by the Safety Systems in Procurement (SSIP) organisation, with SSIP member schemes applying core criteria approved by the HSE (SSIP 2020).

Specific HSE registrations are also required for persons working with, for example, both piped natural gas and liquefied petroleum gas (LPG), asbestos, and with ionising radiation.

The ‘Gas Safe’ Register is the official list of gas businesses who are registered to work safely and legally on gas appliances. All gas engineers must be on the Gas Safe Register.

The Register is not a membership body or trade association.

A business can only be registered if it has at least one gas safety qualified engineer, and a gas engineer can only be aligned to a registered business and be issued with a license to undertake gas work if they hold a valid and current qualification.
