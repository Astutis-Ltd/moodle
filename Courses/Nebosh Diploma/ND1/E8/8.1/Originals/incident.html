Incident investigation v civil claims investigation


An incident investigation aims principally to find out what caused the incident so that the organisation can learn from it and take steps to prevent recurrence. A civil claims investigation on the other had is going to be carried out when the organisation receives a ‘letter of notification’ (which puts the organisation on notice that a formal claim may be started), or if it has received a ‘letter of claim’, or where it is otherwise felt that such a claim may arise at some point in the future. The emphasis in a civil claim investigation would be on preparing for litigation. The report arising out of such an investigation may potentially attract legal privilege.
