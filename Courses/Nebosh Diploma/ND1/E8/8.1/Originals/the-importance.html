The importance of objective investigation


Following any accident at work, there is likely to be an investigation. An investigation can fall into one of two categories:

‣
an investigation that is conducted to find out what went wrong, learn the lessons, and prevent recurrence; or

‣
an investigation conducted with the intention of responding to existing, pending or reasonably contemplated litigation.


It is important that the investigation is carried out as objectively as possible because the investigation report may at some stage be disclosed to a claimant’s legal advisor. This is especially true of the first type of investigation identified above.

The investigation report should not speculate as to blame or legal liability, but should simply focus on the facts. Matters of legal liability are best left to the courts to decide.

It would be unwise to specifically admit liability in an accident investigation report. The author of the report should not rely on the possibility that the investigation report might attract legal professional privilege.
