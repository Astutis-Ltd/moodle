Negative and positive consequences of
admitting liability


The negative consequence of admitting liability in an incident investigation report would be that the organisation might find it harder to defend their legal position.

There may be a positive consequence of admitting liability. In circumstances where it becomes clear that the employer has, on the balance of probabilities, been negligent, it may be pragmatic to admit liability at an early stage and seek to settle any claim. This will likely lead to a lower costs bill and may mean that damages paid could be less than what might have been awarded had the matter gone to court. It would also probably mean that the matter was dealt with more quickly.
