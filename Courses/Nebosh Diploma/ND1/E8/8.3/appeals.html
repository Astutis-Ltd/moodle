Appeals against notices

If an enforcement notice is issued, the safety practitioner will need to brief management on the effects of that notice and also on the grounds for appeal if the organisation believes that the notice was issued unfairly or in error.

Applications to appeal are made to the Employment Tribunal and must be received by the Tribunal office within 21 days of the date on the notice. Grounds for appeal include:

  • the inspector interpreted the law incorrectly
  • the inspector exceeded their powers under HSWA
  • the breach of the law is admitted but the solution is not practicable/reasonably practicable
  • the timescale set for compliance is too short, or
  • the breach was insignificant.

In providing this information, the safety practitioner should be able to indicate which of the grounds for appeal may be relevant, and why.
