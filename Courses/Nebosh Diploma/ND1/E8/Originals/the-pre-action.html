The pre-action protocol


In England and Wales, personal injury and ill health claims are managed in accordance with the relevant ‘Pre-action protocol’, the broad requirements of which were covered in Element 1.5.

The protocol advocates a “Cards on the table” approach, which involves encouraging “the exchange of early and full information about the dispute” (Personal Injury Pre-Action Protocol, para 2.1). Having ready access to the necessary information will help the defendant to comply with their obligations under the protocol. The court may impose sanctions where a party fails to comply with the protocol.

Although the protocol applies only in England and Wales, the need to be able to locate information quickly is universal and will be needed wherever the claim is being brought.

Being able to find relevant documents at an early stage and to quickly pass them on to the insurers and legal advisors gives the defendant more time to properly consider the claim and to decide how to respond. The alternative would be a situation where documentation and evidence that may have been of help has been lost over time and cannot be found; the defendant will be less able to defend the claim, which in a civil case only needs to be proved “on the balance of probabilities”. This may result in a claimant succeeding, which might not have happened had the defendant been able to present clear evidence to show that they did what was reasonable in the circumstances to manage foreseeable risks and to meet the duty of care expected of them.
