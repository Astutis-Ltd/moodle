The role of the safety professional in giving evidence

The main role of the health and safety professional when giving evidence before an inquest is to assist the coroner and the jury in coming to a conclusion. The safety professional should remember that evidence is being given under oath so they must always give honest answers. They should:

  • prepare in advance by reading any relevant documents, including their own witness statement
  • give a full, straightforward and factual account without guessing or speculating
  • explain any technical terms or common health and safety jargon
  • not attempt to give an opinion or answer questions on something that is outside of their area of expertise
  • seek clarification if asked a question that they do not understand
  • correct any incorrect information or proposition with which they do not agree.

The coroner may also ask the safety professional about what has been learnt from the incident. The answer can be helpful when deciding whether a ‘Report to Prevent Future Deaths’ is necessary.
