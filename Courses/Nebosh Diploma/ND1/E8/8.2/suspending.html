Suspending the inquest

The coroner must suspend the investigation or inquest if:

  • a person has been charged with a criminal offence such as manslaughter, or
  • if there is a public inquiry which is set up to investigate the cause of death.

Once the criminal trial or public inquiry is over the coroner can then decide whether to proceed with the inquest. This may not be necessary if all the relevant facts were established at trial or during the inquiry.
