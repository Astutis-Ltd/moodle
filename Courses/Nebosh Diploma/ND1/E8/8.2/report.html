Report to prevent future deaths

The investigation and subsequent inquest hearing may reveal that action could be taken to prevent future deaths. If this is the case, the coroner must write a report bringing this to the attention of an organisation (or person) who may be able to take action, such as the employer or the HSE in the case of a fatal accident at work. This is called a ‘Report to prevent future deaths’.

The recipient of the report is obliged to send to the coroner a written response within 56 days. This response will say what action will be taken.
