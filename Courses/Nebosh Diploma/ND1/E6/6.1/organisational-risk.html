Organisational risk profiling

The risk profile of an organisation informs all aspects of the approach to leading and managing its health and safety risks.

The risk profile is the starting point for determining the greatest health and safety issues facing the organisation. In some businesses the risks will be tangible and immediate safety hazards, whereas in other organisations the risks may be health-related and it may be a long time before the illness becomes apparent.

The range of risks goes beyond health and safety risks to include quality, environmental and asset damage, recognising that issues in one area could impact in another.

A risk profile examines:

  • The nature and level of the threats faced by an organisation
  • The likelihood of adverse effects occurring
  • The level of disruption and costs associated with each type of risk
  • The effectiveness of controls in place to manage those risks.

Health and safety risks range from things that happen very infrequently but with catastrophic effects (high-hazard, low-frequency events, such as an oil refinery explosion) to things that happen much more frequently but with lesser consequences (low-hazard, high-frequency events such as slips and trips).

High-hazard, low-frequency events are high-priority in a risk profile, as the consequences are potentially catastrophic and could compromise the organisationâ€™s ability to function or survive.

The outcome of risk profiling will be that the significant risks have been identified and prioritised for action, and minor risks will not have been given too much priority. It also informs decisions about what risk control measures are needed.

Risk profiling provides important management information at board or senior management level to determine organisational priorities, appropriate allocation of resources, etc. At this level health and safety information is considered holistically with other organisational / enterprise risks.

Note the distinction between organisational risk profiling providing high level management information for strategic planning, and operational risk management dealing with local problems to ensure the effective management of risks to health and safety, so far as is reasonably practicable.
