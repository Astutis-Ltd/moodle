The role of the health and safety professional

It is impossible to come up with a one size fits all definition or job description to explain what a health and safety professional is or does, as this will clearly vary with organisational complexity, the hazard burden and risk profile of the organisation and the individuals position in the management structure.

What is clear though is that health and safety is everyone’s responsibility and should be managed through general line management arrangements.

The IOSH competence framework suggests that the health and safety professional’s role is to apply their technical, behavioural and management abilities to work collaboratively with stakeholders to build an organisational structure, plan activities, assign responsibilities, develop safe working practices and implement management procedures that will help keep colleagues and others healthy and safe at work.

This will involve advising, coaching, mentoring and supporting colleagues from the leadership team, line management and operational workforce as well as monitoring, reviewing and feeding back on the work that is being done.

As a manager in an organisation the health and safety professional may well also have responsibilities to protect their employer from litigation and reputational damage etc which together with pressures to not slow down production or service delivery can lead to professional ethical conflict.
