Continuing professional development


Notions of learning through reflection on past experience as an aid to personal continuous improvement are now common in many professions, notably those in education and medicine.

Continuing professional development (CPD) is the term that describes a commitment to structured skills enhancement and professional competence. Commitment to CPD also acknowledges that becoming qualified is merely the beginning of the professional journey and that lifelong learning is essential to maintain professional standards.

CPD provides a framework for keeping up to date in a rapidly evolving professional landscape and addressing the challenges and opportunities of global competition, technological advances and increasingly demanding clients. The ability to recognise and adapt to the changing landscape faster than the competition provides a source of sustainable competitive advantage.

The IOSH CPD scheme provides a planned and structured approach for identifying technical and personal skill gaps, and creating an action plan to refresh or expand knowledge and enhance professional effectiveness. It is intended to enhance professional effectiveness, create a structured career path and safeguard professional status.

Keeping up to date and recording the development activities is a requirement of the IOSH membership structure. Becoming a chartered safety and health practitioner, graduate member or technician member enhances professional status and comes with the responsibility of demonstrating continuing professional development.
