Professional ethics


Ethics may be considered to be the science, or systematic analysis, of morality where morality implies the codes of conduct or rules of behaviour imposed by a society regarding what is right and wrong.

Professional bodies, such as IOSH, issue codes of conduct to frame the expected standard of professional practice with regard to technical competence, as previously discussed, and professional conduct.

IOSH’s code of conduct is a public document that has at its heart the IOSH vision of ‘a world of work which is safe, healthy and sustainable’.

It is divided into three parts:

‣
Part 1 – Code of conduct

‣
Part 2 – Code of conduct – guidance

‣
Part 3 – IOSH disciplinary procedure.


It is intended to:

‣
provide a strong ethical foundation for all members working towards the IOSH vision

‣
help members demonstrate their value and reliability through adhering to high standards.


The guidance is designed to support members’ efforts to reach and maintain the standards, which are expressed as four core values of integrity, competence, respect and service (see the below table).

The standards are based on public expectations of professionalism.

Members owe a primary loyalty to those at risk and should seek to ensure professional independence in the execution of their duties.

Members must also avoid departing from the standards of integrity, competence and respect in their private lives in any way that could undermine public trust and confidence in the profession.

1) Integrity

Members are required to:

1)
Be honest

2)
Abide by the law

3)
Notify the Institution in writing promptly if they have been:

‣
convicted of a criminal offence by a Court, or

‣
charged with a criminal offence contrary to health and safety legislation, or

‣
adjudged bankrupt or have entered into a formal arrangement with their creditors.

4)
Avoid conflicts of interest wherever possible and if one arises promptly take appropriate steps to manage it.

3) Respect

Members are required to:

1)
Cooperate fully with IOSH and abide by its Charter, Byelaws and Regulations

2)
Respect the rights and privacy of other people and organisations

3)
Cooperate with employers or clients in fulfilling their legal duties under the terms of their employment or consultancy contracts

4)
Have due regard for the effect their professional activities may have on others

5)
Ensure that their professional and business activities are reasonable

6)
Respond promptly and appropriately to disputes and complaints.

2) Competence

Members are required to:

1)
Ensure they are competent to undertake proposed work

2)
Ensure persons working under their authority or supervision are competent to carry out the tasks assigned to them

3)
Undertake appropriate continuing professional development and record it in the manner prescribed by the Institution

4)
Ensure that they make clients, employers and others who may be affected by their activities aware of their levels of competence.

4) Service

Members are required to:

1)
Ensure that the terms of appointment and scope of work are clearly recorded in writing

2)
Ensure that, where necessary, they have adequate professional indemnity insurance when providing consultancy services

3)
Carry out their professional work in a timely manner

4)
Ensure that professional advice is accurate, proportionate and communicated in an appropriate format

5)
Have due regard for levels of service and customer care reasonably expected of them

6)
Inform any person overruling or neglecting their professional advice of the potential adverse consequences and keep a written record of the date, time and nature of this action.
