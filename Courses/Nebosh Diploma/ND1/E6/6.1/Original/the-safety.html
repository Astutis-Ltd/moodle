The safety practitioner as leader and manager


The safety practitioner will have a role to play as both manager in the organisation and leader for health and safety.

As manager the safety practitioner will have clearly defined role and responsibilities within the safety management system and can be held accountable if these responsibilities are not properly fulfilled.

The management responsibilities in the context of the safety management system are discussed in the next section.

From a leadership perspective the safety practitioner may well be the champion within the organisation, providing a vision, direction and strategy, and seeking to motivate and inspire others from board level down to operational staff.

As has been discussed some people may have natural traits, aptitudes and charisma that predisposes them for a leadership role. Whether this is the case or not leadership can be understood and taught as a process, and regardless of preferences for particular theories at a personal or organisational level, ways can be found to lead initiatives successfully.

As discussed earlier in this section reflective practice and personal continual improvement are required in any modern professional role. It is important after every initiative to take the time to reflect on successes and failures and to take the opportunity to learn, improve and modify approaches ahead of next time.
