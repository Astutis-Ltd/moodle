Conflict of interest


Conflict of interest refers to occasions where using professional judgment may lead to problems in securing future or continuing employment or contracts.

A simple example of this type of conflict might be where a consultant is aware that his recommendations are not being implemented but continues to advise the organisation so as not to lose the income from the contract.

The IOSH code of conduct requires members to seek to avoid their professional judgment being influenced by any conflict of interest and to inform their employer, client, or the Institution of any conflict between personal interest and service to the relevant party.
