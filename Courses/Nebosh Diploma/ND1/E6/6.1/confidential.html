Confidential information and data protection

The Data Protection Act 2018 implements the EU’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in the UK.

Everyone responsible for using personal data (i.e. information that relates to an identified or identifiable natural person) has to follow strict rules called ‘data protection principles’ and must make sure the information is:

  • used fairly, lawfully and transparently
  • used for specified, explicit purposes
  • used in a way that is adequate, relevant and limited to only what is necessary
  • accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date
  • kept for no longer than is necessary
  • handled in a way that ensures appropriate security, including protection against unlawful or unauthorised processing, access, loss, destruction or damage.

There is stronger legal protection for more sensitive information about race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, political affiliations, religious belief and genetic and biometric information.

In health and safety management information on trade union membership and health surveillance information falls into the ‘sensitive information’ category.

Personal data in incident investigation reports should be removed or restricted before any report is circulated within the organisation or shared with an insurer.

There is clear “lawful basis” for managing personal data within a health and safety management system, notably legal obligations to keep training records, health surveillance records etc, and a legitimate interest in properly ensuring the health and safety of those employed by the organisation.

The data protection principles must be followed to ensure that the information is stored securely, used appropriately and kept for no longer than necessary.
