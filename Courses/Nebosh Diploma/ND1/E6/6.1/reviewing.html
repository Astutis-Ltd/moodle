Reviewing and developing personal practice

Professional practice can be reviewed in a number of ways including:

  • setting personal objectives and targets, and measuring progress against them
  • reviewing failures or unsuccessful attempts to produce change
  • benchmarking practice against that of other practitioners
  • seeking feedback from clients and colleagues.

Professional practice can be developed by:

  • augmenting core knowledge and competence
  • obtaining a recognised professional qualification
  • keeping current by participating in CPD schemes, subscribing to suitable information sources and networking with peers.
