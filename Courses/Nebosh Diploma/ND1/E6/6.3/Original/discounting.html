Discounting


Discounting allows a comparison between costs and benefits arising in different time periods. In general, the costs of implementing a risk reduction measure will comprise an initial capital outlay and occasional later payments for maintenance and replacement. The benefits will recur year upon year. It is conventional practice in a CBA to:

a)
choose an appraisal period (e.g. 10 years)

b)
discount the values of all costs and benefits arising each year to the first year of the appraisal period, before comparing them.


Discounting adds more weight to earlier costs and benefits than later ones by applying a discount rate. People prefer to receive benefits sooner rather than later and prefer to incur costs later rather than sooner.
