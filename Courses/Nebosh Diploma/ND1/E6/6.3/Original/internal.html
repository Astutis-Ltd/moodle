Internal and external sources of information
to be considered when determining costs


Finding accurate financial information on costs and benefits for a CBA requires access to a range of external and internal sources of information.

As previously mentioned the values attributed to fatalities, other injuries and occupational illnesses (appraisal values or unit costs) can be found on the HSE’s Economics of health and safety webpages.

Other external sources of information include:

‣
Insurance companies – information on claims history, premiums, excesses, etc.

‣
Manufacturers and suppliers – for costs of plant, equipment, components, PPE, etc.

‣
Maintenance contractors – for costs of servicing and maintenance

‣
Designers (e.g. architects and quantity surveyors) – for costs of new structures, structural maintenance or components.


Internal sources of information include:

‣
HR department – recruitment costs, salary costs, staff development (training) costs, agency workers costs, etc.

‣
Finance department – previous budgets and published accounts for information on sales and overheads, etc.

‣
Production department – projected impacts on production

‣
Maintenance department – internal costs for servicing and maintenance.
