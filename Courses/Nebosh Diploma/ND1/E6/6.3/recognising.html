Recognising responsible budget holders

Health and safety may be considered a ‘cost of doing business’ as the employing organisation has to comply with the law and keep employees and others safe, in which case the budget for providing any necessary equipment, PPE and training should be held by the local department or cost centre.

Larger organisations may well have a dedicated ‘health and safety’ budget but this is likely to be for the salary and overhead costs for the safety practioner(s), administration of the safety management system, maintenance of accreditations, certifications, etc. There may also be a contingency fund earmarked for dealing with the consequences of a significant accident.

Many organisations may use a ‘business partner’ model for support services ancillary to production or service delivery (HR, IT, H&S, etc.) with individual departments paying an internal levy to cover the cost of the service. This is based on the generally accepted premise that health and safety management is an operational line management function with the health and safety practitioner providing a professional advice and support service.

The budget for maintenance of the structural fabric of the workplace, utilities, telecommunications infrastructure, grounds maintenance, catering and security services, etc, may well be held by a facilities management department, or contracts manager if contracted out.

Budget holders need to be clear on their responsibilities and accountabilities as both line managers and budget holders.
