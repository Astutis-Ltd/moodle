Influencing ownership of health and safety at all organisational levels

In an organisation with a positive health and safety culture the importance of good health and safety management will be understood and valued throughout the organisation.

At board level the MD / CEO or an appointed champion must be seen to be driving the agenda and leading from the front. Other board members should also be seen to have ‘bought in’ to health and safety and to present a united front.

The safety practitioner may have a significant role in presenting the professional evidence to persuade the leadership team of the importance of the issue.

Line managers may also need to be convinced to play their part effectively and the safety practitioner will have a role to play in peer to peer persuasion and negotiation and in providing technical support to enable line managers to take responsibility locally.

Operational staff also need to be engaged and again the safety practitioner will have a key role to play in devising, implementing and supporting means of providing information and consulting with employees directly, or with their representatives, to enable workforce participation in operational health and safety management.
