The benefits of good consultation


ACAS considers employee consultation to be about involving and developing people in an organisation. For an organisation to be effective it must achieve its goals and this can only be done by individuals and groups within the organisation cooperating and working together in a coordinated way.

Good consultation and communication are essential in encouraging employee cooperation.

Whatever the size of the organisation, and regardless of whether or not it is trade unionised, employees will only be able to perform at their best if they:

‣
know their duties, obligations and rights

‣
have an opportunity of making their views known to management on the issues that affect them.


With current management trends favouring flatter structures and the devolvement of responsibilities to individuals, it is increasingly important that employees have a real understanding not only of what they are required to do but why.

Effective consultation requires resources including time and money but there can be many benefits that can clearly outweigh the costs. The benefits to the organisation may include:

‣
Improved organisational performance. Time spent consulting at the outset of a new project or development can minimise subsequent rumour and misunderstanding

‣
Improved management performance and decision-making. Employee input can help managers arrive at sound decisions which are more readily accepted by employees as a whole

‣
Greater levels of trust and improved management / employee relations as a consequence of giving employees the opportunity to express their views and discuss issues of common interest

‣
Improved employee engagement. ‘Positive discretionary behaviour’ (i.e. employees going the ‘extra mile’) is much more likely if employees feel valued and involved in decision-making

‣
Improved employee commitment and performance. Commitment is likely to be enhanced (together with trust and engagement) if employees know what the organisation is trying to achieve and how they, as individuals, can influence decisions. Performance is also likely to improve if employees are given regular, accurate information about their jobs including information on targets, deadlines and feedback

‣
Increased job satisfaction and better employee motivation are also likely if performance levels are high and the employees are engaged and trust management.
