Ensuring roles and responsibilities are clear,
understood and implemented


ISO 45001 specifies that roles, responsibilities and accountabilities should be defined and allocated, to facilitate effective health and safety management, and that the details should be documented and communicated.

HSG65 recommends clearly defined performance standards for workers at all levels associated with rewards for good performance and sanctions for unacceptably poor performance as an important aspect of ‘organising for health and safety’.

Good performance standards link individual responsibilities to specific organisational outputs and specify how individuals will be held accountable for their responsibilities, including those for health and safety.

Performance standards should clearly specify:

‣
Who is responsible for what

‣
When the work should be completed by

‣
The standard of performance required.


Everyone in the organisation, from the board of directors through line management, to operational staff, and staff with specific health and safety responsibilities (including the safety practitioner), should be clear on their roles and responsibilities.

The safety practitioner may have a role to play in:

‣
ensuring that the information is easy to understand and readily available

‣
monitoring and auditing the ongoing effectiveness of the system to check that staff at all levels are not only aware but are acting accordingly.
