Consultation and negotiation when developing
an organisation’s health and safety objectives


The organisation’s headline priorities and objectives for health and safety are determined through risk profiling.

Once the headline objectives are understood these will need to be cascaded down through the organisation leading to objectives at the divisional, departmental, team and individual levels.

‣
Persuasion – presenting a logical argument to convince others of the need to act

‣
Negotiation – listening to the needs of the other party and coming to a mutually satisfactory resolution.

Each operational area of the organisation will have different hazard burdens and different operational health and safety challenges to address and different perceived priorities which can lead to competition for priority and the corresponding budget and resources.

Consultation with all stakeholders and the open sharing of information and views will play an important part in the process of determining the organisations health and safety objectives.

Influencing skills (i.e. persuasion and negotiation) will also be important in overcoming any conflict between the stakeholders.

The logical presentation of the organisation’s needs, based on sensible health and safety risk management and a holistic view of the organisational impact of alternative action plans, should help to persuade the stakeholders of the need to act for the greater good.

The art of effective negotiation is to engineer a situation where both parties believe they have gained something from the process (the ‘win-win’) without having suffered unacceptable losses to do so. It is important therefore to enter a negotiation with a clear objective (understanding of what the ‘win’ looks like) together with a view of what can be comfortably traded (acceptable losses / compromises) to achieve it.
