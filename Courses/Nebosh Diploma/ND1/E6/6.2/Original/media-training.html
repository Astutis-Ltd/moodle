Media training for top management


Following a major accident, it is imperative that the organisation and its leadership takes responsibility and provides accurate information to interested stakeholders in a timely manner.

If this does not happen the resulting ‘information vacuum’ will be filled by the media from other sources and those directly and indirectly affected by the event start to consider the media as the primary source of information.

Arrangements should be in place for requests for information to be managed through a central communications team which will be responsible for issuing press releases and several members of the senior leadership team including the CEO/MD should be trained in how to deal with journalist interviews for television, radio or newsprint / online.

There is a difficult balance to be struck between taking responsibility, focussing on the human costs and the needs of affected families etc whilst looking to preserve the organisations reputation and looking to manage legal consequences.
