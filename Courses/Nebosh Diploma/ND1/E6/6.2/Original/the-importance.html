The importance of receiving and acting
on feedback


A feedback loop is essential in a self-regulating system, such as an organisation, because evaluating output (performance) against expectation provides management information that can be used to refine processes and adjust required resources (inputs) to drive continual improvement.

The influence of the feedback loop on individual behaviour has been shown to be critical in encouraging repetition of positive behaviour and discouraging negative behaviours.

Many components of the health and safety management system will rely upon the contribution of engaged workers, e.g. hazard spotting, risk assessments, incident reporting, accident investigation and workplace inspections. It is vitally important that contributors receive early regular feedback on their contributions, to know that their efforts are worthwhile and appreciated and to sustain both the individual efforts and the functioning of the systems.

It is also important that the safety practitioner receives feedback on their personal contribution through performance reviews and 360-degree appraisals, etc. This information should be used to drive personal continual improvement / Continual Professional Development (CPD).
