Crown Court


The Crown Court hears indictable offences and those cases (usually of a more serious nature) that the prosecution or defence have elected to have tried before the Crown Court.

Cases in the Crown Court are heard before a judge and jury. The judgeâ€™s role is to ensure that the trial is conducted fairly, and to sum up for the jury on points of law. The jury is responsible for returning a verdict depending on the facts.

An appeal against conviction and / or sentence will go to the Court of Appeal.
