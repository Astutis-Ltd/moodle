Bringing a civil claim

To bring a civil claim it is necessary for the claimant to prove that they have suffered a loss or injury as a result of another person’s negligence or breach of statutory duty.

Most civil disputes do not end up in court, and those that do often don’t go to a full trial. Where a case does go through the courts, the aim is to make it as simple as possible.

The Small Claims Court provides a relatively quick and cheap way of resolving a legal dispute claims for compensation for injuries or illness caused by work are mainly dealt with in the County Court although more substantial or complex cases will be heard in the High Court.

To commence an action, a claimant must file the following documents with the court:

  • a Claim Form
  • a Statement of Claim
  • a Schedule of Special Damage if applicable
  • the Court fee
  • any additional documents required by the Court rules. e.g in a personal injury case it is necessary to file a medical report.

Once the proceedings have been ‘issued’, the Court will send a sealed copy of the documents to the Defendant to include:

  1. a copy of the Claim Form plus a copy of the Statement and Special Damages.
  2. a form for acknowledging service of the claim, and for admitting or defending the action.

If the value of the Claim is between £10 000 and £25 000 the matter will usually be allocated to the fast track. This track is appropriate where:

  • the documentation to prove the case is not extensive
  • where expert evidence can be given in writing
  • where the time to prepare for trial does not exceed 30 weeks
  • where the trial is not likely to last for more than one day.

Approximately four weeks after the date of track allocation standard directions are issued. The directions will typically be as follows:

  • exchange of witness statements (10 weeks)
  • exchange of experts reports (14 weeks)
  • court to send listing questionnaires to confirm all directions complied with (20 weeks)
  • trial (30 weeks).
Claims

Claims will generally fall into three categories:

  • Small claims, with a financial value of up to £10 000, or personal injury claims up to £1 000
  • Fast-track claims, which have a value of between £10 001 and £25 000, the court will set a strict timetable for the conduct of such cases, and generally they should not last for more than one day in court
  • Multi-track claims, where the value of the claim exceeds £25 000, the court takes an active role arranging case management conferences and laying down strict timetables.
