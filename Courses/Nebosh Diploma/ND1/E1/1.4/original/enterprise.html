Enterprise and Regulatory Reform Act 2013


The Lofstedt Report 2011 identified potential unfairness where health and safety at work regulations impose a strict liability on employers, making them legally responsible to pay compensation despite having done all that was reasonable to protect their employees, and recommended that regulatory provisions which impose strict liability should be reviewed.

In its response to the report the Government has introduced the Enterprise and Regulatory Reform Act 2013 (ERRA). Section 69 (Civil liability for breach of health and safety duties) of the ERRA amends Section 47(2) of the Health and Safety at Work Act 1974 removing the right to sue using the tort of breach of statutory duty (unless the health and safety provision in question specifically provides for a civil right of action).
