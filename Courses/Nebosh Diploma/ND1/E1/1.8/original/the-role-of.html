The role of claims handlers and loss adjusters


Claims handler

Traditionally, a Claim Handler handles the processes of a claim. They can normally agree to payouts within a very limited set of internal rules and have to refer anything unusual to a colleague with a greater level of authority, such as a loss adjuster.


Loss adjuster

A "Loss Adjuster“ is generally empowered to “adjust the loss” which means they act with full authority of the insurer to negotiate and settle a claim.

Loss adjusters are independent experts who are usually employed by insurance companies to resolve complex claims, although they may just provide guidance and recommendations to the insurers.

The loss adjusters role typically involves:

1)
verifying whether or not the claimants policy covers the loss or damage by reviewing the policy and policy schedule

2)
verifying the amount, if any, that the policy should pay out.


Where the loss adjuster is acting on behalf of an insurer there will usually be strict service level agreements to help ensure that the claim is dealt with efficiently and fairly.

Chartered Loss Adjusters are experienced claims professionals who have achieved the highest qualification in claims handling. They are members of the Chartered Institute of Loss Adjusters (CILA) and are therefore bound to behave in accordance with the CILA Guide to Professional Conduct.


Loss Assessors

The term Loss Assessor is often used to describe an independent insurance claim professional contracted by the policy holder to protect their interests through the claims process.
