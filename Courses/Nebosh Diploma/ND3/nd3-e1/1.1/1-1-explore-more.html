Safe working environment legislation

The Health and Safety at Work Act 1974

The employer’s overarching duty under section 2(1) of the Health and Safety at Work Act 1974 is “to ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, the health, safety and welfare at work of all his employees”. This includes, under section 2(2)(d), ensuring, so far as is reasonably practicable, that "any place of work under the employer’s control, is maintained in a condition that is safe and without risks to health and that safe means of access and egress are provided and maintained in a safe condition".

The Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992

The Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992 expand on the general duty and include specific requirements intended to protect the health and safety of everyone in the workplace, and to ensure that adequate welfare facilities are provided for people at work.

They set general requirements in four broad areas:

  • Working environment including: temperature, ventilation, lighting including emergency lighting, room dimensions, suitability of workstations and seating, and outdoor workstations (for example, weather protection)
  • Safety including: safe passage of pedestrians and vehicles, windows and skylights (safe opening, closing and cleaning), glazed doors and partitions (use of safe material and marking), doors, gates and escalators (safety devices), floors (their construction, and obstructions and slipping and tripping hazards), falls from heights and into dangerous substances, and falling objects
  • Facilities including: toilets, washing, eating and changing facilities, clothing storage, seating, rest areas, and rest facilities for pregnant women and nursing mothers
  • Housekeeping including: maintenance of workplace, equipment and facilities, cleanliness, and removal of waste materials.
