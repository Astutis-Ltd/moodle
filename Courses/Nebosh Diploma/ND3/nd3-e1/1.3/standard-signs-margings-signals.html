Standard signs, markings and signals

Safety signs need to contain a pictogram to convey the message, instead of relying solely on text. The standard signs are presented in the table below.

Prohibition (red) Prohibits behaviour likely to increase or cause danger (for example, no access for unauthorised persons or no smoking).
Warning (yellow) Giving warning of a hazard or danger (for example, danger, electricity).
Mandatory (blue) Must be done, prescribing specific behaviour (for example, eye protection must be worn).
Safe condition (green) Information on emergency exits, first-aid, or rescue equipment and activities.
Fire-fighting equipment (red) Information on the identification or location of fire-fighting equipment.

As well as the traditional signboards, the Regulations also apply to other types of safety sign and signals, including signs to mark obstacles, dangerous locations and traffic routes, illuminated signs, acoustic signals, spoken communication, and hand signals.
