Footwear

‘Sensible footwear’ policies, specifying flat shoes to maximise sole / floor contact and friction, have been shown to be effective in reducing the numbers of slip incidents.

The sole tread pattern and sole compound are both important for slip resistance. Generally a softer sole and close-packed tread pattern work well with fluid contaminants and indoor environments. A more open pattern works better outdoors or with solid contaminants.

The safety features of footwear, including slip resistance, are tested according to a set of European test standards written into BS EN ISO 20345:2011 – Personal protective equipment. Safety footwear.

Footwear which has passed the EN test for slip resistance is marked as:

  • SRA – tested on ceramic tile wetted with dilute soap solution
  • SRB – tested on smooth steel with glycerol
  • SRC – SRA + SRB.

Footwear should be trialled in each particular working environment.

Suitable floor materials

Flooring should be specified to ensure that it has sufficient slip resistance to cope with its intended use (notes on slip resistance follow).

The junctions between floor surfaces of different slip resistance should be visually obvious. A pedestrian going from a high to low friction surface is more likely to slip if they do not adjust their gait accordingly.

Horizontal floors are inherently less slippery than sloping floors with the same surface.

In addition to its dry slip resistance other parameters to consider in specifying a suitable floor material include:

  1. behaviour when wet or contaminated
  2. comfort under foot
  3. durability
  4. ease of cleaning. Depending on the levels of liquid, or other contamination, expected the floor finish

Depending on the levels of liquid, or other contamination, expected the floor finish may need to be ‘macro-rough’, i.e. exhibit visible, moulded patterns or profiles within the finish, or ‘micro rough’, which although seemingly flat will incorporate microscopic particles or patterns to increase friction and improve slip resistance.
