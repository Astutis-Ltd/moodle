Safe movement of people

The Workplace (Health Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992 require employers to protect employees and others from a range of hazards as they move around the workplace. The hazards and the relevant requirements are summarised in the table below.

Slips, trips and falls on the same level Regulation 12 requires that floor and traffic routes should be of sound construction, adequate strength and stability. They should be free from any hole, slope, or uneven or slippery surface which is likely to cause a person to slip, trip or fall, or drop anything being lifted or carried.
Falls from height Regulation 13 requires the provision of secure fencing to prevent people falling from height. Tanks, pits or similar structures may be securely covered rather than fenced.

(Working at height is discussed in more detail in Element 11).
Struck by objects falling from height Fencing provided to prevent people falling from height should also prevent objects falling onto people.
Struck by moving vehicle Regulation 17 requires that the workplace be organised to allow pedestrians and vehicles to circulate in a safe manner. (Safe movement of vehicles is discussed in detail in Element 12).
Striking against stationary objects Workrooms should have enough free space to allow people to get to and from workstations and to move within the room, with ease. (Regulations 10 and 11)

Glazed door panels, windows and partitions should be made from safety materials, and should be conspicuously marked if necessary to prevent people walking into them. (Regulation 14)

Other requirements of the regulations regarding adequate lighting (discussed later), cleanliness and storage of waste also help to ensure the safe movement of people.
