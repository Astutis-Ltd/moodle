Striking against stationary objects

Space

Workrooms should have enough free space to allow people to get to and from workstations, and to move safely and comfortably within the room.

A minimum of 11 cubic metres of space should be allowed for each worker in a work room. In a typical room, where the ceiling is 2.4m high this equates to a floor area of 4.6m2 per person.

The legal requirement in the Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulation (Regulation 10) is for ‘sufficient unoccupied space’ so these figures may be inadequate if much of the space is filled with furniture or other fixtures and fittings.

Glazed panels

Transparent or translucent surfaces in doors, gates, walls and partitions should be:

  • a safety material, which is an inherently robust material, material which breaks safely, or annealed glass of suitable thickness for the surface areant
  • adequately protected against breakage with a screen or barrier.

Particular attention should be paid to:

  • doors and gates (including side panels), where the glazing is at shoulder level or below
  • windows, walls and partitions, where the glazing is at waist level or below.

Transparent / translucent surfaces should also be conspicuously marked, at a suitable height, to prevent people accidentally walking into them.
