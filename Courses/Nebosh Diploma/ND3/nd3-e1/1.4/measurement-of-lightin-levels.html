Measurement of lighting levels

Field measurements of illuminance may be required for the following reasons:

  • to establish whether a new installation has achieved the design specification;
  • to establish whether an installation meets a desired criterion; or
  • X for trouble-shooting in identifying the causes of complaints about the lighting.

Light meters (or photometers) can be used to measure a range of lighting characteristics. A specific type of photometer called a lux meter is used to measure illuminance in the workplace.

A typical lux meter consists of a selenium or silicon photovoltaic cell connected directly or indirectly by an amplifier to an analogue or digital display. The quality of the lux meter is determined by four factors:

  • its spectral response;
  • its response to light incident on the photocell at different angles;
  • its linearity of response; and
  • its sensitivity to temperature.
