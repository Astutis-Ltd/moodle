High voltage power transmission

As mentioned in Element 8.1, high voltage is generally considered to be greater than 1000V (1kV) AC or 1500V DC. Typical exposure to high voltages will be via underground and overhead cables used for distributions and at sub stations (not on syllabus).

The National Grid (National Grid Company plc) owns and maintains the high-voltage electricity transmission system in England and Wales, together with operating the system across Great Britain. Transmission involves the bulk transport of high voltage (400kV or 275kV) electricity from the generators to the Distribution Network Operator (DNO) companies for distribution and supply.

The DNOs are responsible for the assets transporting electricity from the national grid supply to the consumer. Transport assets include overhead lines, underground cables, switches and transformers with voltages from 132kV to 230V.

Transformers are used to step down from 132kV to 33kV, then from 33kV to 11kV. A substation will then reduce the voltage to 400V (three phase) and 230V.
