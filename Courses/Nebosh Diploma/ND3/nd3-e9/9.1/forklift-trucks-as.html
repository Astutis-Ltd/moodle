Fork lift trucks as working platforms

People should never be lifted on the fork arms of a lift truck, or a pallet balanced on the fork arms of a lift truck because they can easily fall off.

However, when fitted with a suitably designed working platform they can provide a safer alternative to other means of access (such as a ladder), although it will not provide the same level of safety as purpose-built equipment, such as a Mobile Elevated Work Platform (MEWP) (see Section 7.3).

It is generally accepted that an integrated working platform provides a higher level of safety than a non-integrated type.

An integrated working platform has controls that are linked to and isolate the truck controls, so that only a person in the platform can control the lift height of the platform and truck movements.

A non-integrated working platform has no controls in the platform. All truck and working platform movements are controlled by the truck operator.

A permissioning control working platforms is a non-integrated working platform with hold-to-run controls that link to and nullify the truck lift / lower and traction controls, until the controls in the platform are actuated by a person in the platform.

Note: If used for lifting people the fork lift truck will need to be thoroughly examined by a competent person at least once every six months or in accordance with an examination scheme, and each time that exceptional circumstances which are liable to jeopardise the safety of the lifting equipment have occurred.

The Work at Height Regulations 2005, Regulation 7(2)(b) places a duty on employers to select the most suitable work equipment for the task to be carried out, regardless of the duration of the task.

In the UK in exceptional circumstances relating to short duration, occasional use (such as unplanned, reactive inspections of racking or overhead light fittings) the use of non-integrated working platforms with fork lift trucks is allowed, in accordance with guidance note PM28.

This may not be allowed in some other EU member states. Consequently there is NO free movement of non-integrated working platforms throughout the EU and they MUST NOT be CE marked.
