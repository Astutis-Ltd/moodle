Fork lift truck attachments

Fitting an attachment may alter the characteristics of the lift truck and is likely to necessitate a reduction in the actual capacity of the lift truck.

Attachments may be mounted on the fork arms, or directly onto the carriage. In all cases the attachment should be securely fastened, and care taken to ensure that the attachment or securing device does not foul any part of the mast structure during raising or lowering of the attachment.

The main types of attachment are shown in the image and described in the table below.

Fork arm extensions

Hydraulically operated telescopic fork arms may replace the fixed fork arms.

Simple box sections may be slipped onto the fork arms and secured in place.

Extensions may be used to reach loads in deep racking or to handle extra depth loads.
Side shift carriage

May be mounted either on the existing fork carriage or in place of it.

Enables horizontal sideways movement of the fork arms to allow precise positioning.
Fork positioner

A hydraulically operated attachment which allows the operator, while remaining in the operating position, to change the position of the fork arms relative to each other.

The fork arm centres can be adjusted to accommodate different load widths.
Rotator

Mounted on the fork carriage and usually used in conjunction with another attachment.

Allows the load to be rotated vertically about an axis parallel to the longitudinal axis of the lift truck.

Some rotators have the facility to tilt the load forward from the vertical to the horizontal and beyond, and are usually known as ‘tipplers’.
Clamps

Designed for a variety of purposes such as lifting reels, bales or cartons.

May be used in conjunction with a rotator.

May be faced with rubber or other material to improve grip.
Crane jib

May be mounted directly on the fork carriage or carried on the fork arms.

May be of a fixed length or extendible, or embody a number of lifting points.

On some it is possible to vary the angle of the jib from the horizontal.
Hydraulically operated hoppers

Usually carried on the fork arms.

Fitted with a mechanism which, when operated, causes the hopper to roll forward and discharge its contents. The hopper returns to its normal position and re-engages the holding mechanism once empty.
Booms

Usually consist of a circular section pole mounted on the fork carriage.

Designed for lifting rolls of carpet, coils of wire or similar cylindrical loads, they come in a variety of diameters and lengths.
