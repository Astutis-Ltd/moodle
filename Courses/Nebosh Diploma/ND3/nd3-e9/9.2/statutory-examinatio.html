Statutory examination of lifting equipment

Lifting equipment requires a thorough examination by a competent person:

  • before it is put into service for the first time
  • after installation or reassembly
  • during its exposure to conditions which cause deterioration.

Lifting equipment which is exposed to conditions causing deterioration likely to result in dangerous situations should be thoroughly examined as follows:

Lifting accessories and equipment for lifting persons at least every 6 months.
All other lifting equipment

at least every 12 months

or

in accordance with an examination scheme

or

after exceptional circumstances which are liable to jeopardise the safety of the lifting equipment.

Note: It may also be appropriate for the lifting equipment to be inspected by a competent person at suitable intervals between thorough examinations, to ensure the equipment is maintained in good order and that any deterioration is detected and remedied in good time.

The competent person is required to immediately notify the employer and enforcing authority of any identified defects involving existing or imminent risks of serious personal injury, for example:

  • amputation
  • electric shock
  • fracture other than to fingers, thumbs or toes.

The employer should then remove any such defective equipment from use for disposal or repair.

The competent person also has to provide the employer with a written report of the findings of the thorough examination, as soon as is practicable.

The employer has to keep available for inspection any EU declaration of conformity relating to the lifting equipment for as long as he operates it, and to keep the records of thorough examinations available for inspection as in the table below.

Examination before first use
– lifting equipment 		until employer ceases to use.
Examination before first use
– moveable lifting equipment 		until it ceases to be used at place of installation or assembly.
Examination before first use
– lifting accessories 		two years after report is made.
Periodic examinations until the next report or two years – whichever is later.
