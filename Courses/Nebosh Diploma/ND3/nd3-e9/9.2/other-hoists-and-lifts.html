Other hoists and lifts

Gin wheels

Gin wheels are often used on construction sites for lifting materials from the ground to an upper level. To ensure safe use the following steps should be taken:

  • Scaffolders should be advised in advance if lifting tackle is to be attached to the scaffold
  • Poles and hooks must be strong enough to take the load
  • The gin wheel must not be suspended more than 750mm beyond the outer support
  • Ropes must be to the relevant BS EN standard and be marked with a SWL
  • They must be tested and examined every six months.

Scaffold hoist towers

Scaffold hoists are used to carry construction materials to high working levels on a construction site scaffold. To be suitable for use the scaffold hoist should be:

  • Erected by a competent person
  • Subject to periodic statutory examinations
  • Maintained in good working order
  • Able to cope with site conditions
  • Of adequate capacity to cope with the size and weight of the loads to be moved
  • Clearly marked with the safe working load and its suitability for carrying persons (persons must not be transported in lifts designed for goods only)
  • Able to be operated from one position, from where the operator has a clear view of all the landing levels.

The main hazards and precautions are summarised in the table below.

Hazard Precautions
People being struck by the platform or other moving parts

Enclose the hoistway where people might be struck (working platforms or window openings, for example).

Provide gates at all landings and at ground level.
People falling down the hoistway

Fence the hoistway where people could fall down it.

Keep landing gates closed except during loading and unloading.

Ensure gates are secure and not free to swing into the hoistway.

Ensure the edge of the hoist platform is close to the edge of the landing, so that there is no gap to fall through.
Falling materials

Use a hoist with an enclosed platform.

Put loose loads in suitable containers.

Do not carry loose loads such as bricks.

Do not overfill wheelbarrows and chock wheelbarrows on landings.
