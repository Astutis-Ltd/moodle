Overhead cranes

Overhead cranes are common in engineering works and plants where lifting access for conventional cranes is difficult, due to the layout. They can have a capacity of several hundred tonnes, depending on the ability of the structure to support the weight.

Overhead cranes consist of:

  • Wall mounted runway beams which support the crane and allow it to travel along their length
  • The bridge which is the main travelling structure that spans the width of the work area. At its simplest this would be a single bridge girder
  • End trucks house the wheels on which the entire crane travels. They sit at either end of the bridge and ride on the runway beams
  • Trolley hoist, with the trolley carrying the hoist across the bay along the bridge girder(s) traversing the span, and the hoist, which is mounted to the trolley, performing the actual lifting function via a hook or lifting attachment. Hoists are typically wire rope or chain hoists.

Gantry cranes are similar to overhead cranes, but are not fixed to the building structure. The bridge is supported by a gantry framework which may be rail mounted, to allow horizontal movement.

Mobile cranes

There are many different designs of mobile cranes. Those most commonly seen on construction sites include:

  • lorry mounted telescopic jib
  • all-terrain telescopic jib
  • rough-terrain telescopic jib
  • crawler cranes.

Lorry mounted telescopic

Telescopic mobile cranes are suitable for short duration operations, and where mobility around or on / off site is important.

These are versatile plant items which can travel to site on public roads and generally be positioned, rigged and in use in a very short time.

Typically a lorry mounted telescopic crane will have a capacity of between 15 and 1000 tonnes. Larger machines are available but lead to a loss of key benefits as additional counter weights, the boom and outriggers may need to be delivered on separate lorries.

Outriggers must be used to ensure stability. Care must be taken when positioning, as the outriggers must be footed on good ground.

All-terrain crane

An all-terrain crane is similar to a lorry mounted telescopic crane. It is a wheeled crane with a telescopic jib, and a specially designed chassis that allows travel on the highway at normal road speeds. It incorporates features, such as additional steering and driven axles, to give it better manoeuvrability on site.

Most all-terrain cranes can be fitted with fly jibs and luffing fly jibs. They are operated as lorry mounted telescopic cranes.

Note: An all-terrain crane is not the same as a rough-terrain crane and may not be able to travel over rough, un-compacted ground.
