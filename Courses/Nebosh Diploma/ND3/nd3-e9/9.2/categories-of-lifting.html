Categories of lifting equipment

The main categories of lifting equipment are cranes and hoists.

A crane is a machine used for the lifting and lowering and horizontal movement of a load.

Cranes are typically powered, though they can be manually operated and may be fixed (overhead or tower crane) or mobile.

CIRIA (2003) defined a crane as:

A machine incorporating an elevated structural member or jib beneath which suspended loads can be controllably raised or lowered vertically and also moved horizontally either by slewing the crane, derricking the jib or by other means not solely involving a travelling motion of the crane.

Slewing: rotary motion of a crane jib and superstructure about a vertical axis.

Derricking (or luffing): angular movement of the crane jib in a vertical plane.

A hoist is a device used for lifting and lowering a freely suspended (unguided) load.

Hoists may be integral to a crane or mounted in a fixed position, permanently or temporarily. They may be powered (electrical) or manually operated.
