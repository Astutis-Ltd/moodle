Lifting Operations and Lifting Equipment Regulations 1998 – a brief summary

Regulation 4: Strength and stability

Lifting equipment must be of adequate strength and stability for each load. The load and lifting attachments must also be of adequate strength. Note: PUWER applies regarding suitability for purpose.

Regulation 5: Equipment for lifting persons

So far as is reasonably practicable employers should:

  • Prevent people using it from being crushed, trapped, struck or falling from the carrier
  • Prevent risk of carrier falling
  • Ensure anyone trapped in carrier is not exposed to danger and can be freed.

Regulation 6: Positioning and installation

Position to reduce, so far as is reasonably practicable, the risk of:

  • The equipment or load striking a person
  • The load drifting, falling freely, or being released unintentionally.

Regulation 7: Marked with safe working load

Lifting machinery and accessories should be clearly marked with their safe working load, for each configuration if adjustable.

Lifting equipment should be marked as suitable for lifting persons (if that is the case), or as NOT suitable if likely to be used in error.

Regulation 8: Organisation of lifting operation

  • planned by a competent person
  • appropriately supervised
  • carried out in a safe manner.

Regulation 9: Thorough examination and inspection, by a competent person

Pre-use

  • Thoroughly examined before first use
  • If installation conditions are important, then the lifting machinery or accessories should be thoroughly examined following installation and before putting into service
  • If moveable, the equipment should be examined after every assembly on-site, before use.

In use

Periodically examined at least:

  • every 12 months for general equipment
  • every 6 months for person lifting equipment and lifting accessories
  • in accordance with an examination scheme devised by a competent person.

Thorough examination may include ‘testing’, if the competent person deems it appropriate.

The examination scheme may also specify periodic inspections at intervals between thorough examinations.

Regulation 10: Reports and defects

Existing, or imminent, risk of serious personal injury must be notified to the employer and the relevant enforcing authority, forthwith.

The employer must ensure that the equipment is removed from use until the defect is rectified.

Where defects need to be made good within a specified timescale the report should be submitted promptly.

In normal circumstances the competent person should complete the report and forward it within 28 days of the thorough examination.

Regulation 11: Keeping information

The employer must keep any EU Declaration of Conformity relating to lifting equipment for as long as he operates it.

Suitable records must be kept as shown in the table below.

Examination before first use
– lifting equipment 		until employer ceases to use.
Examination before first use
– moveable lifting equipment 		until it ceases to be used at place of installation or assembly.
Examination before first use
– lifting accessories 		two years after report is made.
Periodic examinations until the next report or two years – whichever is later.
