Lifting accessories

Lifting accessories or ‘tackle’ is the work equipment used to attach loads to machinery for lifting, including:

  • chain, wire rope and textile slings
  • hooks, rings, shackles and eye bolts
  • lifting beams and pulley blocks.

Lifting accessories can fail due to any combination of overloading, damaged equipment and inappropriate use.

Examples of inappropriate use would include:

  • use of a wrong eye bolt (one designed for a vertical lift used at an angle, for example)
  • too wide an angle between legs of sling
  • insecure attachment of load
  • not using hooks with safety catches
  • not packing the sharp corners of a load
  • incorrect slinging method.

Ropes slings and chains should be checked for the following signs of damage:

Textile slings
  • chemical burns
  • surface melting or charring
  • snags, punctures, tears, cuts or abrasions
  • broken or worn stitches
  • distortion of fittings.
Natural fibre ropes and slings
  • abnormal wear
  • powdered fibre between strands
  • broken or cut fibres
  • variations in the size or roundness of strands
  • discolouration or rotting
  • distortion of hardware in the sling.
Chains
  • deformation – e.g. stretched, kinked, twisted or bent
  • cracked, worn or corroded links.
Wire ropes
  • kinking
  • broken wires (needles)
  • ovalling due to stretching
  • bird-caging (outer strands springing away from inner core)
  • corrosion and damage to thimbles and ferrules.
