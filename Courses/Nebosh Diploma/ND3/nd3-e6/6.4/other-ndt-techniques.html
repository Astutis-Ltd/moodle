Other NDT techniques

There are many other techniques used for testing and evaluating materials and researchers continue to improve existing techniques and develop new ones. Some other common techniques are outlined below. An exhaustive list can be obtained from the British Institute of Non-Destructive Testing (BINDT – see reference section).

Leak testing techniques use hydrostatic pressure, air pressure, or pressurised gases to find leaks in pressure containment parts, pressure vessels, and structures. Leaks can be detected using a range of techniques of varying complexity from simple soap-bubble tests to the use of electronic listening devices.

Laser-based inspection techniques including:

  • laser shearography (or interferometry) which is used to detect sub surface flaws in laminar structures
  • laser ultrasonics which is used for defect mapping of contoured parts by scanning two laser beams over the surface of the part – one generates ultrasonic waves at the surface of the component and the other detects them.

Thermography uses a television camera with an infrared sensitive detector and a lens to obtain an image of the heat distribution over the surface of an object. Temperature variations as small as 0.1°C can be detected and displayed on screen as shades of grey or a pseudo-colour image.

There are many specialised NDT methods which have found limited use in specific fields, such as microwave methods, proton absorption, positron annihilation, and neutron scatter. Generally, these are laboratory techniques, not yet commercially developed.
