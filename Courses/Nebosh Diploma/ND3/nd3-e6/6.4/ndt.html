What is NDT?

Non-destructive testing is the branch of engineering concerned with the detection and evaluation of flaws in materials, such as cracks or inclusions in welds and castings, or variations in structural properties, which could lead to loss of strength or failure in service of a material or structure.

NDT is important in:

  • quality control
  • assessing plant life
  • assuring on-going safe operation.

NDT is used for in-service inspection and for condition monitoring of operating plant. It is also used for measurement of components and spacings, and for the measurement of physical properties such as hardness and internal stress.

NDT encompasses a range of test processes that produce no harmful effects on the material or structure under test. NDT techniques include:

  • simple visual examination of surfaces
  • dye penetrant techniques
  • radiography (gamma and X-ray)
  • acoustic methods
  • ultrasonic testing
  • eddy currents
  • magnetic particle inspection.
Simple visual examination

Careful visual examination of external and internal surfaces can reveal many flaws. Microscopes and borescopes (fibre optic probes, referred to as endoscopes for medical use) can be used to improve upon simple inspection with the naked eye. Inspections can be filmed and recorded. The cost increases with magnification and additional technology.
