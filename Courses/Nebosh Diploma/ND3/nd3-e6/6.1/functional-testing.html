Functional testing of safety-related parts

Safety-related parts might include:

  • guards and protective devices such as interlocked guards or light curtains (Electro-Sensitive Protective Equipment (ESPE) using Active Opto-electronic Protection Devices (AOPD)) which have to operate effectively to keep workers away from danger
  • Pbrakes and lights on mobile work equipment such as fork-lift trucksoint
  • emergency stop controls on machinery.
The use of new technologies to facilitate safe inspections

As discussed earlier, maintenance activities can potentially expose the workers involved (and others) to all sorts of hazards with the potential to cause serious harm. Some such as working at height and confined space entry must be avoided, so far as is reasonably practicable.

This has necessitated the development of new technologies to ensure inspections of workplaces and work equipment at height and confined spaces can be checked remotely without the need to place workers at risk.

Remote controlled vehicles and robotic devices are used to photograph and video internal surfaces of confined spaces, to take samples of contents or the atmosphere and to conduct non-destructive testing (NDT).

Drones (Unmanned aerial vehicles) are used to conduct remote visual inspections of high rise buildings and tall structures such as offshore platforms and wind turbines. The use of high-definition cameras enables visual inspections to be undertaken remotely and the use of software to 3D model structures and plant and equipment is useful in advanced planning of work that may need to be undertaken at height.

Record keeping

Records are not normally required to be made for the simplest pre-use checks.

Checklists can help in assuring consistency of inspection and record keeping. The focus should be on what is necessary for safety. An excessive approach can be perceived as burdensome and can lead to superficial tick box type checklists.

Records of equipment inspections do not have to be kept in a particular form, but should normally include:

  1. information on the type and model of equipment
  2. any identification mark or number that it has
  3. its normal location
  4. the date that the inspection was carried out
  5. who carried out the inspection
  6. any faults
  7. any action taken
  8. to whom the faults have been reported
  9. the date when repairs or other necessary actions were carried out.
