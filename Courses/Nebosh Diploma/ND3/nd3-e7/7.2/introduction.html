Introduction

There is no specific requirement for risk assessment within PUWER, however, it is implied or referenced in several Regulations, notably:

  • Regulation 4: Suitability of work equipment
  • Regulation 6: Inspections
  • Regulation 7: Specific risks
  • Regulation 11: Dangerous parts of machinery
  • Regulation 12: Protection against specific hazards.

Work equipment risk assessment

  • Identify hazards – mechanical and non-mechanical
  • Consider who might be harmed – operators, maintainers, cleaners, trainees, young people, etc.
  • Evaluate the risk (likelihood and severity of injury) and ensure the adequacy of control (in accordance with the principles of prevention).

The general risk assessment and control requirements of Regulations 3 to 5 of the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999 (MHSWR) overlap with the requirements of PUWER, and are used to help ensure the suitability of work equipment, the identification of hazards, and the implementation of control strategies.
