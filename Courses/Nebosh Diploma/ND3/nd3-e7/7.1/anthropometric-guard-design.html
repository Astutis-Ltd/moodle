Anthropometric guard design

Guards should be designed and constructed with the object of preventing any part of the body from reaching a danger zone. They should take account of the physical characteristics of the people involved and, in particular, their abilities to reach through openings, and over or around barriers or guards.

Openings in a guard

Where it is necessary to provide an opening in a guard, it should be at a sufficient distance to prevent any person from reaching the hazard. This may be achieved by:

  • positioning the guard at the required distance
  • providing a tunnel which extends outwards from it.

The effectiveness of a guard with an opening should be judged by a reach test, carried out with the machinery at rest and in a safe condition.

Access through a guard opening can often be prevented by the use of a false table.

To establish the safe distance for a guard to be away from the danger zone, consideration must be given to the size of openings within the guard (20mm mesh, for example) and therefore the body parts (such as fingers) that can be passed through. Anthropometric data for the working population can then be used to determine safe distances to keep the desired percentage of the population safe (95% or 99%, for example).
