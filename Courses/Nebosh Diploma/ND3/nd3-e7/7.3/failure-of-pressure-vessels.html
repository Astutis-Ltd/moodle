Failure of pressure vessels

Overpressure and over temperature

Overpressure and over temperature are the most common causes of failure of pressure systems, especially boilers.

Over temperature

Over temperature / overheating in boilers arises as a consequence of low water levels which is typically caused by inappropriately specified controls, a lack of testing and maintenance of controls, and inadequate water treatment causing scaling and localised overheating.

Overpressure

Overpressurisation can occur if temperature controls and pressure relief valves are inadequate by design or fail in service as a result of lack of testing or inadequate maintenance. Operator error may also be a factor. Note: pressure vessels are usually manufactured to withstand 3 times their safe working pressure.

Excessive stress

Excessive stress is stress beyond the capability of the vessel or system. This could arise because of poor design, because of stresses arising during manufacture of components or assembly of the system, the application of other external stresses (see below), or weakening of the system due to ‘wear and tear’.

Abnormal external loading

Abnormal external loading may occur from vehicular impacts, explosions from other vessels, or ladders being supported by pipework.

Mechanical fatigue and shock

The positive pressure within a pressure system causes tensile stresses in all directions. If pressure levels vary within a system the stresses also fluctuate. These cyclical fluctuations can give rise to fatigue failures.

Mechanical shock is a sudden acceleration or deceleration of an object typically induced by an impact. Mechanical shocks can cause brittle materials to fracture and ductile materials to stretch or bend.
