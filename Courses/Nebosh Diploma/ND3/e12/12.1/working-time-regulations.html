Working Time Regulations 1998

The Working Time Regulations (1998) (as amended) implement the European Working Time Directive into GB law.

The Regulations impose specific requirements on employers with regard to the number of hours worked and how these hours are scheduled. Compliance with the Regulations may not be sufficient to adequately control fatigue risks as some work patterns could be compliant but still be fatiguing and hence increase the risk of fatigue-related error, incidents, accidents and ill-health.

In general terms the regulations limit the maximum weekly working time to 48 hours over any seven-day period, typically averaged over 17 weeks. For young people under 18the limits are generally 8 hours a day or 40 hours a week.

Drivers of goods vehicles or passenger-carrying vehicles must follow specific rules on hours of driving and appropriate rest breaks.

There are 3 sets of rules that could apply to a particular journey:

  • EU rules
  • AETR rules (European Agreement Concerning the Work of Crews of Vehicles Engaged in International Road Transport) – now the same as the EU Rules
  • GB domestic rules.
EU Rules GB Rules

Drivers hours

  • Must not drive more than:
    • 9 hours in a day - this can be extended to 10 hours twice a week
    • 56 hours in a week
    • 90 hours in any 2 consecutive weeks.
  • All driving must be recorded on a tachograph.

Drivers hours

Goods vehicles

Must not drive for more than 10 hours a day or be on duty for more than 11 hours a day.

Passenger vehicles

Duty time is working time. Must not drive for more than 10 hours a day.

Breaks and rests

Drivers must take:

  • at least 11 hours rest every day - can be reduced to 9 hours rest 3 times between any 2 weekly rest periods
  • an unbroken rest period of 45 hours every week – can be reduced to 24 hours every other week
  • a break or breaks totalling at least 45 minutes after no more than 4 hours 30 minutes driving
  • a weekly rest after 6 consecutive 24-hour periods of working, starting from the end of the last weekly rest period taken.

Breaks and rests

After 5 hours 30 minutes of driving drivers must take a break of at least 30 minutes for rest and refreshment.

Within any period of 8 hours 30 minutes, drivers must take at least 45 minutes in breaks. A break of at least 30 minutes must also be taken at the end of this period, unless it’s the end of the working day.

Drivers must not work more than 16 hours between the times of starting and finishing work - including non-driving work and any off times.

A rest of 10 hours must be taken before the first duty and immediately after the last duty in a working week.

A rest of at least 10 hours must be taken between 2 working days. This can be reduced to 8.5 hours up to 3 times a week.

Every 2 weeks at least one period of 24 hours off duty must be taken.
