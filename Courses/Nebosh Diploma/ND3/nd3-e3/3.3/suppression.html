Suppression

Explosion suppression systems allow the control of a developing explosion by the rapid injection of a suitable suppressing medium into the flame front. They have been developed into reliable systems over years of testing and operating experience. They are classed as autonomous protective systems and need certification and appropriate marking under the Equipment and Protective Systems Intended for Use in Potentially Explosive Atmospheres (EPS) Regulations 1996.

Explosion suppression systems consist of a rapid-acting pressure sensor with a number of pressurised containers of suppressant designed for rapid injection into the protected vessel. The suppressant is commonly a dry powder, similar to those used in fire extinguishers, but in certain circumstances it may be water.

Interconnected plant

Many processes use a series of interconnected units of plant, such as grinders, elevators, cyclones, silos and filters. Appropriate precautions must be taken to prevent an explosion occurring in one unit of plant from spreading to adjacent units causing extensive damage.
