Mitigation measures

Having provided appropriate control measures, consideration should still be given to the residual risk of a fire or explosion occurring and measures taken to mitigate the detrimental effects arising from that risk.

Vitrification – the transformation of a substance into a glass.

Spalling – breaking off in fragments.

Mitigation measures could include:

Minimising the numbers of people potentially affected by:

  • adequately separating hazardous workplaces and storage areas from other parts of the premises and the site boundary
  • separating other workrooms within buildings containing hazardous workplaces by physical barriers that are fire-resisting structures.

Preventing fire and explosion from spreading to other vulnerable equipment or into the workroom by designing plant and equipment with suitable control devices.

For plant and equipment processing highly flammable solids and dusts this might include:

  • rotary valves
  • explosion suppression barriers
  • fast acting valves, chokes and baffles.

For interconnected plant and equipment processing flammable gases and vapours it might include:

  • flame arresters
  • fast acting valves
  • suppression barriers.

Vent pipes should discharge to a safe place outside and normally be provided with a flame arrestor.

Where there is a residual risk that an explosion could occur and cause injury, plant and equipment should be provided with explosion protection measures to minimise that risk of injury.

Explosion protection measures are designed to minimise the risk of injury from an explosion. For plant and equipment processing, dangerous substances include explosion relief venting, explosion suppression equipment, pressure shock resistant plant and pressure resistant plant. The design of the protection measure should mitigate possible explosions by:

  • relieving the explosion pressures and / or hot gases to a safe place outside of the workroom
  • suppressing the explosion before dangerous pressures build-up
  • safely containing the explosion without the plant rupturing.
