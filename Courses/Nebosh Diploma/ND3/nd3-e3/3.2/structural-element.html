Structural elements

The Building Regulations require that buildings “be designed and constructed so that, in the event of a fire, its stability will be maintained for a reasonable period”.

Approved Document B contains guidance on what constitutes a ‘reasonable period’. Structural fire resistance periods vary between 30, 60 and 90 minutes, according to the occupancy, the use of basements, and the height of the building.

BS9999, the Code of Practice for Fire Safety in the Design, Management and Use of Buildings provides an alternative to Approved Document B. It allows the development of solutions based on an understanding of the causes of risk to life and how these can be mitigated.

Under BS9999 buildings are classified according to a risk profile based on occupancy, fire growth rate, ventilation conditions and building height. It is more flexible than Approved Document B in allowing trade-offs based on the provision of automatic sprinklers installation and automatic fire detection.

Levels of fire resistance

The fire tests for ensuring that structural elements and materials achieve appropriate grades of fire resistance for building materials and structures are defined in the various parts of BS476.

Premature failure of the structure can be prevented by provisions for loadbearing elements of a structure to have a minimum standard of fire resistance.

Fire resistance is determined in accordance with performance over three criteria:

  • Resistance to collapse (Ioad-bearing capacity) – which applies to loadbearing elements only, denoted R in the European classification of the resistance to fire performance (BS EN 13501-2:2016)
  • Resistance to fire penetration (integrity) – denoted E
  • Resistance to the transfer of excessive heat (insulation) – denoted I.

Table A1 of Document B details the specific provisions of tests for fire resistance of elements of structure, etc. and specifies the minimum provisions when tested to the relevant part of BS476 (in minutes) for:

  • loadbearing capacity
  • integrity
  • insulation (as applicable).

… of the following structural components:

  • structural frame, beam or column
  • loadbearing wall
  • floors
  • roofs
  • external walls
  • compartment walls
  • protected shafts
  • firefighting shafts
  • enclosure (not a compartment)
  • subdivision of a corridor
  • fire resistant protection enclosing a fire hazard
  • cavity barrier
  • ceiling
  • duct
  • drainage system casings
  • flue.
