Fire alarm sounders

The fire warning sound levels should be loud enough to alert everyone, taking into account background noise. In areas with high background noise, or where people may be wearing hearing protectors, the audible warning should be supplemented with, for example, visual alarms.

In order to prevent excessive sound pressure levels, which can cause disorientation or even damage to hearing, a larger number of quieter sounders is preferable to a few very loud sounders.

BS 5839-1:2017: Fire detection and fire alarm systems for buildings recommends that the sound pressure level of alarm signals should be:

  • generally, not less than 65 dB(A) (though this may be reduced to 60 dB(A) in stairways and small enclosures such as cellular offices)
  • 5 dB above the sound pressure level of the background noise, where it is greater than 60 dB(A)
  • not less than 75 dB(A) at the bedhead within rooms in which the fire alarm system is intended to rouse people from sleep
  • not greater than 120 dB(A) at any normally accessible point.
