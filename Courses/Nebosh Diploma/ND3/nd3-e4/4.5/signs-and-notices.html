Signs & notices

Signs

Signs must be used, where necessary, to help people identify escape routes, find fire-fighting equipment and emergency fire telephones.

Signs must be in pictogram form to comply with the Health and Safety (Safety Signs and Signals) Regulations. The pictogram can be supplemented by text if this is considered necessary to make the sign more easily understood.

Notices

    Notices must be used, where necessary, to provide the following:

  • instructions on how to use any fire safety equipment
  • the actions to be taken in the event of fire
  • help for the fire and rescue service, such as the location of
  • sprinkler valves or electrical cut-off switches.

All signs and notices should be positioned so that they can be easily seen and understood.

Maintaining fire safety in communal areas

As discussed earlier under Article 3 of the RRFSO the ‘responsible person’ / key duty holder is primarily the employer in regard to any workplace and to the extent it is under his control.

    In all other cases the responsible person is:

  • the person who has control of the premises (as occupier or otherwise) in connection with the carrying on by him of a trade, business or other undertaking (for profit or not)
  • or the owner of the premises.

Article 22 lays down the requirements for co-operation and coordination where two or more responsible persons have duties in respect of premises (whether permanently or temporarily).

    Each responsible person must:

  • co-operate with the other(s) to enable them to comply with the requirements and prohibitions imposed on them by the RRFSO
  • take all reasonable steps to coordinate with the other responsible persons the measures necessary to comply with the RRFSO
  • take all reasonable steps to inform the other responsible persons concerned of the risks to relevant persons arising out of or in connection with the conduct of his undertaking.

In communal areas of shared commercial premises and houses in multiple occupation it is likely that the managing agent or owner will be responsible for shared parts of premises (risk assessments, site rules etc.) and shared fire safety equipment such as fire-warning systems, sprinklers, emergency lighting and signage etc.

Each employer will be responsible for what goes on in their own workplace and all parties will need to co-operate and coordinate their activities as discussed above.
