Introduction

A fire risk assessment involves an organised and methodical look at the workplace and the activities carried on there, and a careful consideration of the likelihood that a fire could start and cause harm to those in and around the premises.

    The aims of the fire risk assessment are:

  • to identify the fire hazards
  • to reduce the risk of those hazards causing harm to as low as reasonably practicable
  • to determine what physical fire precautions and management arrangements are necessary to safeguard people, assets and the premises if a fire does start.

The terminology and process of a fire risk assessment are consistent with that of general health and safety risk assessment.

A fire hazard is something that could cause a fire.

Fire risk considers the likelihood of a fire occurring and the seriousness of a fire should it occur.

The fire risk assessment is conducted in 5 steps as shown in the table below.

1

    Identify fire hazards

  • Sources of ignition
  • Sources of fuel
  • Sources of oxygen.
2

    Identify the people at risk

  • People in the area around the premises
  • People especially at risk.
3

Evaluate, remove, reduce and protect from risk

  • Evaluate the risk of a fire occurring, and the risk to people from the fire
  • Remove or reduce fire hazards, and the risks to people*
  • Provide and maintain:*
    • detection and warning equipment
    • fire-fighting equipment
    • escape routes
    • emergency lighting
    • signs and notices.
4

    Record, plan, inform, instruct and train

  • Record significant findings and action taken
  • Prepare an emergency plan*
  • Inform and instruct relevant people, co-operate and coordinate with others
  • Provide training.*
5

    Review

  • Keep assessment under review
  • Revise where necessary.

* Note: General advice on fire safety management, emergency plans and general fire safety training follows.
