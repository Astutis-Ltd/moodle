Regulatory powers of a fire and rescue authority with respect to fire safety

Section 6 of the Fire and Rescue Services Act 2004 places a statutory duty upon the fire and rescue authority to make provision for the purpose of promoting fire safety in its area. Reasonable arrangements must be made for:

  • the provision of information, publicity and encouragement in respect of the steps to be taken to prevent fires and death or injury by fire
  • the giving of advice, on request, about how to prevent fires and restrict their spread in buildings and other property
  • the means of escape from buildings and other property in case of fire.

Article 26(1) of the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 (RRFSO) places a duty on every enforcing authority to enforce the provisions of the Order (relating to fire safety in non- domestic premises) and any regulations made under it in relation to premises for which it is the enforcing authority. See notes on Part 3 of the RRFSO (Enforcement) below.
