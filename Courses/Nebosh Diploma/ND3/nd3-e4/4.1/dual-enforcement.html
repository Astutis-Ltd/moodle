Dual enforcement

The Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 places duties on ‘enforcing authorities’ to enforce fire safety in non-domestic premises.

The Fire and Rescue Authority is normally the main enforcing authority for the Order but there are some special areas where others have been appointed as the enforcing authority.

    The HSE has enforcement responsibility for process fire precautions (e.g under the Dangerous Substances and Explosive Atmospheres Regulations – DSEAR) and general fire precautions under the RRFSO in the following sectors:

  • Construction sites (as defined under the Construction Design and management Regulations 2015 - CDM)
  • Ship building and ship-repair
  • Major Accident Hazard sites, subject to the requirements of the Control of Major Accident Hazard Regulations 2015 (CoMAH).

The Office for Nuclear Regulation (ONR) enforces process fire precautions and general fire precautions in any premises for which a licence or permit is required under the Nuclear Installations Act 1965 and for Crown Premises that would otherwise require such a license or permit. The ONR became a public corporation, separate from HSE, in 2014.

Local authorities (LA) have enforcement responsibility at designated sports grounds and regulated stands under the Safety of Sports Grounds Acts 1975 and the Fire Safety and Safety of Places of Sport Act 1987. Where a sports ground contains a regulated stand, the local authority is the enforcing authority only for the stand and the Fire and Rescue Authority (FRA) is the enforcing authority for the rest of the sports ground. In practise the LA and FRA may well be the same authority.

Fire Inspectors of the Crown Premises Inspection Group are responsible for enforcement of RRFSO in all civilian prisons, young offenders institutions, immigration detention, holding or removal centres, court custody suites, customs and excise detention areas regardless of whether they are operated by a Government department or contracted out.

The Chief Fire Officers Association (CFAO) has prepared an Enforcement Management Model and extensive procedural guidance, including audit and inspection procedures, to assist Fire and Rescue Authorities in their work under the Order. The model is based on that used by the HSE.
