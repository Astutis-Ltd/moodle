Hazard classification

The capability of a sprinkler system is designed to match the type of risk with which it will have to cope.

    There are three main hazard classifications based on:

  • the expected fuel load of the premises
  • the rate of fire growth expected from the contents or processes.

These are:

Light hazard Low combustible loading, with a slow rate of fire growth
Ordinary hazard Low to moderate combustible loading, with moderate to fast rate of fire growth
High hazard High combustible content, with fast to ultra-fast rate of fire growth

    The ordinary and high hazard classes are subdivided to further qualify the type of risk. The hazard classification determines:

  • the minimum amount of water that must be provided at the fire in the form of spray (design density in mm / min or litres per square metre per minute)
  • the Assumed Maximum Area of Operation (AMAO), which is the expected maximum area of the sprinkler system that will be activated by the fire (in square metres).
Basic design considerations – summary

    The key issues to be considered in designing a sprinkler installation are:

  • the capacity of water required (volume and rate of flow)
  • a reliable water supply (and a back-up alternative supply)
  • design of the pumping system with diesel back up, should the mains electricity fail
  • means of activation – frangible bulbs or fire detector
  • relationship with detection and alarm system
  • protection of the sprinkler heads and the electrical system
  • the spray pattern required
  • the area to be covered and the fire loading density (amount and type of combustibles)
  • fire stopping for sprinklers passing through compartmentalisation
  • provision for fire water run-off
  • the arrangements for testing the equipment.

Deluge installations

A deluge sprinkler system would be installed in an area where it is necessary to operate all of the sprinklers simultaneously. The sprinklers, sprayers or nozzles are of the unsealed or open type, and are attached to a system of pipework connected to a deluge valve or, for smaller systems, a multiple control.

Fire sensing may be by electronic fire detection, or by a dry pilot system in the risk area.

Installations of this type are normally found on oil or flammable liquid risks, gaseous risks, cooling from exposure risks and high hazard group 4 process risks.

Drenchers

A drencher system sprays water over a surface (a water curtain) to provide protection against fire exposure / radiated heat. Drencher systems may be fitted to vulnerable buildings or gas storage tanks.
