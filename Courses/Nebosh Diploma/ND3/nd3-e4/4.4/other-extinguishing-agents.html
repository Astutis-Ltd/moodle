Other extinguishing agents

Foam

Foam systems are most commonly used to protect flammable liquid pool / surface fire hazards. Foam is not suitable for live electrical hazards, or three-dimensional running fuel fires.

Gas flooding

    Gaseous systems are an effective means of attacking fires in electrical risks such as:

  • Electronic Data Processing (EDP) facilities, control rooms and communications equipment
  • Ordinary Class A hazards, such as record stores
  • Class B hazards, such as flammable liquid fires in plant enclosures and flammable liquid stores.

Gaseous systems are ‘clean’, as they limit smoke generation and leave no post-fire residues for clear-up.

Modern gas flooding systems use halocarbons or inert gas mixes.

Historically Halon was used in gas flooding systems, but its use as an extinguishing agent in the EU was banned in 2000, because of its high ozone depleting potential.

Carbon dioxide has also been traditionally used, however its asphyxiant properties introduce a major hazard. In 2000, a US Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) study on CO2 related incidents in fire scenarios reported that between 1975 and 2000 there were 51 recorded incidents involving the discharge of CO2 fire extinguishing systems, resulting in 72 deaths and 145 injuries.

Dry powder

Dry chemical powder is intended for use in extinguishing fires involving bulk chemical agents and liquefied gases.

Water mist

The term ‘water mist’ refers to fine water sprays in which 99% of the volume of the spray is in drops with diameters less than 1000 microns.

Water mist systems are able to extinguish a wide variety of fires where natural ventilation, such as open doors and hatches, would render gaseous agents ineffective. Water mist also reduces compartment temperature and improves visibility allowing accessibility to the compartment during fire suppression. The potential for water damage however is a significant concern.

Water mist systems have been popular in the USA for several years. A European Technical Specification (not a Standard) CEN / TS 14972:2011 Fixed firefighting systems–Watermist systems–Design and installation was introduced in 2011 and in 2020 was adopted as a British and European standard (BS EN 14972-1:2020 Fixed firefighting systems. Water mist systems. Design, installation, inspection and maintenance.)

BSI has also published a standard for the design and installation of water mist systems – BS 8489 - 1:2016 Fixed fire protection systems. Industrial and commercial watermist systems. Code of practice for design and installation. This gives recommendations for the design, installation, commissioning and maintenance of fixed watermist systems within industrial and commercial buildings.
