Emergency arrangements

Regulation 5 states that suitable and sufficient emergency rescue arrangements must be in place before anyone enters or carries out work in a confined space.

Arrangements for emergency rescue will depend on the nature of the confined space, the risks identified and the likely nature of an emergency rescue.

Rescue equipment

Rescue equipment, including lifelines, lifting equipment, breathing apparatus and first-aid equipment, should be provided and properly maintained.

Raising the alarm and rescue

Suitable methods of communication are required to initiate rescue procedures or summon help in an emergency.

Safeguarding the rescuers

Multiple fatalities have occurred when rescuers have been overcome by the same conditions that have affected those needing rescue. Those who have been assigned a rescue role must be protected against the cause of the emergency.

Control of plant

Adjacent or nearby plant may need to be shut down before attempting an emergency rescue.

First aid

Trained first-aiders with appropriate first-aid equipment should be provided and available for emergencies until professional medical help arrives.

Public emergency services

In some high risk cases there may be advantage in prior notification to the local emergency services before the work is undertaken. In such cases appropriate liaison with the emergency services is essential.

Arrangements must be in place for the rapid notification of the emergency services should an accident occur.

On arrival, the emergency services should be given all known information about the conditions and risks of entering and / or leaving the confined space before attempting a rescue.

Training

Those likely to be involved in any emergency rescue should be trained for that purpose. Refresher training should be organised and available on a regular basis, for example, annually. Training should include:

  • the likely causes of an emergency
  • use of rescue equipment
  • plant shutdown
  • emergency first aid including resuscitation
  • liaison with emergency services
  • rescue techniques including regular and periodic rehearsals / exercises.
