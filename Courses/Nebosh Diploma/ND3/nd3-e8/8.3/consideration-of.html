Consideration of ergonomic principles

The discomfort, fatigue and physical and psychological stress faced by the operator, under the intended conditions of use, must be reduced to the minimum possible, taking into account ergonomic principles such as:

  • Accommodating variability of the operator’s size, strength and stamina
  • Providing adequate space for movements of the parts of the operator’s body
  • Avoiding a machine-determined work rate
  • Avoiding monitoring that requires lengthy concentration
  • Adapting the man/machinery interface to the foreseeable characteristics of the operators
