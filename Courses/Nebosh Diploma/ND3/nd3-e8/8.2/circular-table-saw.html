Circular saw / table saw and guillotine

Circular saw / table saw

Mechanical hazards

  • Cutting / severing from saw blade
  • Trapping in drive machinery
  • Ejection of work piece.

Non-mechanical hazards

  • Manual handling
  • Noise
  • Vibration
  • Electricity
  • Heat generated by cutting
  • Dust – respiratory and fire hazards
  • Environment – weather
  • Stability of machinery.

Control measures

  • Fixed guards to drive mechanisms and to exposed blade beneath bench
  • Adjustable top guard over the blade
  • Riving knife behind blade to hold cut timber open
  • Push stick used to present timber to blade
  • LEV for dust
  • Good housekeeping around saw
  • Appropriate PPE – Eye protection, hearing protection, dust mask and gloves
  • Regular maintenance, including blade sharpening
  • Regular inspection and testing of electrical equipment.

Guillotine

Mechanical hazards

  • Contacts – cutting on stationary blade edge or cut edges of material
  • Severing / shearing from contact with moving blade
  • Crushing from clamps
  • Impact / crushing from counterbalance
  • High pressure fluid ejection on hydraulic guillotines.

Non-mechanical hazards

  • Noise from clamping, cutting and falling workpieces
  • Vibration from feeding metal
  • Manual handling of sheet metal – weight, shape and sharp edges.

Control measures

  • Front finger guard to protect hands at feed in – opening insufficient to reach clamp(s) or blade
  • Built in lighting and marker to see cutting line
  • Sloped discharge chute to enable workpieces to be retrieved without opening guard
  • Rear interlocking guard and / or light curtain
  • Shrouded foot pedal.
