Mechanical and hydraulic presses

Mechanical hazards

  • Crushing within press mechanism
  • Entanglement around rotating parts of mechanical presses
  • Ejection of work pieces
  • High pressure injection of hydraulic fluid.

Non-mechanical hazards

  • Electricity
  • Noise
  • Materials handling
  • Ergonomics – operating position
  • Housekeeping – slips / trips, etc.

Control measures

    Mechanical press using interlocking guards:

  • Ensure that closing gates overlap by at least 10% of total movement before clutch can be operated
  • Good guard maintenance
  • Shroud foot pedals
  • Repair distorted or damaged bars.

    • Hydraulic press brake using photoelectric guards:

  • Fixed guard back and sides of the press
  • Ensure no-one can stand between the light curtain and machine, or reach under, around or over the light curtain
  • Ensure that tripping the photoelectric light curtain stops downward press movement before dangerous parts can be reached.
