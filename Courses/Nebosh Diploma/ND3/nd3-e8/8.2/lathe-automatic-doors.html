Lathe, automatic doors and gates

Lathe

Mechanical hazards

  • Entanglement around the chuck or rotating stock bar (workpiece)
  • Entanglement in drive mechanisms
  • Contacts – friction off rotating parts, cutting off cutting tool or swarf
  • Ejections – swarf / broken workpiece or tool or tool post / chuck key.

Non-mechanical hazards

  • Electricity
  • Noise
  • Vibration from delivering tool to stock bar
  • Thermal – heat from operation / friction
  • Hazardous substances – coolant / metal dust
  • Ergonomics – operating posture
  • Working environment – space and light
  • Manual handling of stock bars
  • Housekeeping – slips / trips.

Control measures

  • Fixed guard at rear of machine
  • Chuck guard
  • Feed and lead screws guarded or well back from operator
  • Splashguard for suds and swarf.

Automatic doors and gates

Mechanical hazards

  • Will depend on design of door or gate
  • Impact from leading edge
  • Crushing between door / gate and frame / floor.

Non-mechanical hazards

  • Aberrant behaviour – electrical interference.

Control measures

  • Automatic detection trip devices
  • Bump strips.
