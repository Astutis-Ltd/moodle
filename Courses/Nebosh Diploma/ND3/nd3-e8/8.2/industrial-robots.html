Industrial robots

Mechanical hazards

  • Impact from moving arms
  • Crushing between moving arm and other machinery / perimeter fencing
  • Trapping / shearing at articulation points and ‘grippers’
  • Ejection of work pieces – aberrant behaviours
  • High pressure hydraulic fluid injection
  • Entanglement and traps in turntable drive mechanisms.

Non-mechanical hazards

  • Will depend upon design and application of robot arm, for example, welding or paint spraying
  • Electricity
  • Noise.

Control measures

  • Keep people at a safe distance from the robot whenever it is in operation or capable of movement (through perimeter fencing and interlocked gate access)
  • Only allow access when the robot (and ancillary equipment) is in a safe state:
  • Controlled access
  • Isolations
  • Reduced speed operations
  • Hold to operate controls
  • Other protection devices – light curtains, pressure mats, etc.
