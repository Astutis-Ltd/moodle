CE Marking (stages 1-2)

The CE marking requirements under the Machinery Directive relate to new machinery. However, if a machine is modified to such an extent that new hazards are anticipated, this will be considered a significant modification and the same measures will need to be taken as for new machinery.

The process can be broken down into the following stages:

  1. Categorise the product
  2. Check the application of additional directives
  3. Ensure that requirements of safety regulations are met
  4. Perform the risk assessment
  5. Compile the technical file
  6. Issue the declaration of conformity
  7. Affix the CE mark.

(1) Categorise the product

The first thing to be established is whether or not the product is subject to the requirements of the Machinery Directive. The latest Machinery Directive introduced some new products such as pressure vessels, steam boilers and funicular railways, and omitted others such as electrical household and office equipment).

Machinery listed in Annex IV of the Machinery Directive (and Annex IV of SMSR 2008) is considered particularly hazardous (presses, woodworking machinery, service lifts, etc.) and additional requirements must be met for CE marking and the declaration of conformity (see image above).

Sub-systems and ‘partially completed’ machinery also follow a different declaration procedure (Annex IIB).

Safety components are also now treated as machinery and given a CE mark.

(2) Check the application of additional directives/p>

Other EU directives also provide for the affixing of the CE mark, such as the Low Voltage Directive and the EMC Directive. The provisions of these directives (if relevant) must also be met before the CE mark is applied.
