The Work at Height Regulations 2005 – overview

Regulations

Regulation 2: Interpretation

Work at height means:

  1. work in any place, including a place at or below ground level…
  2. obtaining access to or egress from such place while at work, except by a staircase in a permanent workplace…

… where, if appropriate measures are not taken, a person could fall a distance liable to cause personal injury.

Working platform means:

  1. any platform used as a place of work or as a means of access to or egress from a place of work
  2. includes any scaffold, suspended scaffold, cradle, mobile platform, trestle, gangway, gantry and stairway which is so used.

Personal fall protection system means:

  1. a fall prevention, work restraint, work positioning, fall arrest or rescue system, other than a system in which the only safeguards are collective safeguards
  2. rope access and positioning techniques.

Suitable means suitable in any respect which it is reasonably foreseeable will affect the safety of any person.

Regulation 3: Application

Employer includes self-employed workers and anybody else who controls work at height.

Regulation 4: Organising and planning

Employers must ensure that work at height is:

  • properly planned
  • appropriately supervised
  • carried out in a safe manner (so far as is reasonably practicable).

Planning must include:

  • selection of suitable equipment
  • planning for emergencies and rescue.

Work must take place only when weather will not jeopardise safety (except for emergency services in an emergency).

Regulation 5: Competence

Employers must ensure that all those engaged to organise, plan or carry out work at height are competent (or adequately supervised if under training).

Regulation 6: Avoidance of risks from work at height

Precautions to be based on risk assessment as per Regulation 3 of the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations (MHSWR).

Work at height must be avoided so far as is reasonably practicable (sfairp).

Where work at height cannot be avoided suitable and sufficient measures must be taken sfairp to prevent any person fall a distance liable to cause personal injury.

The measures are firstly to work from an existing safe place, and if this is not reasonably practicable then to provide sufficient work equipment to prevent a fall (see Regulation 7).

If prevention of falling is not possible then the requirement is to minimise the distance fallen and the consequences thereof.

Suitable training should be provided where necessary.

Regulation 7: Selection of work equipment

In selecting equipment employers must:

  • Give collective measures priority over personal protection
  • Take account of:
    • working conditions
    • others at the place of work
    • distance and consequences of a fall
    • duration and frequency of use
    • the need for emergency evacuation and rescue
    • risks imposed by installation and removal of equipment.
  • Select equipment which is appropriate to the task and the foreseeable loadings and is the most suitable equipment for the task and situation.

Regulation 8: Requirements for particular work equipment

Regulation 8 imposes specifications contained in the Schedules to the Regulations (see next page for more details about the Schedules).

Regulation 9: Fragile surfaces

Work on or near a fragile surface is prohibited if it is reasonably practicable to do it from elsewhere. If work on or near fragile surfaces is undertaken, suitable measures, including where appropriate warning signs and barriers to prevent falls through fragile surfaces, must be in place.

Regulation 10: Falling objects

Requires measures to prevent the fall of any object or material and, where this is not possible, to prevent injury therefrom.

Tipping or throwing of materials is prohibited where it is liable to cause injury.

Materials and objects must be stored in such a way as to prevent risk from unintended movement.

Regulation 11: Danger areas

Danger areas (where there is a risk of any person at work falling a distance or being struck by a falling object, which is liable to cause personal injury) should be clearly indicated and measures taken to prevent entry.

Regulation 12 & Regulation 13: Inspections of work equipment and places of work at height

Equipment in Schedules 2 to 6 should be inspected before first use in a new location, periodically, and after exceptional circumstances that may affect safety.

The surface and every parapet, permanent rail or other such fall protection measure of every place of work at height should be checked on each occasion before the place is used.

Work platforms above 2m and used in construction should be inspected at least every seven days.

Regulation 14: Duties of persons at work

All persons at work must:

  • report any activity or defect relating to work at height that is likely to cause danger
  • work in accordance with instructions and training.

(Note: this replicates Regulation 14 of MHSWR)

Schedules
Schedule Specific requirements for:
1 Existing places of work and means of access for work at height
2 Collective fall prevention (for example, guard rails and toe boards)
3 Working platforms
4 Collective fall arrest (for example, nets, airbags, etc.)
5 Personal fall protection (for example, work restraints, work positioning, fall arrest and rope access)
6 Ladders and step ladders
7 Inspection reports (for working platforms in construction only)
