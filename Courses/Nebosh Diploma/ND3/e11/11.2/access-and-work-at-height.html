Access and work at height equipment: hazards and control measures

Mobile Elevating Work Platforms (MEWPs)

Mobile Elevating Work Platforms (MEWPs) are either of a scissor lift (vertical lift only) or ‘cherry picker’ design. Cherry pickers may have telescopic or articulated booms, which allow a range of platform movements. MEWPs may be vehicle mounted, trailer mounted, or self-propelled.

The increasing use of MEWPs is believed to have reduced the numbers of fatal fall from height accidents in the construction sector. However between 2003 and 2009 seven people were killed as a result of being crushed between a MEWP and a fixed structure. Other fatalities have been reported as a result of a MEWP collapsing or overturning, and as a result of operators being thrown from the carrier.

HSE analysis of reported accidents identified the following range of primary causes:

  • equipment failure
  • ground conditions
  • outriggers (not used or faulty)
  • trapping against fixed structure
  • MEWP being struck by vehicle
  • load / unload of MEWP under power
  • overloading a carrier
  • carrier struck by a load.

Safe systems of work for using a MEWP can be developed by considering the hazards and corresponding precautions relating to the plant, workplace and operator.

Safe plant

The right MEWP should be selected for the job, following consideration of ground conditions, working height, safe working load (people and equipment) and manoeuvrability.

The machine should be inspected before use to ensure it is in good working order and free of obvious defects. Ground and cage controls should be checked, to ensure they are working effectively.

Evidence of its statutory 6 monthly inspection under LOLER should also be checked.

The platform should be of sufficient size and be fitted with edge protection comprising guard rail, intermediate rail and toe boards.

Appropriate fall protection anchors should be provided, suitable for use with the fall protection equipment determined by risk assessment. This may be:

  • work restraint systems which prevent falling from the carrier
  • fall arrest systems which stop a person falling to the floor.

Emergency lowering controls should be provided to bring the working platform down safely in an emergency.

MEWPs should only be moved with a worker in the elevated position if the machine is designed to do so and only on level ground. The route should be walked and checked for potholes, etc. beforehand. Jolts from wheel impacts will be amplified along the boom and may have a severe effect at the working platform.

Outriggers, if fitted, should be fully extended and chocked in place prior to raising the carrier. Manufacturer’s instructions should be followed.

The MEWP must be maintained in safe working order. Planned preventative maintenance should be carried out by competent persons in accordance with the manufacturer’s instructions.

The MEWP should be regularly inspected to ensure it remains in safe working order. The inspection regime should include:

  • pre-use checks by the operator
  • weekly inspections by a responsible person
  • thorough examination by a competent person at least once every six months.

Safe workplace

Hazards presented at the workplace can be sub-categorised as relating to the general environment, the management of the site, and the activity to be undertaken.

Environment

Site access – terrain and gradient to get to work position.

Ground conditions – i.e. rough, prepared, poured slab, finished surface, etc. If the ground is rough, are there issues with softness, slopes, pot holes, etc?

Overhead obstructions, especially electricity cables – MEWPs should keep at least 9m clear of overhead electricity supplies on timber poles and 15m from steel pylons. MEWPs should not pass under overhead power lines if extended to any degree.

Weather conditions (for example, maximum wind speed – most MEWPs are not capable of operating in winds greater than a strong breeze (Beaufort scale level 6 – 28 to 31mph or 12ms-1 ).

Management

  • segregation of other site traffic from the proposed work area
  • effective communication systems
  • permit to work procedures
  • emergency plans.

Work activity

As an example, if MEWPs are to be used directly to install materials, it is essential to know the weight and dimensions of those materials and to properly consider any manual handling and load distribution issues.

Safe operator

Personal characteristics

The operator must be physically fit to operate the equipment (good eyesight and hand-eye coordination and no problems with heights), and mature enough to cope with the responsibility of the role.

Training

The operator must be competent to use the class of MEWP in the operating environment (approved training by Construction Skills or International Powered Access Federation – IPAF).

Operators should also be provided with any necessary task-specific training for the operations to be conducted from the MEWP.

Fall Protection

It is not normally necessary for personnel working from a vertical lift (such as a scissor lift) to wear fall protection equipment, other than in exceptional circumstances.

Anchor points in the work platform should be rated and marked as suitable for either work restraint or fall arrest, and the numbers of people for which they are rated.

A full body harness with an adjustable lanyard (used to provide work restraint and adjusted to be as short as possible) is used when working from a boom type Mobile Elevating Work Platform (MEWP).

Retractable lanyards are often used to provide the occupants with maximum freedom of movement, together with immediate restraint in the event of impact or levelling system failure.

The use of fall arrest equipment within MEWPs presents a number of significant challenges:

  • A fall distance of >5m is necessary for effective deployment
  • The dynamic impact of a fall may cause the MEWP to flex, resulting in extreme swing movements that could cause the MEWP to topple, or throw people and equipment from the carrier.
