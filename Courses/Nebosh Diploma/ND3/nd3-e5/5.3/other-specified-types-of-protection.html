Other specified types of protection

The NEBOSH Diploma syllabus requires an understanding of the following protection methods:

  • intrinsic safety (Ex i)
  • flameproof (Ex d)
  • increased safety (Ex e)
  • non-sparking (Ex n).

The table below provides an overview of the approach to protection, and appropriate uses of each type.

Flameproof Ex d

Equipment that may cause an explosion is contained within an enclosure which can withstand the force of an explosion and prevent transmission to the outside hazardous atmosphere.

Also prevents the hazardous atmosphere from entering the enclosure and coming into contact with equipment.

Examples include motors, switchgear and hand-lamps. 		2

Increased safety Ex e

Intended to prevent a potential ignition arising, by applying extra precautions to ensure increased security against the possibility of excessive temperatures and sparks.

Equipment that normally causes sparks is excluded from use within this method of protection.

Examples include transformers and induction motors. 		2

Non-sparking Ex n

Precautions are taken so that electrical equipment that has the potential to arc is not capable of igniting a surrounding explosive atmosphere.

Most applications of intrinsically safe systems require a signal to be sent out of, or into, the hazardous area. The equipment mounted in the hazardous area and the safety barrier must be approved for use in an intrinsically safe system. The barrier designed to protect the system must be mounted outside of the hazardous area, in the non-hazardous (safe) area where the hazard will not be present.

The basic design of an intrinsic safety barrier uses Zener diodes to limit voltage, resistors to limit current, and a fuse.
