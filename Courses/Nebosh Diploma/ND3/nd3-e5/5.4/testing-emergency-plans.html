Testing emergency plans

One of the principal inputs to the process of reviewing the emergency plans comes from the results of testing emergency plans.

    Testing should be designed to assure confidence in:

  • the completeness, consistency and accuracy of the emergency plan and other key documentation
  • the adequacy and operability of the equipment and facilities under emergency conditions
  • the competence of staff to carry out their identified duties and use equipment and facilities.

Overall the testing regime for internal and external emergency plans should examine the following aspects of the emergency response:

Activation and notification

Alerting emergency services.

Sounding alarms.

Mobilising identified personnel.
On-site Emergency Control Centre (ECC)

Suitable location for directing and coordinating the on-site response to the emergency within a suitable time.

The ability to establish an alternative on-site ECC should the designated ECC become untenable.
Off-site ECC A suitable place from where the off-site response to the emergency can be directed and co-ordinated.
Supply of information to ECC

Information from participating organisations is supplied promptly and accurately.

The ECC, has an up-to-date picture of the emergency and the response to it, to enable effective decision making.
Communicating within the ECC Necessary information is exchanged and disseminated in a form that can be understood and assimilated.
Team working Participating organisations are able to work together effectively to develop the response to the emergency.
Decision making Advice is provided by all participating organisations allowing rational decisions to be made and implemented.
Communication and public information

Information on the emergency, and the response to it, is passed to all participating organisations and to the appropriate media.

Preparing briefs for the media and establishing a media briefing centre.

Consideration of the effect of the media in the event of an emergency, and the ability of the ECC to make information available for handling their enquiries.
Equipment and facilities Equipment identified as having a role in the response to an emergency is operational, and participants are able to use it.

Exercises to test internal and external emergency plans (and train key personnel in preparation for dealing with an emergency) include:

Drills Testing a specific and relatively simple aspect of the emergency plan in isolation, for example, fire drills, alarm testing, evacuation, roll call and searching, cascade telephone calls, spillage control and recovery.
Seminar exercises Facilitate discussion about the different organisations’ responses in particular circumstances during an emergency.
Walk-through exercises Training staff or developing emergency plans. The emergency response is ‘walked through’, including visiting appropriate facilities such as ECCs.
Table-top exercises Allowing information exchange and dissemination between organisations at the ECC, together with decision making, to be tested. They are carried out in relation to a model, plans or photographs to depict the establishment. They could involve using information technology or virtual reality systems.
Control post exercises Testing the communication arrangements during an emergency, with participating organisations located where they would be during an emergency.
Live exercises Fully testing some or all aspects of the emergency plan for the internal and external response.
