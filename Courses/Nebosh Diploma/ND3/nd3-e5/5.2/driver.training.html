Certain drivers of road vehicles carrying dangerous goods may need to hold a Vocational Training Certificate (VTC) or ‘ADR certificate’. In Great Britain, the VTC is obtained after a driver has attended a training course approved by the DfT (Department for Transport) as competent authority, and successfully completed an examination set and marked by the Scottish Qualifications Authority (SQA).

Training should be carried out before a person assumes responsibilities in relation to dangerous goods. Otherwise duties should be carried out only under the direct supervision of a trained person.

The carrier is required to keep a record of training, and drivers are required to carry their training certificates. ADR requires a standard ‘credit card’ format with security features including a photo of the holder – all new or replacement certificates will be in this format.

General training covers:

  • awareness of hazards arising in the carriage of dangerous goods
  • basic information for minimising the likelihood of an incident taking place
  • measures necessary for ensuring personal safety and the safety of the public and the environment, should an incident take place
  • information for limiting the effects of an incident.

ADR training is intended to assure the drivers knowledge and skills in regard of:

  • Core operational duties
  • Practical application of specific health and safety techniques
  • Preventative and safety measures appropriate to the various types of hazards in Classes 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, or 9
  • Carrying dangerous goods in packages and / or bulk-fixed tanks / demountable tanks / tank containers / portable tanks or Multiple-Element Gas Containers (MEGCs)
  • Specialist knowledge and skills necessary for carrying Class 1 substances or articles (explosives)
  • Specialist knowledge and skills necessary for carrying Class 7 substances or articles (radioactive materials).
