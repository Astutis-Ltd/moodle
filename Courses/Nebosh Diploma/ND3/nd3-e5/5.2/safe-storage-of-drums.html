Safe storage of drums

Separation

Separation protects people (and property) from the effects of a fire in the store itself, and also protects the store from fires which may occur elsewhere on site. Separation distances depend on various factors, including the quantity of nitrocellulose, how it is stored and whether or not the location is considered to be high risk (i.e. vulnerable populations or large numbers of people at risk).

The separation distances in the table below are primarily for storage of nitrocellulose in the open air, without any additional fire protection measures. The distances may be reduced by the use of fire protection measures such as:

  • fire resistant storage building
  • fire walls
  • water spray protection.

Separation may also be used to prevent or delay the spread of fire to other storage or process areas where hazardous materials are present in quantity.

Nitrocellulose should be stored separately from:

  • incompatible materials such as strong acids and alkalis, oxidising agents and any other unstable or heat sensitive substance
  • compressed gases and liquids (such as liquefied petroleum gas and liquid oxygen) which may become over pressurised in a fire
  • flammable liquids and other combustible material.

Fire resistant storage buildings or storerooms

Detached storage buildings or dedicated storerooms in a building should incorporate the following features:

  • Sufficient fire resistance to allow adequate time, in the event of a fire in the store, for the alarm to be raised and for people to escape
  • Good means of escape from the store itself
  • Escape routes from any other part of the site should not be compromised
  • Lightweight roof or relief panel, positioned so that, in the event of a fire, escaping heat and fumes are directed to a safe place.

Control of ignition sources

The following measures should be in place:

  • Prohibiting smoking and other ignition sources such as matches or lighters in the storage area
  • Excluding all process activity from the store, including dispensing
  • Controlling construction or maintenance work that could affect the safety of the stored material, such as PTW for hot work
  • Controlling the use of electrical equipment, which is suitable for use in hazardous areas
  • Adequate separation from heat sources, such as steam pipes
  • Protection against prolonged exposure to strong sunshine, such as non-combustible canopy or shade
  • High standards of housekeeping to ensure that the store is kept free of combustible rubbish (including vegetation) which may contribute to the fire risk.

Restricted access

Access to the store should be by authorised people and vehicles only.

The standard of security at the store will depend upon the conditions in the area surrounding the store but also on the general level of security at the premises.

Safety signage

Warning notices should be posted at the entrances to stores to indicate:

  • that it is a nitrocellulose store
  • that the materials stored are flammable solids and, where appropriate, that they also contain highly flammable liquids
  • that smoking and other ignition sources are prohibited
  • the procedure to be followed in case of fire.
