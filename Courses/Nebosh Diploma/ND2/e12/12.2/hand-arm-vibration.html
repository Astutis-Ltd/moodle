Hand-Arm Vibration (HAV)

Hand-arm vibration is vibration transmitted from work processes into workersâ€™ hands and arms. It can be caused by operating handheld power tools, such as road breakers, and hand guided equipment, such as powered lawnmowers, or by holding work pieces being machined.

Damaged and very old models of equipment may be hazardous even when used for very short periods. Most types of hand-held, hand-guided or hand-fed powered equipment can cause ill health from vibration if used incorrectly.

For powered hand-tools, regular and frequent use of modern, well-designed, well-maintained tools is likely to result in exposure at or above the Exposure Action Value (EAV) after:

  • about 15 minutes use of a hammer action tool, or
  • about one hours use of non-hammer action tools.

The Exposure Limit Value (ELV) is likely to be reached after:

  • use of a hammer action tool for about one hour
  • use of non-hammer action tools for about four hours.

Health effects

Employees whose hands are regularly exposed to vibration may suffer from symptoms in the hands and arms. These symptoms occur in three categories:

Neurological Numbness and tingling in the fingers and a reduced sense of touch, temperature and pain perception which can make it difficult to feel, and to work with, small objects.
Vascular

Temporary reduction in blood circulation in the fingers with parts of the fingers becoming white / blanched (Vibration white finger - VWF).

The fingers feel numb when blanched, then as blood circulation returns to normal, either by itself or after warming they can be throbbing, red and painful.

Although vibration causes VWF, it usually does not trigger white finger attacks. The main trigger is exposure to the cold. Initially affects the tips of the fingers but symptoms usually get worse and spread along the finger towards the palm with continuing exposure.

The thumb may also be affected. Rarely, in extreme cases, blood circulation may be permanently reduced in the affected fingers.
Musculoskeletal Joint pain and stiffness in the hand and arm. Grip strength can be reduced.
