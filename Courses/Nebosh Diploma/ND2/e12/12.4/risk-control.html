Risk control

The hierarchy for control which minimises the risk from vibration exposure, in order of priority, is as follows:

  • Eliminate vibration exposure by changing the work processes
  • Reduce exposure by mechanisation
  • Reduce exposure by good process control
  • Avoid high-vibration tools, machines, vehicles and accessories
  • Maintain machines, vehicles and accessories
  • Reduce the transmission of vibration into the body
  • Reduce the durations of exposure (including job rotation)
  • Keep warm and dry (HAV).

Eliminate vibration exposure by changing the work processes

The most effective and reliable way of eliminating the risk from vibration is to design (or redesign) work processes so that employees are not exposed to vibration at all.

Reduce exposure by mechanisation

Mechanisation, the use of robotics, remote control or other forms of automation can eliminate or reduce exposure.

Reduce exposure by good process control

Good process control is important for maintaining product quality and production efficiency; and can also help reduce overall vibration exposure. Improving the quality control can minimise the need for remedial work using powered tools for chiselling, grinding, cutting, sanding etc.

Avoid high-vibration machines and accessories

After doing all that is reasonably practicable to replace or modify work processes, employees may still be exposed to vibration. If so, unnecessarily high vibration exposures should be avoided by careful selection of work equipment.

The available vibration emission information for a range machines of a similar type should be reviewed and a maximum level of vibration emission for the specific machine class should be determined and specified for future purchase or hire.

Reduce the transmission of vibration into the body

When the use of vibrating equipment and exposure to vibration is, it is often possible to control the extent to which damaging vibration is transmitted to the body. For HAV this involves minimising grip and push (or feed) forces. For WBV the use of properly adjusted suspension seats ids recommended.

Reduce the durations of exposure (including job rotation)

When the vibration levels have been reduced so far as is reasonably practicable, further reduction in exposure can only be achieved by limiting the time employees are exposed to vibration. This can be essential to keep exposures below the ELV.

Keeping warm and dry (for HAV)

Low hand or body temperature increases the risk of finger blanching because of reduced blood circulation. Gloves and other warm clothing can help vibration exposed workers maintain good circulation.
