Control of Vibration at Work Regulations 2005

Regulation 4 - Exposure limit values and action values

The Exposure Action Value (EAV) is the level of daily exposure which, if reached or exceeded, requires specified action to be taken to reduce risk.

The Exposure Limit Value (ELV) is the level of daily exposure which must not be exceeded, save as set out in Regulation 6(5).

For hand-arm vibration:

  • the daily exposure limit value is 5 m/s2A(8)
  • the daily exposure action value is 2.5 m/s2A(8).

For whole body vibration:

  • the daily exposure limit value is 1.15 m/s2A(8)
  • the daily exposure action value is 0.5 m/s2A(8).

Regulation 5 - Assessment of the risk to health created by vibration at the workplace

Where employees are likely to be exposed to risk from vibration the employer shall make a suitable and sufficient assessment of the risk, and identify the measures necessary to comply with the requirements of the Regulations.

The risk assessment should consider:

  • observation of specific working practices
  • reference to relevant information on the probable magnitude of the vibration, corresponding to the equipment used in the particular working conditions
  • measurement of the magnitude of vibration where necessary to assess if employees are likely to be exposed to vibration at or above an EAV, or above an ELV.

The risk assessment should consider:

  1. the magnitude, type and duration of exposure, including any exposure to intermittent vibration or repeated shocks
  2. the effects of exposure to vibration on employees whose health is at particular risk from such exposure
  3. any effects of vibration on the workplace and work equipment
  4. manufacturer’s information
  5. the availability of lower vibration replacement equipment
  6. any extension of exposure beyond normal working hours including at rest facilities
  7. specific working conditions such as low temperatures
  8. appropriate information obtained from health surveillance.

The significant findings of the assessment should be recorded. The assessment should be reviewed regularly, particularly if there are significant changes or grounds to believe it is no longer valid.

Regulation 6 - Elimination or control of exposure to vibration at the workplace

The risk from exposure to vibration should be either eliminated at source or, where this is not reasonably practicable, reduced to as low a level as is reasonably practicable.

Where it is not reasonably practicable to eliminate risk at source and an EAV is likely to be reached or exceeded, exposure should be reduced to as low a level as is reasonably practicable through a programme of organisational and technical measures which is appropriate to the activity.

The control measures should be based on the general principles of prevention set out in Schedule 1 to the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999, and should include consideration of:

  1. other working methods which eliminate or reduce exposure to vibration
  2. choice of work equipment of appropriate ergonomic design which, taking account of the work to be done, produces the least possible vibration
  3. the provision of auxiliary equipment which reduces the risk of injuries caused by vibration
  4. appropriate maintenance programmes for work equipment, the workplace and workplace systems
  5. the design and layout of workplaces, work stations and rest facilities
  6. suitable and sufficient information and training for employees, such that work equipment may be used correctly and safely, in order to minimise their exposure to vibration
  7. limitation of the duration and magnitude of exposure to vibration
  8. appropriate work schedules with adequate rest periods
  9. the provision of clothing to protect employees from cold and damp.

Employees must not be exposed to vibration above an ELV. If an ELV is exceeded the employer is required to ‘forthwith’:

  1. reduce exposure to vibration to below the limit value
  2. identify the reason for that limit being exceeded
  3. modify the control measures to prevent it being exceeded again.

Where the employee’s exposure is usually below the EAV, but varies markedly from time to time, the ELV may occasionally be exceeded provided that:

  1. any exposure to vibration averaged over one week is less than the ELV
  2. there is evidence to show that the risk from the actual pattern of exposure is less than the corresponding risk from constant exposure at the ELV
  3. risk is reduced to as low a level as is reasonably practicable, taking into account the special circumstances
  4. the employees concerned are subject to increased health surveillance.

Measures taken in compliance should be adapted as necessary to protect any employee, or group of employees, whose health is likely to be particularly at risk from vibration.

Regulation 7 - Health surveillance

If the risk assessment indicates that there is a risk to the health of employees who are, or are liable to be, exposed to vibration, or are likely to be exposed to vibration at or above an EAV, the employees should be placed under suitable health surveillance.

Health surveillance, is intended to prevent or diagnose any health effect linked with exposure to vibration and is appropriate where the exposure of the employee to vibration is such that:

  1. a link can be established between that exposure and an identifiable disease or adverse health effect
  2. it is probable that the disease or effect may occur under the particular conditions of work
  3. there are valid techniques for detecting the disease or effect.

A suitable health record should be kept for each employee who undergoes health surveillance. The record should be maintained and kept available in a suitable form.

Where, as a result of health surveillance, an employee is found to have an identifiable disease or adverse health effect, the employer shall ensure that the employee is examined by a doctor or specialist.

The risk assessments and risk control measures should be reviewed following the discovery of an identifiable disease or adverse health effect.

Regulation 8 - Information, instruction and training

Where the risk assessment indicates that there is a risk to the health of employees, or employees are likely to be exposed to vibration at or above an EAV, the employees and their representatives should be provided with suitable and sufficient information, instruction and training, including:

  1. organisational and technical control measures
  2. the ELVs and EAVs
  3. the significant findings of the risk assessment, including measurements and an explanation of the findings
  4. why and how to detect and report signs of injury
  5. entitlement to, and purpose of, health surveillance
  6. safe working practices to minimise exposure to vibration
  7. the collective results of any health surveillance undertaken.

The information, instruction and training should be updated to take account of significant changes in the type of work carried out, or the working methods used by the employer.
