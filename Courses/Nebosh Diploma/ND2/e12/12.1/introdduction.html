Introduction

Vibration refers to the mechanical movement of a body rhythmically back and forth through a fixed point. Under the Control of Vibration at Work Regulations 2005, vibration is a reference to the exposure of that employee to mechanical vibration arising out of, or in connection with, his work.

Vibration can occur in all three planes (or about three axes), which means:

  • up and down
  • left to right
  • forwards and backwards.

The movement may be in a straight line or circular, as in tidal movements - pitch, roll and yaw.

There are three parameters that could be measured:

  • the distance travelled (displacement)
  • the speed of travel
  • the acceleration.

The international standard for human vibration measurement specifies the measurement of the average acceleration across all three planes as the appropriate measurement of magnitude. Acceleration is measured in metres per second per second, or metres per second squared (m/s2).

As with noise, Root Mean Square (RMS) values are used rather than peak values.
