Introduction

This element looks at the roles and relationships between three pieces of legislation REACH, CLP and CoSHH. The relevant aspects are discussed in more detail later.

REACH

REACH is the European Union Regulation for the Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and Restriction of Chemicals. REACH was designed to fill the knowledge gaps around the safety of chemicals used every day in industry and in consumer products.

It takes a broad view of chemical use, and places new duties on businesses depending on where they sit in the supply chain. ‘Registration’ requires companies who manufacture chemical substances or import them into Europe (i.e. those at the top of the European supply chain) to ‘register’ a dossier of technical information about each substance they manufacture or import above a tonne a year with the ECHA in Helsinki.

Distributors and suppliers have specific duties to pass information (Safety Data Sheets) down to their customers, and also to pass information back to their own suppliers when customers ask them to do so, thus overlapping with CLP

Businesses that use chemicals in their work have a duty to use chemicals safely in accordance with the information on risk management measures that has been passed down the supply chain in labels and SDS, and in accordance with specific workplace legislation such as CoSHH.

CLP

The EU Classification, Labelling and Packaging (CLP) Regulation entered into force in January 2009, and introduced a method of classifying and labelling chemicals based on the United Nations’ Globally Harmonised System (GHS). Consistent classification and labelling helps to ensure that the hazards presented by chemicals are clearly communicated to workers and consumers.

CoSHH

The Control of Substances Hazardous to Health Regulations 1999 requires employers to control substances that are hazardous to health. A basic understanding of the Regulations is assumed knowledge. This element revisits the details of Regulations 6 and 7, the assessment of health risks created by work involving substances hazardous to health; and the prevention or control of exposure to substances hazardous to health.
