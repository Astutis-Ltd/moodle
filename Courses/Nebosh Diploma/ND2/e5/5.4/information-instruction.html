Information, instruction and training (Regulation 12)

The risk of cancer from exposure to a hazardous substance cannot in most cases be presumed to be reduced to zero except by eliminating exposure. Furthermore, the adverse effects may not show in the short term. It is, therefore, especially important that information, instruction and training provided is of an appropriately high standard. It is essential to ensure that employees are aware not only of the need to maintain control of exposure, but also the additional compounding risks due to smoking. Smokers appear to face an increased risk of occupational cancer, since they take a mixture of substances into their lungs with the smoke.

In addition to the information specified by CoSHH and the ACOP, employees and their safety representatives, and any other people likely to be exposed to carcinogenic or mutagenic substances, should be kept aware of:

  • the type or form of the risk
  • the special features of carcinogenic and mutagenic substances
  • the circumstances in which they may be exposed to carcinogenic or mutagenic substances.
