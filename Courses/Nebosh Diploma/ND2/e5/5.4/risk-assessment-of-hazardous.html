Risk assessment of hazardous substances (CoSHH Regulation 6)

The employer is required to make a suitable and sufficient assessment of the risks to his employees’ health arising from workplace exposure to substances hazardous to health.

For less significant risks the CoSHH assessment can be made as part of, or as an extension of, the more general risk assessment duties placed on employers by Regulation 3 of the Management Regulations.

If the hazardous substance is flammable, explosive or oxidising the assessment can also be used to fulfil the requirements of the Dangerous Substances and Explosive Atmosphere Regulations 2002 (DSEAR).

The purpose of the assessment is to enable the employer to make a valid decision about the measures necessary to prevent or adequately control the exposure of their employees to substances hazardous to health arising from the work.
