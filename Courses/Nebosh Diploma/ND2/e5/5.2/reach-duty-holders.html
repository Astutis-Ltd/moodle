REACH duty holders

There are three main types of REACH duty holder:

  • Manufacturers / importers
  • Distributors and suppliers
  • Downstream users.

Manufacturers / importers

Businesses that manufacture or import (from outside the EU) 1 tonne or more of any given substance each year are responsible for registering a dossier of information about that substance with the ECHA.

Note: Manufacturers / importers of articles may also be registrants if substances in those articles are intended to be released during use.

Distributors and suppliers

Businesses that sell chemicals have specific duties to pass information down to their customers, and also to pass information back to their own suppliers when customers ask them to do so.

Downstream users

Downstream users are businesses that use chemicals in their work. These businesses have a duty to use chemicals safely (i.e. in accordance with the requirements of CoSHH, CLAW, etc.), and in accordance with the information on risk management measures that has been passed down the supply chain in labels and SDS.

Downstream users can also pass information about use back to registrants so that this can be taken account of when assessing the risks of chemical used.
