The Control of Electromagnetic Field at Work Regulations 2016 part 2

‘Electromagnetic field’ means a static electric, static magnetic and time-varying electric, magnetic and electromagnetic field with a frequency of up to 300GHz.

Regulation 3: Application

The Regulations do not apply to the master or crew of a ship or to the employer of such persons in respect of the normal shipboard activities of a ship’s crew which are carried out solely by the crew under the direction of the master.

‘Ship‘ includes every description of vessel used in navigation, other than a ship forming part of Her Majesty’s Navy.

Regulation 4: Limitation on exposure to electromagnetic fields

The employer must ensure that employees are not exposed to electromagnetic field levels in excess of the Exposure Limit Values (ELVs), with the following exceptions:

  • any activity in respect of which a suitable and sufficient exposure limitation system is in place, where that activity is carried out:
    • by a person acting in the capacity of a member of either Her Majesty’s armed forces or a visiting force
    • by any civilian working with such a person
    • on any premises or part of premises under the control of the Secretary of State for the purposes of the Ministry of Defence or the service authorities of a visiting force.
  • the development, testing, installation, use and maintenance of, or research related to, magnetic resonance imaging equipment for patients in the health sector, where:
    • the exposure of employees to electromagnetic fields is as low as is reasonably practicable
    • employees are protected against any health effects and safety risks related to that exposure.

Exposure may also exceed the sensory effect ELVs during work activities in respect of which the employer has taken the applicable safety measures set out in the Schedule (a).

Regulation 5: Exposure assessment

The employer must make a suitable and sufficient assessment of the levels of EMF to which employees may be exposed.

Where regulation 4(1) applies the assessment must demonstrate whether that regulation is complied with, if necessary through the use of calculations and measurements and the employer may, in accordance with the Schedule(c), assess exposure against the action limits (ALs) in order to determine that specific ELVs are not exceeded.

The assessment may take into account:

  • emission information and other safety related data provided by the manufacturer or distributor of equipment
  • industry standards and guidelines
  • guidance produced by the European Commission
  • guidance produced by the HSE.

The employer must review the assessment when there is reason to suspect it is no longer valid, or there has been a significant change in the matters to which it relates, and make such changes to it as are necessary to ensure it remains suitable and sufficient.

Regulation 6: Application of regulations 7 to 9

Regulation 7 does not apply:

  • where the most recent exposure assessment under regulation 5 demonstrates compliance with regulation 4(1)(i.e. that employees are not exposed to EMF levels above the ELVs)
  • in relation to armed forces or health sector activities set out in regulation 4(3).

Regulations 8 and 9 do not apply where:

  • the most recent exposure assessment under regulation 5 demonstrates that employees are not exposed to EMF levels in excess of the ELVs
  • the indirect effect ALs are not exceeded
  • there are no employees at particular risk.

Regulation 7: Action plan

The employer must make and implement a suitable and sufficient action plan to ensure compliance with regulation 4(1).

The action plan must include consideration of, where relevant:

  1. other working methods that entail lower exposure to electromagnetic fields
  2. replacement equipment designed to reduce the level of exposure
  3. technical measures to reduce the emission of electromagnetic fields, including, where necessary, the use of interlocks, screening or similar health protection mechanisms
  4. demarcation and access control measures
  5. maintenance programmes for work equipment, workplaces and workstation systems
  6. the design and layout of workplaces and workstations
  7. limitations on the duration and intensity of exposure
  8. the availability of suitable personal protective equipment.

Where, despite the measures taken under paragraph (1), the exposure of employees exceeds any ELV the employer must, as soon as is reasonably practicable, identify and implement any changes to the action plan which are necessary to ensure compliance with regulation 4(1).

Regulation 8: Risk assessment

The employer must make a suitable and sufficient assessment of the risks to employees arising from their exposure to EMF. The risk assessment must include consideration of, where relevant:

  1. the ALs and ELVs
  2. the frequency range, level, duration and type of exposure, including its distribution over the employee’s body and the workplace
  3. direct biophysical effects
  4. replacement equipment designed to reduce the level of exposure
  5. information obtained from any health surveillance or medical examinations provided under regulation 11
  6. information provided by the manufacturer or distributor of equipment
  7. multiple sources of exposure
  8. simultaneous exposure to multiple frequency fields
  9. indirect effects
  10. any effects on employees at particular risk
  11. other health and safety related information.

Employees at particular risk include:

  • Pregnant workers.
  • Workers with Active or Passive Implanted Medical Devices (AIMDs or PIMDs) or Body Worn Medical Devices (BWMDs)
  • Those who work in close proximity to electro-explosive devices, explosive materials or flammable atmospheres.

This regulation does not include the risk of effects:

  • caused by contact with live conductors
  • caused by multiple and separate instances of exposure
  • which continue to develop when exposure has ceased.

The employer must review the assessment when there is reason to suspect it is no longer valid, or there has been a significant change in the matters to which it relates, and make such changes to it as are necessary to ensure it remains suitable and sufficient.

Regulation 9: Obligation to eliminate or reduce risks

The employer must ensure sfairp that the risks identified in the most recent risk assessment under regulation 8 are eliminated or reduced to a minimum by:

  • Application of the general principles of prevention as set out in Schedule 1 to the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999
  • Consideration of technical progress, the potential to restrict access to parts of the workplace, and the availability of measures to control the production of EMF at source.

Regulation 10: Information and training

The employer must provide the following information and training (if relevant) to any employees who are likely to be subjected to the risks identified in the most recent risk assessment under regulation 8:

  1. the measures taken under regulation 9
  2. the concepts and values of the ALs and ELVs and the possible risks associated with them
  3. the possible indirect effects of exposure
  4. the results of the most recent exposure assessment under regulation 5
  5. how to detect and report sensory and health effects
  6. the circumstances in which employees are entitled to health surveillance and medical examinations under regulation 11
  7. safe working practices
  8. any additional measures taken in respect of employees at particular risk.

Regulation 11: Health surveillance and medical examinations

The employer must ensure that health surveillance and medical examinations are provided as appropriate to any employee who is exposed to EMF levels in excess of the health effect ELVsand reports experiencing a health effect to the employer.

Any health surveillance or medical examinations must be provided during any reasonable hours chosen by the employee.

The employer must keep a suitable record of any health surveillance and medical examinations provided.

Regulation 12: Records

An employer who employs five or more employees must:

  • record the significant findings of the most recent exposure assessment under regulation 5
  • and where required to make them, record:
    • the most recent action plan under regulation 7
    • the significant findings of the most recent risk assessment under regulation 8.

Regulation 13: Exemptions

The HSE may by a certificate in writing exempt employers from the requirements of regulations 4(1) and 7 in relation to one or more work activities.

Any exemption must be limited in time and subject to the following conditions:

  • the exposure of employees to EMF is as low as is reasonably practicable
  • employees are protected against any health effects and safety risks related to that exposure.

The HSE may amend or revoke an exemption at any time by a further certificate in writing.

Schedules

The ALs and ELVs are set out in tables and grouped according to their potential effects, being:

  • thermal effects, related to the heating of tissue due to its absorption of EMF
  • non-thermal effects, related to the stimulation of nerves or sensory organs due to the presence of EMF.
