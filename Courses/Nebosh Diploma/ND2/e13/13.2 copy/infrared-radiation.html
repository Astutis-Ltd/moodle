InfraRed Radiation (IRR)

Infrared radiation is located between microwaves and visible light. It is subdivided into three bands, based on wavelength and the absorption characteristics of IR in tissue and the resulting different biological effects.

  • IRA is the shortest wavelength, from 780nm to 1.4μm
  • IRB from 1.4μm to 3μm
  • IRC is the longest wavelength, from 3μm to 1mm.

Infrared radiation is a natural part of the human environment and thus people are exposed to it, in small amounts, in all areas of daily life. Intense exposures can result from technical processes in the workplace.

Many industrial processes use infrared radiation lamps for thermal curing of various kinds of materials. The heat sources used for thermal curing and the heated material itself will usually emit high levels of IRR. Other sources of IRR include: flames, gas torches, acetylene torches, pools of molten metal and incandescent metal.

Workers such as bakers, glass blowers, kiln workers, foundry workers, blacksmiths, smelters and fire-fighters are potentially at risk of exposure.
