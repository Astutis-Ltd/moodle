Laser Protection Advisor (LPA)

The British Standard user’s guide for laser safety, PD IEC TR 60825-14:2004 recommends that a Laser Safety Officer (LSO) is appointed where Class 3B and Class 4 lasers are used.

The required level of competence of a Laser Safety Officer should be balanced against the risks from the work with the laser, including any maintenance or servicing operations. Laser safety expertise may only be required at the start of the work with the laser product and when changes are made. In such circumstances expertise in all aspects of laser safety would be unnecessary.

The term Laser Protection Adviser (LPA) is used to describe a person (or organisation) who can be consulted on complex laser safety management issues.

The use of Class 3B and Class 4 lasers and Intense Pulse Light (IPL) systems in the independent healthcare sector is currently regulated across the UK by:

  • The Care Quality Commission (England)
  • The Healthcare Inspectorate Wales
  • The Care Inspectorate (Scotland)
  • The Regulation and Quality Improvement Authority (Northern Ireland).

The National Minimum Standards used in England and Wales specifically require a Laser Protection Adviser (LPA) to be consulted, and in Scotland and Northern Ireland the regulators will refer to the guidance from the Medicines and Healthcare Products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) which also details the need for a LPA.

The functions of a Laser Protection Advisor are to:

  • Act as an external source of expert advice on laser safety matters
  • Ensure that the client’s lasers are used, and continue to be used, in a safe manner
  • Help the client achieve compliance with applicable laser safety regulation
  • Help the client satisfy the applicable regulatory authorities that lasers are being operated safely.

The general duties involve:

  • Carrying out risk assessments and recommending appropriate control measures
  • Checking all laser equipment and facilities before use
  • Producing a set of local rules and written operating procedures
  • Checking safety eyewear, and other safety equipment, for compliance with standards
  • Recommending and checking area signs and correct labelling of equipment
  • Checking that all laser operators have received adequate safety training.
