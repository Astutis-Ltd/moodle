Making the management plan

A written (electronic or paper) management plan should be prepared to set out how the risks identified from asbestos will be managed. Details should include:

  • identifying the person(s) responsible for managing the asbestos risk
  • a copy of the asbestos record or register and how to access it if it is kept electronically
  • instructions that any work on the fabric of the building cannot start without the relevant parts of the record / register being checked.

The plan should include details for how this will be achieved through procedures and arrangements to ensure that:

  • the record / register is checked in good time before the work starts
  • checks are made to ensure that the information on the presence of asbestos has been understood and will be taken into account
  • checks are made that competent asbestos-trained contractors will carry out the work and that the correct controls will be used
  • plans are in place for any necessary work identified from the risk assessment requiring repair, protection or removal of ACMs
  • monitoring the condition of ACMs is properly scheduled
  • the content of the management plan is effectively communicated
  • contingency arrangements are in place if the main contact person for asbestos risk management is not available.

The action plan should:

  • prioritise the actions identified
  • give high priority to damaged material and materials likely to be disturbed; these will need to be repaired, sealed, enclosed protectively or removed using trained and competent personnel.

If unsure how to implement the management plan themselves, the dutyholder should seek competent specialist advice from an asbestos surveyor, a laboratory or a licensed contractor, or other competent person as appropriate.

The plan should include procedures and responsibilities to ensure that the asbestos register is shared with any worker / contractor carrying out maintenance or other work.

The asbestos register, including drawings, should be available on site for the entire life of the premises and should be kept up to date.

Work should only start once the dutyholder is satisfied that the information in the asbestos record / register is known and understood by the workers who are doing the work on site and easily accessible for anyone who needs to inspect it.

A simple asbestos management plan is generally presented in four sections:

  1. A site plan – highlighting the areas which contain, or may contain asbestos
  2. The asbestos register – detailing the location of asbestos and ACM’s, the type of asbestos, its condition and accessibility, and material and priority scores
  3. The action plan – detailing required works, deadlines and responsible persons
  4. The communication plan – detailing arrangements for marking (applying stickers to) asbestos items in doors, and for providing information to operational staff, maintenance staff, contractors and other interested / affected parties.
