Duty to manage asbestos in non-domestic premises (Regulation 4)

The dutyholder is any person who by virtue of a contract or tenancy has an obligation (of any extent) regarding the maintenance or repair of non-domestic premises or the means of access to or egress from those premises. This is typically the occupier, landlord or managing agent.

Where there are no contracts or tenancy agreements for any parts of the premises, every person who has, to any extent, control of that part of the premises or its means of access or egress is a duty-holder.

Where there is more than one duty-holder, the relative contribution of each towards complying with the requirements of this regulation is determined by the nature and extent of the maintenance and repair obligation owed by that person.

Every person must cooperate with the dutyholder so far as is necessary to enable the duty-holder to comply with their duties.

In order to manage the risk from asbestos the duty-holder must take all reasonable steps to ensure that a suitable and sufficient assessment is carried out as to whether asbestos is, or is liable to be, present in the premises.

The assessment must consider:

  • the condition of any asbestos which is, or has been assumed to be, present in the premises
  • building plans or other relevant information
  • the age of the premises and
  • include an inspection (asbestos survey) of all parts of the premises which are reasonably accessible.

The dutyholder must ensure that the assessment is reviewed without delay if there is reason to suspect that the assessment is no longer valid or there has been a significant change to the premises.

The conclusions of the assessment and every review must be recorded.

Where the assessment shows that asbestos is or is liable to be present in any part of the premises, the dutyholder must ensure that:

  • the risk from the asbestos is determined
  • the measures which are to be taken for managing the risk are specified in a written plan identifying those parts of the premises concerned.

The measures to be specified in the plan for managing the risk must include adequate measures for monitoring the condition of any asbestos or any substance containing or suspected of containing asbestos. The asbestos / asbestos containing materials must be properly maintained, or where necessary, safely removed.

Information about the location and condition of any asbestos or asbestos containing materials (the asbestos register) is provided to every person liable to disturb it, and the emergency services, as necessary.

The dutyholder must ensure that the asbestos management plan is reviewed and revised at regular intervals, and without delay if:

  • there is reason to suspect that the plan is no longer valid, or
  • there has been a significant change in the premises to which the plan relates;
  • the measures specified in the plan are implemented.

The measures taken to implement the plan must be recorded.
