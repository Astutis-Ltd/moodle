Asbestos record and management plan

A ‘management survey’ (workplace inspection is conducted to identify where asbestos is located or where there might be asbestos in the workplace. This is recorded in the asbestos record (asbestos register).

The HSE recommends that each entry in the asbestos register should be given a material score and a priority score. ACM’s in good condition score ‘1’ for both material and priority.

The HSE’s ‘Material and priority scoring tool’ is used to get material and priority scores for materials in poor condition. The highest risk materials (blue asbestos) and highest risk conditions (unsealed / not encapsulated / significant damage etc) score 3, with an intermediate score of 2.

The scores for each asbestos material should be recorded in the asbestos register.

The asbestos register must be kept current and should be updated following any work done on asbestos materials.

All materials in the register should be checked at least once a year to make sure they haven’t deteriorated.

Employees and contractors should be informed and regularly reminded of the need to check the asbestos register before undertaking any works which may disturb asbestos materials. Systems should be in place to ensure that the work is authorised by a manager or supervisor and that the necessary checks are made.
