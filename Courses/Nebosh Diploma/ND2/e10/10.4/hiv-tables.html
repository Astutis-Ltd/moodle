Disease HIV
Occupational context Blood-to-blood infection through needle stick injury in a healthcare setting (although in 2010 there had not been a case in the UK for more than five years).
Occurrence Infected blood, semen, vaginal fluids or breast milk.
Symptoms

Acute: some people experience acute retroviral syndrome (ARS) within 2 to 4 weeks of infection. ARS is often described as ‘the worst flu ever’. Not everyone develops ARS, and it can take up to 3 months to appear in those who do.

Chronic: severe illness, with an extremely damaged immune system several years later.

Treatment HIV is treated with antiretrovirals (ARVs), which work against the HIV infection by slowing down the spread of the virus in the body.
