Microbiological safety cabinet class Biological organism hazard group Details
Class lll 4 and 3 (where the agent is highly infective, for example: Brucellosis)
  • Highest risk
  • Maximum protection for the operator, work and environment, but because of the pattern of airflow there is a greater likelihood of contamination of the work after a spill than in a Class I cabinet
  • Working area is totally enclosed
  • Incoming and outgoing air is HEPA* filtered
  • Access is provided by use of arm-length gloves attached to ports in the front panel of the unit
  • Requires high standards of maintenance and operator training.
Class ll 1 - 3
  • Worker and product protection:
    • escape of airborne particles controlled by an inward airflow at the front of the cabinet, filtered before circulation within it
    • down flow of HEPA* filtered air over the working surface protects the work.
  • Front opening to carry out task
  • Modern Class II cabinets can provide protection of the same order as Class I cabinets, but are more susceptible to disruption of airflows. They should be used only where protection of the work is essential.
Class l 1 - 3
  • Lower risk
  • Worker protection, no product protection
  • Front opening to carry out task
  • Inward airflow through the working front opening
  • HEPA filtration of the exhaust air.
*HEPA filter = High Efficiency Particle Arrestor
