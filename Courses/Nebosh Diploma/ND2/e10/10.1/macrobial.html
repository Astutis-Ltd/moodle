Macrobial parasites

Macrobial parasites are large enough to be seen without a microscope. Two categories are important for human health:

  • Helminthes, including flatworms (platyhelminths) and roundworms (nematodes)
  • Arthropods, such as lice, ticks and fleas.

Note: No macrobial parasites are specified on the NEBOSH National Diploma syllabus.
