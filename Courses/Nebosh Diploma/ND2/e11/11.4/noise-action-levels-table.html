Noise action levels Daily / weekly average Peak sound pressure Employers’ duties
Lower exposure action value 80dB(A) 135dB(C)
  • General duty to reduce risk to the lowest level that is reasonably practicable
  • Assessment of noise exposure by a competent person
  • Consult with exposed workers or their representatives
  • Provide information, instruction and training
  • Make hearing protection available.
Upper exposure action value 85dB(A) 137dB(C)
  • Take reasonably practicable measures to reduce noise exposure (engineering controls / technical measures)
  • Provide mandatory hearing protection pending engineering controls and, where necessary, after engineering controls
  • Mark ear protection zones
  • Ensure ear protection is used.
Exposure limit value 87dB(A) 140dB(C)
  • Ensure level is not exceeded, taking hearing protection into account.

Noise action levels: Upper exposure action value

  • Daily / weekly average: 85dB(A)

    • Provide mandatory hearing protection pending engineering controls and, where necessary, after engineering controls

    • Ensure ear protection is used.
