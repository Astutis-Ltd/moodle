Type / description Advantages Disadvantages

Ear muffs:

  • Hard plastic cups which fit over and surround the ears
  • Sealed to the head by cushion seals under tension from a headband
  • Inner surfaces of the cups are covered with a sound-absorbing material
  • Available in a range of sizes.
  • Easy to fit and use
  • Clearly visible so easy to monitor use.
  • May not be compatible with other PPE
  • May be uncomfortable in warm conditions
  • Long hair, beards and jewellery may interfere with seals.

Helmet mounted earmuffs:

Individual cups attached to safety head-gear such as a visor or a hard hat, usually by adjustable arms.

Avoids issue of compatibility with safety helmets.
  • May not be compatible with other PPE
  • May be uncomfortable in warm conditions
  • Long hair, beards and jewellery may interfere with seals.

Earplugs:

  • Fit into the ear or cover the ear canal to form a seal
  • Available in different forms (pre-shaped, user formable or semi-insert)
  • Reusable or disposable
  • May be attached to a cord or a neckband.

Suited for use with safety glasses and other forms of PPE.
  • Can be hard to fit and only effective when fitted properly
  • Difficult to monitor by observation
  • Work loose over time reducing efficiency
  • Unsuitable for dirty dusty environments or work requiring regular removal.

Custom moulded earplugs

Made from a material such as silicone rubber, individually moulded to fit a person's ears.

  • Easier to fit than other types of earplug
  • Comfortable.

Poor performance if manufacturing and initial fitting are not done properly.
