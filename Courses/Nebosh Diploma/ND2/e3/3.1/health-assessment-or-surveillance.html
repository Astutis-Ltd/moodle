Health assessment or health surveillance?

Generally the distinction between assessment and surveillance is the same as the distinction between measuring and monitoring. An assessment is a one off measure, whereas surveillance involves a series of regular measures or checks to detect the early signs of occupational ill-health.

There are many activities that can be done to measure or monitor the health of employees that should not be confused with health surveillance. Examples include measures necessary to comply with legal duties such as:

  • Pre-placement and annual medical examinations to assess an individual’s fitness for work, under the Ionising Radiations Regulations 2017
  • Health screening such as eye and eyesight testing under the Health and Safety (Display Screen Equipment) Regulations 1992
  • Assessments of fitness to drive or operate cranes within dock premises under the Docks Regulations 1988
  • Fitness for work health assessments offered to night workers under the Working Time Regulations 1998.

Other related activities, with no legal duty, include:

  • Post recruitment health enquiries to check an individual’s health status and medical history
  • Monitoring sickness absence records as a means of assessing risk where more formal health surveillance procedures are not appropriate
  • Lifestyle health promotion and education initiatives might be extended to include screening clinics to test cholesterol and blood pressure levels etc.
  • Annual health checks of key personnel
  • General testing of workers for evidence of drug or alcohol misuse, unless there are specific safety-critical implications (discussed later).
