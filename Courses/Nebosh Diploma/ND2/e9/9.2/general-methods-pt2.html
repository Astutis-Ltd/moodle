General methods for sampling (part 2)

Quantitative sampling methods

Quantitative or long-term sampling techniques sample air over a reference period of time (it does not necessarily have to be a long time), using a calibrated air pump to deliver a known volume of air to the sampling device. This allows calculations to be made of Time Weighted Average (TWA) exposures, which can then be compared to Workplace Exposure Limits (WELs).

Personal monitoring sampling apparatus consists of: a sampling head, positioned in the wearers breathing zone (clipped to collar, for example), connected with tubing to a calibrated portable medium flow pump (powered by rechargeable battery) and clipped to a belt or carried in a holster (See the illustration below).

Particulate sampling

Particulate sampling uses a personal sampler, takes a long term sample, provides an indirect reading and actively samples the air.

There are four types of sampling head recommended for particulate sampling: IOM sampling head, multi-orifice total inhalable sampler, conical inhalable sampler and respirable dust cyclone sampler. The table below gives more details.
