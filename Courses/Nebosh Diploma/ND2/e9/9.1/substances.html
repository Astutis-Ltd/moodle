Substances with no WEL

Workplace Exposure Limits (WELs) do not exist for a great many substances. The absence of an airborne substance from the EH40 list of WELs does not indicate that it is safe. WELs only apply to the airborne substances. Exposure to substances without a WEL should be controlled to a level to which nearly all the workers can be exposed daily, without any adverse effects on health.

For all substances (with or without a WEL) more emphasis is now placed on the following:

  • Hazard control banding (defining substances in hazard bands or ranges of target exposure rather than single values) as in CoSHH essentials, outlined below. (NB CoSHH essentials is not specifically mentioned in the Diploma syllabus but the approach is useful in practise)
  • Focusing more on prescriptive risk control measures, as well as exposure monitoring and comparison with an existing WEL.

CoSHH Essentials

The HSE’s CoSHH Essentials provide a generic hazardous substance risk assessment process with the aim of providing small and medium sized enterprises readily available advice on proportionate risk control measures.

CoSHH Essentials advice is provided via two types of ‘control guidance sheets’:

  • Industry-specific ‘direct advice sheets’
  • Generic ‘control guidance sheets’.

If there is a ‘direct advice sheet’ for a particular task or process in a given industry it should be followed. Otherwise the CoSHH e-tool can be used to identify which generic control guidance sheets are appropriate.

The system is based upon a generic risk assessment that considers a substances hazard band (based on H statements) and exposure potential based on the form (dust or vapour) and concentration of the substance (the amount suspended in air) to recommend 1 of four basic control approaches.
