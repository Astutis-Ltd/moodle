Note: from December 2011 extensive Carc, Sen and Sk notations are no longer provided. Notations are now only applied to substances identified in the Indicative Occupational Exposure Limit Values (IOELV) directives.

Notation Explanation
CAS number A unique numerical identifier assigned by the Chemical Abstracts Service to every chemical described in the open scientific literature.
ppm

Parts per million, by volume.

Used to express the limits for volatile substances.
mg / m3

Milligrams per meter cubed.

Used to express concentrations of airborne particles, such as fume, dust etc.
fibres / ml

Fibres per millilitre of air.

Used to express concentrations of fibres, for example: man-made mineral fibres (MMMF).
Carc Capable of causing cancer and / or heritable genetic damage. Includes substances which are assigned the risk phrases.
Sk

Can be absorbed through the skin.

The assigned substances are those for which there are concerns that dermal absorption will lead to systemic toxicity.
Sen Capable of causing occupational asthma.
BMGV

Biological monitoring guidance value.

Non-statutory standards based on a relationship between biological concentrations and health effects. Listed in table 2 of EH40.
