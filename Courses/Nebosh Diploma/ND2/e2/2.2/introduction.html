Introduction

The majority of people with mental health problems are capable of continuing to work productively. Evidence shows that employment can be of great benefit, both to the employer and to the employee.

Mental health problems may meet the definition of ‘disability’ in which case the employer would be required to make reasonable adjustments to prevent disabled employees from being disadvantaged at work. Examples of reasonable adjustments or more general accommodations might include:

  • flexible working arrangements,
  • managed return to work after time off
  • provision of counselling or peer support services.

Depending on the scale and maturity of an organisation there are a number of departments and professional groups that could have responsibility, for, or an interest in mental-health in the workplace, notably occupational health and safety, occupational health, human resources or employee assistance programmes.

The involvements, responsibilities and inter-relationships of the various parties need to be understood and coordinated to ensure it is straightforward for a concerned colleague to get the necessary advice and support.
