Benefits to the organisation of wellbeing strategies

Dissatisfaction with work and job-related stress are two of the main causes of mental and physical ill-health in the UK. Happy workers are 20% more productive, 34% less likely to leave their jobs and 37% less likely to take time off sick. (Mind, 2020)

In 2013 BITC / Workwell research showed that FTSE 100 companies that prioritised employee engagement and wellbeing outperformed the rest of the FTSE 100 by 10%. The benefits were shown to include enhanced morale, loyalty, commitment, innovation, productivity and profitability.

It is worth noting that for many years the HSE has been looking to business to move beyond compliance with health and safety regulations to continuously improve all aspects of the working environment that result in a workforce that is “happy, healthy and here”. The HSE also recognised that the major challenge for improving occupational health and safety the UK lies in improving occupational health which is less amenable to regulation than occupational safety.
