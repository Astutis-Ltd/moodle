Body protection

There are several different types of body protection, for example:

  • overalls
  • aprons
  • appropriate footwear.

Different materials will protect against different chemical and physical hazards. It is important to check with the clothing manufacturer to make sure that the material selected will provide protection from the specific chemical exposure.

Materials for chemical protective clothing include:

  • Paper-like fiber

    • Disposable suits made of this material provide protection against dust and splashes.

  • Treated wool and cotton

    • Protective clothing made from treated wool and cotton adapts well to changing workplace temperatures and is comfortable as well as fire resistant. Treated cotton and wool clothing protects against dust.

  • Rubber, neoprene, and plastics

    • Protective clothing made from these materials protects against certain acids and other chemicals.
