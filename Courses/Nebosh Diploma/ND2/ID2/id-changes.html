A workstation is an area where work of a particular nature is carried out. This could include a supermarket checkout; a point of work on an assembly line; a car park attendants kiosk; or a desk in an office.

There is additional, specific advice for Display Screen equipment (DSE) workstations.

Every workstation should be arranged to be suitable for any person who may work there and for any work which is likely to be done there.

Outdoor workstations shall, so far as is reasonably practicable:

  • provide protection from adverse weather
  • enable any person to leave it swiftly, or be assisted in the event of an emergency
  • ensure persons at the workstation are not likely to slip or fall.

Provision of a suitable seat for each person at work, whose work includes operations of a kind that the work (or a substantial part of it) can be done sitting, including a suitable footrest where necessary. Note: the seat must be suitable for both the person and the operations.
