V-Mac should be used where - V-Mac should not be used for / where
  • The job involves repetitive manual lifting and / or carrying
  • The job is carried out regularly - at least once a week
  • The handling lasts for more than four hours
  • The weights of the loads vary significantly (at least a 2kg difference between the lightest and the heaviest loads)
  • The ART tool cannot be used.
  • Seated handling, such as at a supermarket checkout
  • Handling objects by sliding them
  • Handling people or animals
  • Team handling activities
  • All the weights handled are less than 2.5kg. The job isn’t done regularly
  • The shift lasts longer than 12 hours
  • Note: The V-MAC also does not take into account the demands of related activities such as pushing, pulling and walking etc.

Colour band What it means Action to take (sfairp)
P

Very high level of risk

Such operations may represent a serious risk of injury and should come under close scrutiny. 		Eliminate these lifting operations as the highest priority.
R

High level of risk - Prompt action needed

This may expose a significant proportion of the working population to risk of injury.

Consider:

  • Eliminating manual handling of these items
  • Mechanising the handling
  • Reducing the item weights
  • Reducing the number of lifts per day of these items?
If any of the summary value bars fall into this zone, take prompt action to reduce the overall load handled.
A

Medium level of risk - Examine tasks closely

Consider:

  • Reducing the item weights
  • Reducing the number of lifts per day of these items.
G

Low level of risk

 Consider the vulnerability of special risk groups (such as pregnant women, or young workers) where appropriate.
