Class Body part No. Description
Cervical Neck 7

These are the smallest vertebrae, which support the smallest amount of weight (your head). The atlas and axes provide a pivot point on which the skull rotates, and allow free movement of the head.

The neck area is quite vulnerable to whiplash injuries.
Thoracic Chest / Upper back 12

These support the 12 pairs of ribs that form a partial cage for the protection of the heart, lungs and liver.

As the ribs form strong links with the vertebrae, there is little scope for movement in this area, other than slight twisting. Injury in this area is therefore less common.
Lumbar Lower back 5

This group of vertebrae have dual functions:

  • supports all the weight of the upper body
  • offers a large range of movement for bending and stretching.
Because of these functions, this area of the spine is under most strain. It is often called the ‘weak link’ as it is probably the commonest area for back injuries.
Sacrum and Coccyx Tailbone 5 + 4

The sacrum is made up of 5 bones fused into a triangular shape that fits into the pelvis and forms a protective plate over the bowel and other organs.

It is also fused to the coccyx, a collection of 4 tiny bones that have no known use. The coccyx may be the remnant of a tail.
